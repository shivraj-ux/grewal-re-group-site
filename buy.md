## The Austin Buying Process

Buying a home takes planning, local knowledge, and strategic execution. We walk you through each phase from the first conversation to closing day.

### 1. Discovery Call and Financing Coordination

Before you start looking, we align on your timeline, budget, and priorities. You will work with a lender to get pre-approved, which shows sellers your offer is backed by financing. Pre-approval also gives you confidence in your price range. Use our affordability calculator to explore how income, down payment size, and mortgage rates affect what you can buy.

### 2. Neighborhood Comparison and Search Strategy

Austin has 25 plus distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character, schools, commute, and value. From Westlake Hills and The Domain to East Austin's emerging areas to family-focused communities like Davenport Ranch, we match your lifestyle and budget to the right area. We research amenities, property trends, and long-term appreciation potential so you choose with confidence.

### 3. Tailored Property Search

We combine MLS access, market monitoring, and off-market relationships to find homes that fit your criteria. Austin's market moves fast. We alert you to new listings, track price changes, and uncover opportunities before they hit major portals. If new construction interests you, we guide you through builder negotiations and contract terms.

### 4. Offer Strategy with Market Data

A successful offer requires both the right price and the right terms. We analyze comparable homes, current market conditions, and the seller's motivation to position your offer competitively. We negotiate contingencies, earnest money amounts, closing timelines, and repair allowances to protect you and stay attractive to the seller. Every offer is custom, never one-size-fits-all.

### 5. Inspection Guidance and Repair Negotiation

You have the right to a professional inspection. We recommend trusted inspectors, walk through the inspection with you to explain what we find, and guide your decisions on repair requests or renegotiation. If the appraisal comes in lower than your offer, we work with your lender and the seller on next steps.

### 6. Closing Management and Final Walkthrough

In the final week, we coordinate with your lender, title company, and the seller's agent. We review your Closing Disclosure (outlining all costs), explain what to expect at signing, and schedule a final walkthrough 24 hours before closing to confirm all agreed repairs are complete and nothing has changed. Then you sign and get the keys.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the Austin home buying process?

Home buying in Austin follows these steps: pre-approval and budget setting, neighborhood search and property viewing, making an offer, inspection and appraisal, final walkthrough, and closing. The full timeline is typically 30 to 45 days from accepted offer to getting your keys.

### What programs help first-time Austin home buyers?

Austin-area first-time buyers may qualify for down payment assistance programs, Texas-specific first-time buyer loans with lower rates or reduced fees, FHA loans (3.5 percent down), VA loans (if military), and conventional loans with 3 percent down. We review your financial situation and help you find the best fit.

### How much house can I afford in Austin?

Your buying power depends on your income, down payment, debt, and current mortgage rates. Use our affordability calculator to find your price range. A common rule of thumb is aiming for a home price between 2.5 and 3 times your gross household income, but every situation is different.

### What are closing costs when buying?

Closing costs typically range from 2 percent to 5 percent of the purchase price and include loan origination fees, appraisal, title insurance, inspection, survey, property taxes, and homeowners insurance. Your lender provides a Closing Disclosure three days before closing that itemizes every cost. Buyers and sellers can negotiate who pays specific costs as part of the offer.

### How long does it take to buy a home in Austin?

Most Austin home purchases take 30 to 45 days from when your offer is accepted to closing day. This includes loan processing, appraisal, inspection (usually 10 days), title review, and final walkthrough. Cash offers or streamlined financing can close in 15 to 20 days.

### Should I buy new construction or a resale home?

New construction offers modern systems, builder warranties, and no surprises on the inspection. Resale homes offer established neighborhoods, larger lots often at lower prices, and negotiating power. Austin has strong options in both categories. Consider your lifestyle priorities, renovation tolerance, timeline, and budget when deciding.

### What is earnest money?

Earnest money is a deposit showing the seller you are serious about your offer. In Austin, earnest money is typically 1 percent to 2 percent of your offer price. It is held in escrow and applied to your down payment at closing. If you back out for a reason not covered by your contingencies, you may forfeit this amount.

### What is the current Austin real estate market like?

Austin remains a competitive market with strong demand from relocating professionals, tech workers, and investors. Inventory and pricing vary by neighborhood and price range. Homes in desirable areas can sell quickly, requiring competitive offers, strong pre-approval, and strategic positioning. We provide current data, comp analysis, and market strategy tailored to your search.
