Northwest Austin, Fastest-Growing Market

Liberty Hill TX Real Estate Guide 2026

By Shivraj Grewal, CLHMS Guild May 9, 2026 16 min read Williamson County
Bottom line for buyers: Liberty Hill, TX is the Austin area's fastest-growing small city in 2026, offering remarkable new construction value, the nationally award-winning Santa Rita Ranch master-planned community, genuine rural character with Hill Country proximity, and home prices from $350K to $1.5M+. If you're seeking space, newer homes, equestrian-friendly land, or a slower pace with Austin access, Liberty Hill is the market to watch.

Why Liberty Hill Is Austin's Fastest-Growing Small City

Thirty-five miles northwest of downtown Austin in Williamson County, Liberty Hill sits at the intersection of Highway 29 and Ronald Reagan Blvd, a location that has made it ground zero for one of the Austin metro's most dramatic residential expansions. A city that once measured its growth in hundreds of new residents per year is now processing thousands of new permits annually as buyers seeking space, value, and Hill Country character migrate northwest from Austin's increasingly expensive core.

What distinguishes Liberty Hill from comparable growth markets is its character. Unlike heavily commercial suburban corridors, Liberty Hill retains a small-town core with a historic downtown, creek crossings, and the kind of open sky that draws buyers who want to feel the Texas landscape rather than simply observe it from a cookie-cutter subdivision. It is a city in rapid transition, adding residents and amenities at speed, while still offering the rural-adjacent lifestyle that drove that growth in the first place.

Key reasons buyers choose Liberty Hill in 2026:

  • Santa Rita Ranch, one of the most decorated master-planned communities in Texas, delivering resort amenities at attainable prices
  • New construction value: national builders competing for buyers keep pricing and incentives strong
  • Liberty Hill ISD, growing district with newer campuses including Santa Rita High School
  • Acreage opportunities: larger lots, horse properties, and equestrian-ready land unavailable closer to Austin
  • Hill Country proximity: cedar-covered rolling hills, limestone terrain, and open landscapes throughout and adjacent to the city
  • Lower price points than comparable Leander or Cedar Park new construction
  • Williamson County location with strong employer base and infrastructure investment

For buyers priced out of Leander's Travisso or Cedar Park's established neighborhoods, Liberty Hill frequently provides a compelling alternative that delivers more square footage, more land, and stronger community amenities per dollar than many comparable markets closer to Austin's core.

Santa Rita Ranch: Liberty Hill's Premier Community

Santa Rita Ranch is the undisputed flagship of Liberty Hill's real estate market and one of the most celebrated master-planned communities in all of Texas. Developed on a sweeping ranch landscape with Hill Country terrain, the community has earned national recognition for its extraordinary amenity programming, community culture, and quality of life, winning multiple "Community of the Year" designations from the National Association of Home Builders and Austin-area industry organizations.

Visit santaritaranch.com for full community details, builder information, and current availability across all villages.

Santa Rita Ranch by the Numbers

The community spans thousands of acres and is organized into distinct villages, each with its own character and price point. Key builders in Santa Rita Ranch have included Brightland Homes (formerly Centex), Perry Homes, Pacesetter Homes, and several custom/semi-custom operators. Home prices range from approximately $400,000 to $900,000+, with larger estate lots and custom builds pushing higher.

Santa Rita Ranch amenities are in a class of their own for the Liberty Hill market:

  • Multiple resort-style pools including a lazy river feature
  • Water park amenity complex, one of the most extensive in any Texas master-planned community at this price point
  • Fitness center and recreation facilities
  • Miles of walking, biking, and nature trails through native Hill Country terrain
  • Dog parks, splash pads, and playground structures throughout the community
  • Community events programming with hundreds of annual resident events
  • Onsite community managers and a robust HOA infrastructure
  • Fire pit gathering areas and community gardens

Liberty Hill ISD Schools

Liberty Hill ISD has grown dramatically to keep pace with the city's population surge, and the district has invested significantly in new facilities and campus capacity. The district serves the entire Liberty Hill community and surrounding Williamson County area, with campuses at all grade levels drawing improving ratings as newer facilities come online.

Key Liberty Hill ISD campuses:

  • Liberty Hill High School, the district's established flagship high school with growing AP and extracurricular programs
  • Santa Rita High School, newly opened campus serving the fast-growing southwest sections of the district, including Santa Rita Ranch residents; modern facilities and a strong early trajectory
  • Stiles Middle School, serves the middle grades for much of the growing Liberty Hill community
  • Multiple elementary campuses in various stages of construction and buildout to serve rapid growth

A key distinction between Liberty Hill ISD and neighboring Leander ISD: Liberty Hill ISD is growing fast, with newer campuses and improving metrics, but it has not yet achieved the long-established reputation of Leander ISD. For buyers where school district performance is a primary criterion, this distinction matters. For buyers who value brand-new facilities, growing communities, and the trajectory of a district scaling rapidly to meet demand, Liberty Hill ISD offers genuine appeal.

For current enrollment data, campus ratings, and attendance zone maps, visit libertyhillisd.org.

Acreage & Equestrian Properties

One of Liberty Hill's most distinctive advantages over its peer markets is genuine availability of acreage lots, horse properties, and equestrian-friendly land, at prices that would be unimaginable closer to Austin. On the rural roads and country lanes west and north of Liberty Hill's city center, buyers regularly find properties with 2, 5, 10, or even 20+ acres of Hill Country terrain, often with mature oaks, seasonal creeks, and working agricultural infrastructure.

Types of acreage properties available in the Liberty Hill area:

  • Horse properties with existing infrastructure: barn, fencing, round pen, pasture, water access, these turn-key equestrian setups are actively traded in the $600K–$1.2M range
  • Raw acreage for custom build: unimproved land with Hwy 29 or county road access, ranging from 2–50+ acres in $150K–$500K+ ranges depending on location and utilities
  • Mini-ranch lifestyle properties: 3–10 acres with a new or existing home, full privacy, and room for horses or livestock; typically $700K–$1.5M
  • Agricultural-exempt properties: larger tracts with existing agricultural exemptions that significantly reduce property tax liability, a major financial consideration for acreage buyers

Acreage Buyer Checklist for Liberty Hill

  • Confirm water source: city water, well water, or co-op water service and capacity
  • Verify septic system type, age, and capacity for intended use
  • Check deed restrictions and right-of-way easements, especially on rural roads
  • Confirm horse and livestock allowances with Williamson County zoning
  • Review agricultural exemption status and continuation requirements to preserve lower tax rates
  • Inspect any existing barn, fencing, and outbuilding structures before closing
  • Factor in TxDOT road access and driveway requirements for rural tracts, see txdot.gov

For property tax research on specific parcels, use the Williamson County Appraisal District (wcad.org) to look up current assessed values, tax rates, and exemption status for any address or parcel ID. Agricultural exemptions can reduce effective tax liability by 50–80% on qualifying properties, making this one of the most important due-diligence items for acreage buyers.

New Construction Options

Liberty Hill is overwhelmingly a new construction market in 2026. The vast majority of homes listed and sold are less than 5 years old, with active builder communities providing a continuous supply of inventory across price points. This creates both opportunity and complexity for buyers, navigating builder contracts, understanding incentive timing, and selecting the right lot position within a community all require experienced guidance.

The primary active builders in Liberty Hill in 2026 include:

  • Brightland Homes, active in Santa Rita Ranch and other Liberty Hill communities; attainable to mid-range price points
  • Perry Homes, strong value at mid-range, active in Santa Rita Ranch and Rancho Santa Clara
  • Pacesetter Homes, regional builder with strong product quality at the mid-range
  • David Weekley Homes, known for design-forward plans and energy efficiency
  • Taylor Morrison, active in select Liberty Hill communities with a premium product
  • Multiple regional custom builders serving the acreage and luxury segments

New Construction Navigation Tips, Liberty Hill 2026

  • Engage an independent buyer's agent before visiting model homes, builder commissions are built into pricing whether or not you have representation, so bring your agent
  • Compare base prices carefully: Liberty Hill base prices frequently omit lot premiums, upgrades, and structural options that add 10–25% to final cost
  • Ask about rate buydown programs: many builders are offering 2-1 buydowns or permanent rate reductions that meaningfully lower monthly payments
  • Confirm the MUD district tax rate and any additional HOA assessments in the specific section you're purchasing
  • Request a soil report for any lot with unusual terrain or drainage characteristics
  • Negotiate on move-in-ready inventory: completed homes sitting 30+ days often have the best incentive packages

Retail & Services Growth

Liberty Hill's retail and services landscape in 2026 is still catching up to its population growth, and this is one of the market's few genuine trade-offs versus closer-in Austin suburbs. Current residents rely primarily on nearby Cedar Park and Georgetown for major retail, dining, and services.

Current and near-term retail and services situation:

  • HEB: A new HEB store has been announced and is in development for Liberty Hill, this is a major quality-of-life upgrade for current residents who drive to Cedar Park or Leander for HEB grocery access
  • Existing retail: Liberty Hill's Hwy 29 corridor has CVS, Dollar General, local restaurants, and limited service retail
  • Cedar Park access: Major retail (HEB Plus, Costco, Target, major restaurants) is approximately 20–30 minutes south on Ronald Reagan Blvd in Cedar Park
  • Georgetown access: The Georgetown Square, HEB, and expanding retail along I-35 are accessible in 20–25 minutes to the east
  • Commercial development pipeline: The city's rapid growth is attracting commercial interest, and both the Hwy 29 corridor and Ronald Reagan Blvd are expected to see significant retail buildout over the next 3–5 years

Honest Assessment: The Retail Trade-Off

Liberty Hill buyers should plan for Cedar Park or Georgetown as their primary retail destinations for the near term. The incoming HEB and continued commercial development will improve the situation substantially over the next 2–3 years, but buyers accustomed to neighborhood-walkable retail should set realistic expectations. The trade-off is real, and it's also why Liberty Hill prices remain lower than Cedar Park and Leander equivalents, which is a structural advantage for buyers building long-term equity.

Commute to Austin

The Liberty Hill commute to Austin is the market's most significant practical consideration. At 35 miles northwest of downtown, Liberty Hill lacks the MetroRail access that makes Leander uniquely appealing for downtown Austin commuters. Highway driving is the primary option.

Frequently Asked Questions, Liberty Hill TX Real Estate

What is the average home price in Liberty Hill TX in 2026?

Liberty Hill home prices range from approximately $350,000 to $700,000 for new construction in master-planned communities like Santa Rita Ranch, Rancho Santa Clara, and Northgate Ranch. Luxury sections, larger lots, and acreage properties push prices to $700,000 to $1.5M+. Santa Rita Ranch is the most prestigious address, with prices spanning $400,000 to $900,000+ depending on builder, plan, and lot selection.

Is Santa Rita Ranch in Liberty Hill TX a good community?

Yes, Santa Rita Ranch is genuinely one of the best master-planned communities in Texas at its price point. It has won multiple national "Community of the Year" awards from the National Association of Home Builders. Key features include resort-style pools, a water park amenity complex, miles of trails, robust community programming, and professional HOA management. For buyers prioritizing lifestyle amenities within a new construction budget, it consistently delivers exceptional value relative to its price point.

How are Liberty Hill ISD schools rated?

Liberty Hill ISD has solid and improving ratings, with the district investing heavily in new facilities to match rapid population growth. Key campuses include Liberty Hill High School (established flagship), Santa Rita High School (newly opened, modern facilities), and Stiles Middle School. The district's trajectory is positive, though it has not yet achieved the long-established reputation of neighboring Leander ISD. Visit libertyhillisd.org for current ratings and campus information.

Can I find acreage and horse property in Liberty Hill TX?

Yes, Liberty Hill is one of the best places in the Austin metro to find acreage lots, horse properties, and equestrian-friendly communities. Properties with 2 to 20+ acres are regularly available on outlying roads west and north of the city center. Types include turn-key equestrian properties with barn and fencing ($600K–$1.2M), raw acreage for custom builds, and agricultural-exempt tracts that offer significant property tax advantages. Always verify water source, septic capacity, and agricultural exemption status with the Williamson County Appraisal District.

How far is Liberty Hill TX from downtown Austin?

Liberty Hill is approximately 35 miles northwest of downtown Austin, typically a 40–55 minute drive depending on traffic and time of day via Ronald Reagan Blvd and US-183A. There is no commuter rail access. For buyers whose primary employment is at The Domain, Cedar Park, or Williamson County campuses, commute times are considerably shorter, generally 35–50 minutes in normal conditions.

What are property taxes like in Liberty Hill TX?

Liberty Hill's combined property tax rate (Williamson County + Liberty Hill ISD + city + applicable MUD) typically ranges approximately 2.1% to 2.8% of assessed value depending on the specific subdivision and MUD district. New master-planned subdivisions often carry MUD assessments that increase the overall rate. Acreage properties with agricultural exemptions can see dramatically lower effective tax rates. Always verify the specific rate for any property at wcad.org before budgeting.

When is HEB coming to Liberty Hill TX?

A new HEB store has been announced for Liberty Hill and is in active development. This is one of the most anticipated quality-of-life improvements for current and prospective Liberty Hill residents. Until that store opens, residents primarily drive to Cedar Park (approximately 22 minutes) or Georgetown (approximately 26 minutes) for HEB grocery access. For the most current opening timeline, check local news sources and libertyhilltexas.org.

Is Liberty Hill TX a good place to invest in real estate in 2026?

Liberty Hill offers strong fundamentals for buyers building long-term equity: active population growth, improving infrastructure, a top-performing master-planned community in Santa Rita Ranch, and land prices that remain substantially below comparable Austin-closer markets. The primary risk factors are commute dependency on highway driving, retail infrastructure still catching up to population, and new construction supply that can moderate price appreciation in the near term. For buyers who plan to stay 5+ years and value space and community quality, Liberty Hill's value proposition remains compelling in 2026.
