## Why Buyers Choose Spicewood TX

Spicewood occupies a genuinely distinctive position in the Austin real estate market: it's close enough to Austin for practical commuting (or remote work with occasional in-person days), yet rural enough to offer the large-lot, low-density living that the city's near suburbs simply cannot replicate. As an unincorporated community straddling Travis and Burnet counties, Spicewood has no incorporated city limits — which means no city property tax layer and significantly fewer land-use restrictions than incorporated municipalities.

This combination — acreage availability, lake proximity, no city taxes, and wine-country cultural cachet — has attracted a diverse and growing buyer profile. Tech executives seeking weekend retreats that double as primary residences. Retirees from high-cost metros who want Texas's fiscal advantages with a nature-immersed lifestyle. Investors acquiring short-term rental properties on or near Lake Travis. And lifestyle buyers drawn specifically by the proximity to the [Texas wine trail](https://www.txwines.org) and Hill Country's outdoor recreation ecosystem.

Unlike master-planned suburbs such as Bee Cave or Lakeway, Spicewood does not offer curated retail corridors or walkable amenity centers. Its appeal is fundamentally different: open sky, native terrain, cedar and oak woodland, creek corridors, and a genuine sense of distance from urban density. Buyers who thrive in Spicewood are those who have consciously chosen the rural-adjacent lifestyle, not those who defaulted to it for price reasons.

**Key Draw:** Spicewood offers the closest approximation to true Texas Hill Country ranchland living within a practical drive of Austin. No incorporated city taxes, large lots, Lake Travis water access, and a thriving wine tourism economy create a lifestyle combination unavailable anywhere else in the metro.

The community's cross-county geography — partly in [Travis County](https://www.traviscad.org) and partly in [Burnet County](https://www.burnetcad.org) — affects property tax rates, appraisal processes, and school district assignments in ways buyers need to understand before purchasing. Working with an agent who has specific Spicewood transaction experience is not optional; the due diligence requirements are more complex here than in standard suburban markets.

## Lake Travis Waterfront in Spicewood

Spicewood's most coveted properties are its Lake Travis waterfront estates — and this is a genuine lake, not a pond or reservoir with restricted access. Lake Travis, managed by the [Lower Colorado River Authority (LCRA)](https://www.lcra.org), is one of the Highland Lakes chain on the Colorado River. It stretches approximately 65 miles and provides swimming, boating, kayaking, fishing, and water-skiing in a setting that remains predominantly natural along its shores.

Within the Spicewood area, several communities and sections offer direct or near-direct Lake Travis water access:

### Briarcliff

One of Spicewood's most established lake communities, Briarcliff includes a community marina, park, and golf course. Properties here range from lakefront lots with older cabins ripe for renovation to updated homes and custom builds. Prices run $500K to $2M+ depending on waterfront status and improvements. The community has an active HOA and a strong owner-occupant culture, though STR activity is common for lake-view and waterfront properties.

### Pedernales Estates & River Ranch

These sections offer larger lots — often one to five acres — with varying degrees of lake and river proximity. The Pedernales River, which feeds Lake Travis, runs through portions of this area, providing a different water access character than the main lake body. Properties here combine acreage with water access in a combination that commands strong premiums. Custom ranch-style homes and newer contemporary construction coexist with legacy lake cabins on larger parcels.

### Bee Creek & Lake Travis Corridor

The Bee Creek area and the Highway 71 corridor approaching Lake Travis offer a mix of residential and mixed-use parcels, including some of the most dramatic lakefront cliff-top lots in the Austin market. Estates with infinity-edge pool positions overlooking Lake Travis represent the pinnacle of Spicewood real estate — properties regularly trading at $2M to $4M+ for premium waterfront or high-bluff view positions.

### Water Levels at the Northwest End

Spicewood sits toward the northwest end of Lake Travis, where the lake narrows as it approaches the upper Pedernales arm. Lake Travis water levels fluctuate seasonally and in response to upstream precipitation and [LCRA](https://www.lcra.org) management decisions — and at the Spicewood end, where the lake is narrower and shallower, low-water periods have a proportionally larger impact on dock usability and lake access than they do at the wider southern sections. Buyers considering waterfront or water-access properties here should review historical LCRA water level data before committing. The trade-off is genuine seclusion: boat traffic is lighter, views are wilder, and true lakefront parcels at this end trade infrequently — making them worth tracking actively with an agent who watches this specific market.

## Large Lot & Ranch Properties

One of Spicewood's most distinctive real estate offerings is its inventory of genuine large-lot properties — from 2-acre ranchettes to 20+ acre working ranches. This inventory type is nearly extinct in Austin's inner-ring suburbs and commands significant premiums in other Hill Country communities. In Spicewood, it remains accessible at a range of price points.

### Ranchettes (2–10 Acres)

Two to ten-acre properties — often called ranchettes — represent the most common large-lot offering in the Spicewood market. These parcels are large enough for horses, gardens, outbuildings, and genuine privacy buffers, but small enough to be maintained without full-time agricultural staff. Prices typically run $600K to $1.5M depending on improvements, water availability (well vs. municipal), access to utilities, and proximity to the lake or highway.

Many ranchette buyers in Spicewood are making a lifestyle transition — from suburban HOA environments to genuine land ownership with agricultural exemption potential. Texas's agricultural exemption (ag exemption), when properly maintained, can dramatically reduce property tax burden by qualifying land under an agricultural use classification rather than residential. An experienced agent can walk buyers through the eligibility requirements and transition process.

### Hilltop Ranch Estates (10–30 Acres)

Larger parcels in the 10–30 acre range offer the most dramatic Hill Country views in the Spicewood market — hilltop positions with 270–360-degree panoramas over the Lake Travis watershed, cedar breaks, and distant ridgelines. These properties typically include a primary residence, guest quarters or casita, a barn or equipment storage, and fencing. Prices range from $900K to $2.5M+ depending on location, improvements, and water features.

Custom construction on raw or minimally improved hilltop acreage is an active sub-market. Buyers purchasing land for custom builds should factor in well drilling, septic engineering, utility extension, and access road construction — costs that vary significantly by parcel and can run $150K–$400K before the home is framed. Working with a builder experienced in Hill Country construction is as important as finding the right land parcel.

## Texas Wine Country Proximity

Few aspects of Spicewood living generate as much buyer enthusiasm as the proximity to Texas's wine country corridor. Spicewood sits in what is arguably the most accessible section of the [Texas wine trail](https://www.txwines.org) from Austin — with multiple estate wineries within 10–25 minutes of most Spicewood addresses.

### Nearby Wineries

- **Duchman Family Winery** (Driftwood, ~20 min): A leading Texas winery known for Italian varietals grown from Texas grapes, with a stunning stone winery building, event space, and wood-fired pizza. One of Austin's most-visited weekend destinations.

- **Perissos Winery** (Spicewood, ~10 min): An estate winery literally in Spicewood's backyard — producing Bordeaux-style wines from Hill Country AVA vineyards with a tasting room and relaxed rural setting.

- **Pedernales Cellars** (Stonewall, ~45 min): One of Texas's most acclaimed producers, Pedernales crafts award-winning Tempranillo and Viognier from its Pedernales Valley estate. Worth the additional drive for serious wine enthusiasts.

- **William Chris Vineyards** (Hye, ~50 min): A Texas wine trail anchor known for wine dinners, farm-to-table events, and a devoted following of Austin-area wine enthusiasts.

- **Jester King Brewery** (near Dripping Springs, ~30 min): For buyers who follow craft fermentation culture broadly, this landmark farmhouse brewery is a weekend-drive staple from Spicewood.

The wine tourism ecosystem around Spicewood creates a quality-of-life texture that is genuinely distinctive — weekend afternoons at tasting rooms, private winery dinners, harvest events, and wine club memberships are woven into the social fabric of the community. It attracts a cultivated, lifestyle-oriented buyer who values these experiences as part of daily living, not just occasional tourism.

From an investment perspective, the wine country proximity also strengthens STR demand for properties with outdoor entertaining spaces, covered terraces, and pool-spa combinations. Guests traveling to the Texas wine trail from Houston, Dallas, and San Antonio specifically seek Airbnb and VRBO properties that serve as convenient bases for multi-winery days — and Spicewood's positioning is ideal for this use case.

## School Districts in Spicewood

School district assignment in Spicewood is less straightforward than in incorporated suburban cities and requires careful due diligence for family buyers. The community spans multiple jurisdictions:

| District | Coverage Area | High School | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lake Travis ISD | Southern & central Spicewood (Travis Co.) | Lake Travis High School | Strong academics, UIL sports, largest district in area |
| Lago Vista ISD | Northern Spicewood near Lake Travis north shore | Lago Vista High School | Smaller district, more personal; good for families wanting smaller school environment |
| Marble Falls ISD | Far western Spicewood / Burnet County border | Marble Falls High School | Burnet County properties; strong district for a smaller city |

[Lake Travis ISD](https://www.ltisdschools.org) is the most common district for Spicewood buyers who prioritize school quality in a larger system context. Lake Travis High School offers a robust AP curriculum, strong athletics, fine arts programs, and consistent college placement results. The district's student population has grown substantially as western Austin has developed, bringing with it increased resources and program depth.

Lago Vista ISD serves buyers in the northernmost Spicewood sections. The smaller district creates a more personalized school experience — a meaningful consideration for families who prefer smaller class sizes and stronger teacher-student relationships over the broader program offerings of a larger district.

**Critical Step:** Never assume school district assignment based on neighborhood name or general location. In Spicewood, district boundaries can differ between adjacent parcels, and the school district boundary does not follow the Travis–Burnet county line precisely — two properties on the same road, a few tenths of a mile apart, can sit in different districts. Always verify through [Travis CAD](https://www.traviscad.org) (Travis County parcels) or [Burnet CAD](https://www.burnetcad.org) (Burnet County parcels) before making an offer on any property where school district access is a priority.

## STR Income Potential

Spicewood's unincorporated status makes it one of the most STR-permissive communities in the Austin area. With no city-level short-term rental ordinance, **no permit requirement**, and **no density restrictions**, investors and owner-occupants alike can operate vacation rentals on their properties without the regulatory hurdles that have made STR management increasingly complex inside Austin city limits.

### STR Performance by Property Type

STR performance in Spicewood varies significantly by property type and location:

- **Lakefront & Lake-View Properties:** The strongest performers. Properties with private boat docks, pools, and outdoor entertainment spaces can achieve gross annual revenues of $80,000–$180,000+. Peak demand runs Memorial Day through Labor Day, with a secondary season during fall foliage and wine harvest events. Occupancy rates for well-managed lakefront properties typically exceed 65%.

- **Wine Country Estates:** Properties positioned near Perissos, Duchman, or other wineries with outdoor entertaining infrastructure command strong weekend bookings year-round, with event weekends (harvest, special releases) driving premium nightly rates. Gross revenues of $50,000–$120,000 are achievable for quality wine-country-adjacent properties.

- **Hilltop Ranch Properties:** Dramatic views and privacy are strong STR draws, particularly for corporate retreats, family reunions, and couples' getaways. Properties with multiple sleeping units, hot tubs, and full outdoor amenities perform best. Revenues of $40,000–$90,000 annually for properties managed professionally.

- **Rural Ranchettes:** STR demand is more limited for basic rural properties without distinguishing amenities. However, horse-friendly properties attract a niche but loyal guest base. Revenues typically $25,000–$50,000 for well-presented offerings.

**Important Note:** STR income projections are estimates based on comparable performance data and vary significantly with management quality, property amenities, listing optimization, and seasonal factors. Buyers considering STR use should conduct specific due diligence with a qualified STR manager before purchase. Grewal RE Group does not guarantee STR performance outcomes.

Professional STR management companies with Hill Country expertise can handle listing creation, guest screening, cleaning coordination, and dynamic pricing — making passive income generation more realistic for owner-investors who don't want to self-manage. Management fees typically run 20–30% of gross revenue.

## Rural Due Diligence: Wells, Septic, Ag Exemptions & Unrestricted Land

Buying acreage in Spicewood is a fundamentally different transaction than buying a home in a master-planned Austin suburb, and the due diligence checklist is longer. These are the rural-specific items every Spicewood buyer should address before going under contract:

### Water Supply: Well vs. Water Co-op

Most Spicewood properties rely on a private well for potable water, and well performance — depth, yield, and water quality — varies significantly across the Hill Country limestone aquifer system. A professional well inspection and water quality test should be standard due diligence items, not optional add-ons, and well yield matters especially where irrigation, livestock watering, or multiple structures are planned. Some sections of the 78669 area are instead served by a rural water supply corporation (WSC) that delivers treated water via distribution lines — confirm whether a property is on well or co-op water before assuming either.

### Septic Systems

Septic systems handle wastewater on essentially all Spicewood properties. Conventional systems, aerobic treatment units (ATUs), and low-pressure dosing systems all appear in this area, and the type, size, and condition of the existing system should be evaluated by a licensed septic inspector. If you plan to add square footage, occupants, or an STR guest structure, the septic system's permitted capacity and available drain field area become planning constraints to understand upfront.

### Ag Exemptions & Rollback Taxes

Many Spicewood properties carry an agricultural exemption under the Texas Tax Code, which dramatically reduces assessed value for property tax purposes. Qualifying uses include livestock grazing, hay production, wildlife management, and beekeeping, among others. Critically, ag exemptions do not transfer automatically at sale — the new owner must establish their own qualifying agricultural use within a specific window or risk a rollback tax assessment covering up to five years of the tax savings. Understanding whether a property has an active exemption, what use supports it, and what continuation requirements apply is essential financial due diligence.

### Surveys, Deed Restrictions & Utilities

- **Survey and boundaries:** An accurate survey is non-negotiable on any acreage purchase. Rural plat maps and historic fence lines do not always correspond to legal property boundaries — commission a new survey by a licensed Texas land surveyor if the existing one is more than a few years old or any boundary is unclear.

- **Deed-restricted vs. unrestricted:** Spicewood is a mixed landscape of deed-restricted properties (particularly older rural subdivisions with recorded covenants) and completely unrestricted tracts. Unrestricted land offers maximum flexibility — build what you want, keep the animals you choose — but your neighbor has the same freedom. Confirm whether any deed restrictions are recorded and review them fully.

- **Utility access and costs:** Extending electric service can range from modest to very expensive depending on distance to the nearest Pedernales Electric Cooperative (PEC) line — confirm service availability and extension cost before finalizing a budget. Propane handles gas where natural gas lines do not reach.

- **Flood zone awareness:** Hill Country terrain creates dramatic drainage patterns, and the Edwards Plateau is subject to severe flash flooding. Properties near creeks, draws, or low-lying areas should be checked against FEMA flood maps and reviewed with a floodplain analysis before proceeding.

**Market Timing Note:** Days on market for Spicewood acreage properties typically runs longer than suburban Austin norms — often 60–120 days per [ABoR market data](https://www.abor.com/market-statistics) — reflecting the smaller buyer pool for rural land. This benefits prepared buyers: well-priced properties stay available long enough for proper due diligence, and longer-sitting sellers tend to be more negotiable than suburban sellers in multiple-offer situations.

## Commute & Connectivity

Spicewood's primary commute route to Austin is Highway 71, which connects west through Bee Cave and Lakeway before entering Austin via Hwy 360 (Loop 360) or Mopac (Loop 1). From most Spicewood addresses, the commute to downtown Austin runs 35–50 minutes in normal conditions — with morning eastbound rush hours and Friday afternoon westbound traffic representing the most congested windows.

Within the community itself, access corridors include RR 2322 (connecting toward Marble Falls to the west), RR 2147 providing a more northerly approach, and Spicewood Springs Road serving interior portions of the area. These are largely rural two-lane roads — beautiful to drive, but not high-speed arterials — and that road character is part of what preserves Spicewood's low-density feel.

### Commute Reality Check

Buyers considering Spicewood should honestly assess their commute frequency before purchasing. If you're in the office five days per week with early morning hours, the Spicewood commute represents a material lifestyle trade-off relative to Bee Cave or Lakeway. If you're hybrid or remote-first with two or fewer in-office days per week, the commute becomes manageable — particularly when offset by the lifestyle and value advantages of Spicewood living.

- **Spicewood to downtown Austin:** 35–50 minutes via Hwy 71 / Loop 360

- **Spicewood to The Domain (Tech corridor):** 30–45 minutes via Hwy 71 / Hwy 183

- **Spicewood to Austin Bergstrom Airport:** 50–65 minutes (airport-bound routes are less congested than downtown routes)

- **Spicewood to Marble Falls:** 20–30 minutes (for Burnet County amenities, shopping, and services)

Internet connectivity in Spicewood has improved substantially in recent years. Starlink satellite internet is widely used by residents in areas without fiber coverage, providing reliable bandwidth for remote work and video conferencing. Several sections of Spicewood now have access to fiber internet through local providers. Buyers should verify connectivity options for any specific property address before purchasing if remote work reliability is a primary concern.

The broader trend toward hybrid and remote work has materially improved Spicewood's buyer pool. Pre-2020, the community skewed heavily toward retirees and weekend cabin owners. Since 2021, a meaningful influx of working-age professionals with flexible schedules has shifted the community demographic and supported price appreciation across all property types.

## Natural Attractions & Outdoor Lifestyle

Spicewood's outdoor lifestyle credentials are exceptional even by Hill Country standards. The community's position within the Lake Travis watershed, adjacent to multiple state natural areas and park systems, provides a depth of outdoor recreation opportunity that draws serious outdoor enthusiasts.

- **Krause Springs:** A beloved natural swimming area with spring-fed pools cascading through ancient cypress trees and limestone bedrock in Spicewood proper — the main swimming hole drops over a limestone shelf into a deep, clear pool that holds a consistent temperature year-round. The family-owned property charges day entry by the person and also offers tent camping and RV hookups along the creek. Summer weekends fill early — capacity is limited and gates can close to new entrants by mid-morning on peak days. For Spicewood residents, it functions as the neighborhood pool and summer social hub; verify current access status seasonally.

- **Pedernales Falls State Park** ([TPWD](https://tpwd.texas.gov/state-parks/pedernales-falls)): Approximately 30 minutes from central Spicewood, this park features dramatic limestone falls, tubing, birding, and hiking along the Pedernales River. A consistent weekend destination for Spicewood residents.

- **Lake Travis Water Recreation:** Boating, wakeboarding, kayaking, paddleboarding, and fishing are all accessible from Spicewood's marinas and public boat ramps. The LCRA manages shoreline access points and maintains the lake system.

- **Balcones Canyonlands National Wildlife Refuge:** Located north of Spicewood, this refuge protects critical Hill Country habitat and provides exceptional birding — particularly during migration seasons. A destination for serious birders who are increasingly choosing Spicewood as a base.

- **Enchanted Rock State Natural Area:** Approximately 60 miles from Spicewood, this iconic pink granite dome offers rock climbing, hiking, and stargazing in one of Texas's most dramatic natural settings — a manageable day trip for residents.

- **Dark Skies:** Spicewood is genuinely dark at night in ways that central Austin and even outer suburbs are not — the Milky Way is visible on clear nights. This absence of light pollution is a direct product of the community's low density and cannot be manufactured; for buyers who value the night sky, it is a real amenity worth protecting through mindful site lighting on any property they develop.

## Spicewood vs. Lago Vista vs. Marble Falls

Buyers exploring Austin's northwest corridor typically compare Spicewood against Lago Vista and Marble Falls — three distinct communities along the same geographic band that serve very different buyer profiles.

### Spicewood (78669)

The most rural of the three. Spicewood offers the largest average lot sizes, the least commercial infrastructure, the strongest ag-exempt and unrestricted-land character, and pricing that reflects both rural remoteness and proximity to Lake Travis. There is no grocery store in Spicewood proper — the nearest full-service commercial corridors are Marble Falls to the west (roughly 20–25 minutes) and the Bee Cave / Lakeway area to the east (roughly 25–35 minutes). This is the choice for buyers whose priority hierarchy starts with acreage and ends with HOAs.

### Lago Vista

Sitting on Lake Travis's north shore in Travis County, Lago Vista offers a more organized residential character while retaining a small-town feel: its own city government, city utilities in most areas, and a conventional single-family home market. Its primary draw is lake access through multiple water access association parks with boat ramps, fishing piers, and swimming areas, and Lago Vista ISD serves the community with a tight-knit campus culture. Prices overlap with Spicewood's lower range but trend higher for lakefront and lake-access properties. Buyers who want a recognized small town on the lake with city water and services tend to prefer Lago Vista over the more unstructured Spicewood.

### Marble Falls

A proper city with a growing downtown, city utilities, direct Lake LBJ and Lake Marble Falls access, and a commercial amenity base Spicewood entirely lacks — restaurants, a walkable small-city downtown, and a real grocery store within minutes of home. Marble Falls ISD serves the city, and prices range from surprisingly affordable in established neighborhoods to significant for lakefront estates. For buyers who want Hill Country life but feel the pull of a real town center, Marble Falls often wins the comparison — and at 20–30 minutes from Spicewood's core, it doubles as the errand hub for Spicewood residents.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Spicewood TX Real Estate

**Q: What is the average home price in Spicewood TX in 2026?:**

Home prices in Spicewood, TX range widely: from around $400K for rural ranchettes and lakeside cabins to $3M+ for true waterfront estates on Lake Travis. Large-lot hilltop ranches and wine-country estates typically run $600K to $2M. The wide range reflects the exceptional diversity of property types available in this unincorporated community — from modest cabins on small lake lots to expansive custom estates on 20+ acres.

**Q: Is Spicewood TX good for short-term rentals (STR)?:**

Yes. As an unincorporated community straddling Travis and Burnet counties, Spicewood has no city-level STR restrictions. Lake-adjacent and waterfront properties attract strong vacation rental demand from Austin residents and out-of-state visitors year-round. Properties with private water access or lake views can generate $50,000 to $150,000+ annually in gross STR revenue depending on size, amenities, and management quality. Wine-country-adjacent properties also perform well, particularly during harvest events and winery weekends.

**Q: Which school district serves Spicewood TX?:**

Spicewood is served by two primary districts depending on the specific parcel: **Lake Travis ISD** covers most of the Spicewood area closest to Austin (and is the most common district for buyers prioritizing school quality), while **Lago Vista ISD** serves portions further north and west near Lake Travis's north shore. A small portion near the Burnet County line may fall in Marble Falls ISD. Always verify the district for any specific property address through [Travis CAD](https://www.traviscad.org) or [Burnet CAD](https://www.burnetcad.org) before purchase.

**Q: How far is Spicewood TX from Austin?:**

Spicewood is approximately 30–40 miles west of Austin, depending on the specific address and route. The commute to downtown Austin typically runs 35–50 minutes in normal conditions via Highway 71. Residents closer to Bee Cave via FM 620 can sometimes reduce this by 5–10 minutes. Remote and hybrid work has significantly improved quality of life for Spicewood residents who previously faced the full daily commute five days per week.

**Q: What wineries are near Spicewood TX?:**

Spicewood sits in the heart of Texas's Hill Country wine region on the [Texas wine trail](https://www.txwines.org). Within 10–25 minutes are Perissos Winery (in Spicewood itself), Duchman Family Winery (Driftwood), and several other producers. Pedernales Cellars, William Chris Vineyards, and other acclaimed estates are within 45–60 minutes. The proximity to multiple estate wineries — many with event facilities — is a unique quality-of-life differentiator that attracts a growing segment of lifestyle buyers from Austin and beyond.

**Q: Can I buy acreage or a ranchette in Spicewood TX?:**

Yes. Spicewood is one of the closest communities to Austin where buyers can realistically find 2–20+ acre properties without paying full ranch prices for extreme rural remoteness. Large-lot estates, ranchettes, and working agricultural parcels are available from roughly $600K to $2M+, with prices varying significantly based on acreage, water access (lake, river, or well), existing improvements, and view quality. Many parcels qualify for Texas's agricultural exemption if properly maintained under an ag use classification, which can significantly reduce annual property tax burden.

**Q: What is Krause Springs in Spicewood TX?:**

Krause Springs is a privately operated natural swimming hole and campground in Spicewood, TX — a series of spring-fed pools and creeks flowing through cypress trees and limestone rock formations, widely regarded as one of the most scenic swimming destinations in the Texas Hill Country. The family-owned property charges a day-entry fee and offers tent and RV camping on its wooded grounds. It operates seasonally, and entry may be limited on peak summer days. For Spicewood-area residents it is a major quality-of-life asset — effectively a neighborhood amenity rather than a tourist stop.
