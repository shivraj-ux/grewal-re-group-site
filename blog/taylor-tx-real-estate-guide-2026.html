Taylor TX Real Estate Guide 2026

By Shivraj Grewal, CLHMS Guild • CNE • May 9, 2026 • 15 min read
Taylor, Texas, once a quiet agricultural town 35 miles northeast of Austin, is now one of the fastest-transforming real estate markets in Central Texas. Samsung's $17B+ semiconductor campus has arrived, 2,000+ direct jobs are online, and home values have appreciated 20–40% since the 2021 announcement. This guide covers everything buyers, sellers, and investors need to know about Taylor TX real estate in 2026.

Taylor TX: Austin's Next Semiconductor Boomtown

For most of the 20th century, Taylor, Texas was known for cotton, Czech heritage, and some of the best Texas barbecue in Williamson County. Today it's becoming known for something much bigger: one of the largest semiconductor manufacturing investments in American history. When Samsung announced its Taylor Semiconductor campus in 2021, real estate professionals, economists, and savvy investors took notice immediately.

The city of Taylor sits in Williamson County, sharing the same business-friendly, low-tax environment that has made the broader Austin metro one of the most sought-after real estate markets in the United States. With SH-130 providing direct toll road access to Austin in 35–45 minutes and Georgetown just 20 minutes away, Taylor is firmly within the Austin commuter orbit, yet its housing prices still represent significant value compared to inner-ring suburbs.

As of May 2026, median home prices in Taylor range from $230,000 to $500,000, depending on age, size, and location. That represents a remarkable appreciation curve from pre-announcement levels, but analysts note there remains meaningful upside as Samsung's campus reaches full staffing capacity and supporting industries cluster nearby.

Samsung Taylor Campus

The Samsung Austin Semiconductor Taylor campus is among the largest greenfield semiconductor manufacturing investments in United States history. The initial announced investment of $17 billion has grown as Samsung has accelerated production timelines, and the facility now represents one of the most significant chip fabrication plants on American soil, comparable in scale to Intel's Ohio campuses and TSMC's Arizona fabs.

The campus sits just north of central Taylor on a massive land footprint that required years of site preparation, water infrastructure upgrades, and road improvements coordinated between Samsung, the City of Taylor, and Williamson County. The facility manufactures advanced logic chips, products essential to smartphones, automobiles, data centers, and AI hardware, positioning Taylor at the center of the domestic semiconductor manufacturing movement.

Economic Ripple Effects on Taylor Real Estate

The direct employment of 2,000+ Samsung positions at the Taylor campus significantly underrepresents the total economic impact on the region. Economists typically apply a multiplier of 3x–5x for advanced manufacturing jobs: each direct semiconductor role supports 3–5 additional jobs in suppliers, services, logistics, retail, and healthcare. That suggests the campus could support 6,000–10,000+ total jobs in the regional economy by peak operations.

For real estate, the implications are significant. Demand for housing in Taylor, Hutto, Georgetown, and Round Rock has grown substantially, and the supply pipeline, while expanding rapidly, has not fully caught up to absorption rates. Rental vacancy rates have tightened, single-family home absorption has accelerated, and new master-planned community developments continue to break ground throughout western and northern Taylor.

Taylor ISD Schools

Taylor Independent School District serves the majority of Taylor residents. Families considering a move to Taylor should understand both the current state of the district and the trajectory of improvement underway as Samsung investment, including community benefit agreements and expanded local tax base, flows into educational resources.

Taylor High School is a smaller 4A campus that has historically been overshadowed by higher-rated districts in the region. However, enrollment is growing, new facilities are being planned, and district leadership has made improving academic programming a priority as the community's demographic and economic profile shifts rapidly.

Taylor ISD Campus Overview

  • Taylor High School, Grades 9–12; growing enrollment; competitive athletics program
  • Taylor Middle School, Grades 6–8; core curriculum with electives
  • Naomi Pasemann Elementary, Pre-K through 5th grade
  • TJ Rusk Elementary, Pre-K through 5th grade
  • Ed & Laura Negley Elementary, Pre-K through 5th grade

Families seeking higher-rated district options may find that some Taylor-adjacent parcels qualify for Georgetown ISD or Hutto ISD, depending on exact location. Always verify school zoning directly with each district before purchasing if school assignment is a primary decision criterion. For official information, visit taylorisd.org.

School District Shopping Tip

  • School district boundaries do not always align with city limits, verify by address
  • New master-planned communities may be gerrymandered into different ISDs than surrounding properties
  • Samsung's tax contributions to Taylor ISD are expected to fund significant capital improvements by 2027–2028
  • Private school options in Georgetown (~20 min) and Round Rock (~25 min) are accessible for families seeking alternatives

Historic Taylor Downtown

Taylor's historic downtown, centered on Main Street and the surrounding blocks, is one of the city's most charming assets and a meaningful quality-of-life differentiator. Unlike many Texas boomtowns that developed entirely as suburban sprawl, Taylor has a genuine walkable historic core with a distinct, authentic identity that new residents consistently cite as a key reason they chose Taylor over comparable communities.

Murals District

Taylor's Murals District has emerged as a cultural landmark, with large-scale public art installations celebrating the city's Czech heritage, cotton-farming history, and evolving identity as a technology hub. The murals draw visitors from across Central Texas and provide a sense of place that cookie-cutter master-planned suburbs simply cannot manufacture.

Murphy Park

Murphy Park anchors Taylor's downtown outdoor experience, hosting community events, farmers markets, and seasonal festivals throughout the year. The park sits within walking distance of local restaurants, coffee shops, boutiques, and the historic Moody Hotel, one of the oldest continuously operating hotels in Texas, now a boutique destination drawing weekend visitors from Austin.

Taylor International Barbeque Cookoff

One of the most prestigious BBQ competitions in Texas calls Taylor home. The Taylor International Barbeque Cookoff draws tens of thousands of visitors annually and reinforces the city's authentic Texan identity in an era when many Austin-area communities feel increasingly generic. For residents, it represents an annual community anchor event that builds neighborhood cohesion, a quality-of-life factor that genuinely matters in the long run.

For community events and downtown information, visit taylor.tx.us.

New Construction Growth in Taylor TX

Taylor's housing supply pipeline is the direct market response to post-Samsung demand. As of May 2026, multiple master-planned communities are in various stages of development across the Taylor area, primarily along major arterials and in growth corridors to the west and north of existing neighborhoods. The pace of permitting has increased significantly year-over-year, and city infrastructure investments, including water, wastewater, and road improvements, are keeping pace with growth.

Active Builders in Taylor TX (2026)

National builders including DR Horton, Lennar, and Centex/Pulte have established active communities in and around Taylor. Several regional and semi-custom builders are also active, offering product at higher price points with greater personalization options. The competitive builder landscape is generally favorable for buyers, with incentives including mortgage rate buydowns, closing cost contributions, and appliance packages commonly offered on unsold inventory.

Investment Opportunity in Taylor TX

Taylor's investment case rests on several converging fundamentals that are relatively rare in the Austin metro at this stage of the real estate cycle: a clear and durable demand catalyst (Samsung), a still-affordable entry point relative to inner-ring suburbs, a business-friendly county environment, improving public infrastructure, and a historic downtown core that differentiates Taylor from purely suburban alternatives.

Residential Rental Investment

Single-family residential investment in Taylor has attracted attention from both individual investors and institutional players. Rental demand is strong, driven by Samsung employees and contractors who have not yet committed to purchasing, as well as construction workers, logistics professionals, and service industry workers who have relocated to support the growing local economy. Short-term rental demand also exists near downtown Taylor, particularly during events like the BBQ Cookoff and the bluebonnet season.

Long-Term Appreciation Outlook

Long-term appreciation in Taylor is tied primarily to Samsung's operational trajectory and the broader semiconductor industry cycle. Analysts generally view the Taylor campus as a multi-decade industrial anchor, the equipment cost and facility investment make it economically irrational to idle or relocate. Assuming the semiconductor cycle continues and domestic production incentives remain in place, Taylor's economic foundation should remain solid through 2035 and beyond.

Secondary appreciation drivers include: SH-130 corridor commercial development, Georgetown-to-Taylor infrastructure improvements, potential future employers attracted by Taylor's workforce and infrastructure, and continued population growth in Williamson County which remains one of the fastest-growing counties in the United States.

Getting to Austin from Taylor TX

Taylor's commutability to Austin is one of the key factors in its residential appeal. While it sits 35 miles northeast of Austin's urban core, two major routes provide reliable connectivity. For TxDOT road conditions and planning, visit txdot.gov.

SH-130, The Primary Commute Corridor

State Highway 130 (the Central Texas Turnpike) is the primary commute corridor for Taylor residents heading to Austin, Round Rock, Cedar Park, or the Domain. SH-130 connects directly to the Austin toll road network and provides access to I-35, SH-45, and MoPac Expressway. In typical morning traffic, Taylor to the Domain is approximately 35–45 minutes; Taylor to downtown Austin is 40–55 minutes.

SH-79, The Georgetown Connection

SH-79 connects Taylor westward through Hutto and Round Rock before intersecting major Austin arterials. This route provides more direct access to Round Rock (20 minutes), Georgetown (20 minutes), and North Austin employment corridors including the Samsung Austin Semiconductor campus on the north side of Austin.
