## Pflugerville and Manor, Austin's Affordability Corridor

Pflugerville and Manor sit northeast of Austin along the I-35 corridor, separated from the city's core by roughly 12-18 miles of suburban development that has grown steadily since the 1990s. Pflugerville is the more established of the two, with a population of approximately 72,000, its own city infrastructure, a strong parks system anchored by Lake Pflugerville, and a diverse housing stock spanning everything from 1990s ranch homes in Pecan Park to new master-planned communities with resort-style amenities. The 78660 and 78664 zip codes that define Pflugerville are among the most searched in the Austin metro for buyers whose budgets cap around $400,000.[[1]](#src-1)

Manor is smaller, roughly 17,000 residents, and sits just 12 miles from downtown Austin along Highway 290 East. It is Austin's closest affordable suburb by distance, which creates an unusual dynamic: Manor buyers get genuine proximity to the city at prices that feel more like Bastrop or Hutto than a true Austin-adjacent community. The city's rapid growth is reshaping its character in real time; new master-planned developments have arrived faster than supporting retail and infrastructure in some areas, creating pockets of suburban density without the commercial density that makes a neighborhood feel complete. That is changing as the Samsung supply chain and new employers anchor themselves in the northeast corridor, but it is the honest reality buyers should factor into their decisions today. Together, Pflugerville and Manor form a 30,000-plus acre affordability corridor that is directly positioned to benefit from one of the most significant economic events in Central Texas history.

The most consequential long-term driver for Pflugerville and Manor real estate is not a local decision at all, it is a $17 billion bet placed by Samsung on a patch of land in Taylor, Texas, 25 miles northeast of Austin. Samsung's Taylor semiconductor fabrication facility, which began initial production phases and is expanding toward full operation, is designed to create approximately 3,000 direct high-wage jobs in its first phase, with long-term projections reaching 30,000 when full capacity and associated supplier facilities are considered. The ripple effect through the northeast Austin corridor is already visible in the numbers and will accelerate as the facility reaches full employment.[[7]](#src-7)

Taylor itself is approximately 25-35 minutes from Pflugerville and Manor under normal traffic conditions, making both cities natural bedroom communities for Samsung employees and the supplier ecosystem that follows any major semiconductor fab. TSMC's Arizona facility created 40,000+ indirect jobs in the Phoenix metro, the Taylor facility is smaller but in a market with significantly less existing housing inventory, meaning demand pressure concentrates more acutely. Pflugerville and Manor benefit most because they are the closest established communities to Taylor with existing infrastructure, schools, and retail. For buyers with a 5-10 year horizon, the Samsung tailwind is a structural demand driver that is not priced into current values the way it eventually will be.

## Pflugerville Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Pflugerville's housing stock spans four decades of development, which means buyers have genuine choice across price points, lot sizes, and community character. Blackhawk is the community that surprises most buyers who have written off Pflugerville as entry-level only, a golf-anchored master-planned development with a public course, resort-style pool and amenity center, and homes ranging from $380,000 to $580,000 for larger four- and five-bedroom builds. The Blackhawk Golf Club gives residents a recreational anchor that elevates the community's feel well above its price point, and resale demand here is consistent.[[1]](#src-1)

Verona is Pflugerville's mid-market sweet spot, a master-planned community with well-maintained common areas, neighborhood pools, and homes from $350,000 to $500,000. The community draws young families who want the Pflugerville ISD schools and suburban amenities without the premium associated with Blackhawk. Pecan Park is where Pflugerville's established, value-oriented stock lives: 1990s and 2000s construction, larger lots than most new communities, and a price range from $310,000 to $420,000 that gives budget-conscious buyers genuine options. Copperfield and Springbrook round out the mid-market, with Springbrook skewing slightly newer and Copperfield offering some of the best value-per-square-foot in the city. Across all of these communities, Pflugerville ISD schools are the common thread, and the district is trending in the right direction as it has added resources and improved campus leadership over the past several years.

## Manor, Austin's Closest Affordable Suburb

Manor occupies a peculiar position in the Austin market: geographically, it is the closest affordable community to downtown Austin, sitting just 12 miles from the city center along Highway 290 East. Psychologically, it still carries some of the stigma associated with its past as a small, underserved rural community at the edge of Austin's growth boundary. That gap between geographic reality and perceived position creates an opportunity for buyers willing to do the homework.[[4]](#src-4)

ShadowGlen is Manor's standout community, a golf course neighborhood that consistently attracts buyers who want the Manor price point without the Manor stigma. Homes range from $310,000 to $480,000 with golf-front lots commanding the top of that range, and the ShadowGlen Golf Club gives the neighborhood a focal point and physical identity that separates it from generic suburban Manor development. Beyond ShadowGlen, several newer master-planned communities along Georgetown Road and the Highway 290 corridor offer recent construction in the $330,000-$440,000 range with community amenities including pools and playgrounds. Manor ISD is the key school district consideration here, and at a 5-7/10 range on GreatSchools, it is improving but not yet a premium driver the way LTISD or Round Rock ISD are. Buyers primarily focused on school quality should model carefully before committing to Manor over Pflugerville.

## Pflugerville ISD vs Manor ISD, What Buyers Need to Know

Both communities have their own independent school districts, and the performance gap between them is a real factor that affects both lifestyle and long-term value. Pflugerville ISD serves approximately 28,000 students across a comprehensive campus network, with schools generally rated 6-7/10 on GreatSchools. Pflugerville High School has seen meaningful improvement in its academic programs and extracurriculars, and the district has made visible infrastructure investments in recent years. While Pflugerville ISD does not match the prestige of Round Rock ISD or Leander ISD, it is a functional, improving district that serves working families well, and the affordability of the zip codes it covers means buyers get significantly more house than they would in a Round Rock or Cedar Park address at the same budget.[[5]](#src-5)

Manor ISD is smaller, roughly 11,000 students, and its school ratings span a wider range, from 5/10 to 7/10 depending on the campus. The district faces the challenges common to fast-growing suburban districts: enrollment is outpacing facility construction in some areas, and the incoming population is more economically diverse than many Austin suburbs. For families where school quality is the primary location driver, this is an honest limitation of the Manor market. For buyers primarily motivated by price, proximity to downtown, or investment return, Manor ISD's limitations are priced in, and the value opportunity is real precisely because the district has not yet become a premium driver the way better-rated districts have in their respective corridors.

## Investment Analysis, Buy and Hold in Northeast Austin

For investors and buyers who think about real estate as a financial asset alongside a lifestyle choice, Pflugerville and Manor offer the strongest cash-flow math in the Austin metro. Single-family rentals in Pflugerville typically achieve cap rates of 5-6% on current acquisition cost, meaningfully higher than the 3-4% cap rates available in central Austin, Mueller, or South Congress-adjacent zip codes. Manor pushes that further, with cap rates of 5.5-6.5% on entry-level product, reflecting both the lower acquisition prices and the strong rental demand from the growing workforce in the northeast corridor.[[2]](#src-2)

The investment thesis here is not purely income-focused, it is appreciation-plus-income. Lower entry prices mean buyers can acquire multiple properties at the capital required for one central Austin home, creating portfolio diversification within a single market. The Samsung demand story creates a long-tail appreciation catalyst that is not speculative in the way tech-boom appreciation can be, semiconductor manufacturing facilities run for 30+ years and anchor communities the way auto plants and military installations once did in manufacturing cities. For investors running a 10-year hold analysis, the combination of positive cash flow today and structural demand growth from the Taylor fab corridor creates a risk-adjusted return profile that compares favorably against higher-priced Austin alternatives. Property tax rates are higher in both Pflugerville (2.45%) and Manor (2.71%) than in central Austin (approximately 1.97%), which affects net operating income, this must be modeled explicitly rather than assumed away. But even after factoring in the higher tax burden, the returns hold up when entry prices are $340,000-$370,000 versus $650,000+ for comparable square footage inside Austin proper.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Pflugerville TX a good place to buy a house?:**

Yes. Pflugerville offers the best combination of affordability and Austin metro proximity in the northeast corridor. With a median home price around $370,000, a 25-minute commute to downtown, and the long-term demand tailwind from Samsung's $17B fab plant in nearby Taylor, Pflugerville gives buyers more house for their money than virtually any other Austin suburb at a comparable distance from the city core. Pflugerville ISD schools rate 6-7/10 and are improving, and the community has extensive parks, Pflugerville Lake, and master-planned neighborhood amenities across multiple communities at different price points.

**Q: What is the average home price in Pflugerville TX?:**

The median home price in Pflugerville, TX as of early 2026 is approximately $370,000. Prices range from around $310,000 for smaller homes in established neighborhoods like Pecan Park to $580,000+ for larger homes in the Blackhawk golf community. New construction in active master-planned communities generally starts around $360,000 and runs to $490,000 for move-in ready product. Property tax rates of approximately 2.45% add roughly $9,000-$9,800 per year in taxes on a $370,000-$400,000 home, higher than Austin proper but offset significantly by the lower purchase price.

**Q: How far is Pflugerville from Austin?:**

Pflugerville is approximately 18 miles northeast of downtown Austin, roughly a 20-30 minute drive under normal traffic conditions via I-35 or TX-130. During peak commute hours, particularly southbound on I-35 in the morning, the drive can extend to 40-50 minutes. Many Pflugerville residents use the TX-130 toll road as a reliable alternative to I-35, shaving significant time off commutes to south and central Austin. The community is also well-positioned for employers along the I-35 North corridor and in the Domain/North Austin tech cluster.

**Q: Is Manor TX safe?:**

Manor's safety profile has improved as the city has grown and invested in its police department and infrastructure. Newer master-planned communities like ShadowGlen and developments along Georgetown Road tend to have lower crime rates than older areas closer to downtown Manor. As with any city, neighborhoods vary significantly, buyers should review specific neighborhood crime data through the Travis County Sheriff's Office and visit communities at different times of day before committing. The rapid population growth driven by Samsung-era demand has also brought more retail, lighting, and commercial activity that generally correlates with improved safety conditions in emerging suburban communities.
