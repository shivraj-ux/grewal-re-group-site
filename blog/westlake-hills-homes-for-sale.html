Westlake Hills Homes for Sale, 2026 Buyer's Guide

A complete guide to buying a home in Westlake Hills, Texas: Eanes ISD ratings, price ranges by home size, the best streets and micro-neighborhoods, tax advantages, and what every buyer must know before making an offer in 2026.

By Shivraj Grewal · CLHMS Guild · TREC #736060 · May 8, 2026 · Westlake Hills, Austin, Texas

Westlake Hills homes for sale represent some of the most sought-after residential real estate in the Austin metro, and for good reason. The City of Westlake Hills offers a rare combination that buyers pay a premium to access: Eanes Independent School District, one of Texas's highest-rated public school systems; Hill Country topography with views and mature live oak canopy; private acreage lots with meaningful separation from neighbors; and an 8-to-12-mile commute to downtown Austin or the major tech campuses along MoPac and Loop 360. This guide covers everything a buyer needs to understand before entering the Westlake Hills market in 2026, from price ranges and neighborhood micro-sections to tax implications, utility infrastructure, and the legal due diligence points that frequently surprise first-time buyers in this submarket.[1]

Why Westlake Hills Homes Command a Premium Over the Rest of Austin

The premium embedded in Westlake Hills real estate is not arbitrary, it is the product of several structural factors that have persisted through market cycles for decades and show no signs of reversing.

Eanes ISD. The single most powerful driver of Westlake Hills home values is access to Eanes Independent School District. Eanes ISD consistently ranks among the top five school districts in Texas by academic performance metrics, and Westlake High School regularly places in the top 10 high schools statewide. For families with school-age children, who represent a disproportionate share of luxury home buyers, this is not a marginal consideration. It is often the determining factor that keeps buyers focused on the Eanes corridor even when comparable square footage is available for significantly less money in other parts of Austin.[5]

Hill Country topography and views. Westlake Hills sits on the Balcones Escarpment, the geological boundary between the Texas Hill Country and the Austin metro flatlands. This gives the neighborhood dramatic elevation changes, panoramic views of rolling limestone terrain, and the visual character of the Texas Hill Country, all within 10 miles of Austin's central business district. Views from hilltop lots in Westlake Hills can stretch 20–30 miles on clear days, and the mature live oak tree canopy throughout the neighborhood creates privacy and a naturalistic feel that is simply not replicable in flat, newer subdivisions.

Privacy and acreage. Lots in Westlake Hills range from 0.25 acres for older platted subdivisions near the Bee Cave Road corridor to 5+ acres for estate properties along the hilltops and ridgelines. Even at the more modest end, homeowners enjoy meaningful separation between structures, no shared walls, no postage-stamp yards, and typically significant setbacks from the street. City of Westlake Hills zoning regulations enforce minimum lot sizes and setback requirements that protect the neighborhood's low-density character.[6]

Commute access. Despite its Hill Country feel, Westlake Hills is genuinely close to Austin's employment centers. The drive to downtown Austin on MoPac Expressway is 15–20 minutes in normal conditions. The Domain tech campus and major employers on the north side of Austin are accessible via Loop 360 in 25–35 minutes. For buyers coming from competitive markets like the San Francisco Bay Area, New York, or Seattle, the combination of Hill Country lifestyle and sub-30-minute commute is a significant and meaningful quality-of-life upgrade.

Westlake Hills Price Ranges by Home Size and Condition, 2026

Understanding the price tiers within Westlake Hills helps buyers calibrate expectations and identify where their budget fits best in the current market.

Entry tier: $900,000–$1.3 million. At this price point, buyers are typically looking at 1970s–1990s construction, 1,800 to 2,400 square feet on lots of 0.3 to 0.5 acres. These homes are priced for the land and the school district access rather than for turnkey condition. Many in this range require kitchen and bath updates, have single-car garages, and sit on older lots with mature tree coverage that can limit additions or pools without careful planning. They represent excellent value for buyers who can tolerate a renovation project or who plan to tear down and build custom.

Mid-range tier: $1.5 million–$2.5 million. The most active segment in Westlake Hills. Homes here are typically 2,600–4,200 square feet, updated or renovated within the last decade, on lots of 0.5 to 1.5 acres with pools, outdoor living spaces, and three-car garages. Hill Country views appear at this price tier, and finishes begin to include quartz or stone countertops, hardwood floors throughout, and primary suites with soaking tubs and walk-in showers. This is the sweet spot where the Eanes ISD premium is most efficiently captured.[1]

Luxury tier: $3 million–$8 million. Custom and semi-custom construction, 4,500–7,000+ square feet, private gated entries, chef's kitchens, home offices, theaters, and resort-style outdoor spaces. Views from these properties are often exceptional, panoramic Hill Country vistas, canyon views, or treetop perspectives that make the lot itself as valuable as the structure. Architectural pedigree matters in this range; buyers should research whether the home was designed by a notable architect and who the builder was, as quality varies widely.

Estate tier: $10 million and above. A small but consistent segment of ultra-luxury estate properties changes hands in Westlake Hills each year. These are typically multi-acre compound properties with guesthouse or casita structures, private pools and tennis courts, wine cellars, and views that have been professionally cleared to maximize sight lines. Supply in this tier is extremely limited, often only 2–5 properties at any given time, and buyers frequently transact off-market through private networks. This is where Compass Private Exclusives and established agent relationships become critical to access.

Top Streets and Micro-Neighborhoods Within Westlake Hills

Westlake Hills is not a monolithic neighborhood, it contains distinct micro-sections with meaningfully different character, price ranges, and lifestyle attributes. Understanding these micro-markets helps buyers focus their search and avoid overpaying for a location that does not match their priorities.

The Bee Cave Road corridor (78746, near Loop 360). The most accessible and entry-level section of Westlake Hills. Streets off Bee Cave Road between Loop 360 and Capital of Texas Highway offer older 1970s–1980s construction within walking distance of Eanes Elementary School. These properties are highly sought by families because of the school proximity, and they trade at a premium relative to their condition because the lot values are well-established. Convenient to Central Market, restaurants, and amenities on Bee Cave Road.

Rob Roy and the ridgeline estates. The elevated ridgelines above Bee Cave Road, including the gated community of Rob Roy, represent some of the most exclusive real estate in the Austin metro. Properties here command panoramic Hill Country views, private gated access, and lot sizes of 1–5+ acres. Rob Roy HOA homes rarely dip below $3 million and often trade in the $4M–$8M range. These are for buyers who prioritize privacy, prestige, and views above all else.

Rollingwood-adjacent streets. The southeastern portion of Westlake Hills, adjacent to the City of Rollingwood, offers a slightly more accessible entry point. Streets like Redbud Trail and nearby cul-de-sacs have smaller lots but excellent school access and proximity to Barton Creek Greenbelt trail heads. Many buyers who cannot stretch to the hilltop price points find tremendous value in this micro-section.

Lake Austin Boulevard waterfront corridor. The northwestern edge of the 78746 zip code borders Lake Austin, and a small number of homes here have Lake Austin waterfront access or boat dock rights. These properties command significant waterfront premiums, typically $1M to $3M above comparable non-waterfront homes, and they trade in a very limited, highly specialized market. Waterfront properties in this corridor sell quickly when priced correctly and attract national relocation buyers.

Westlake Hills Schools, Eanes ISD Deep Dive

The Eanes Independent School District is the primary reason buyers pay a premium for Westlake Hills real estate, so it deserves detailed attention beyond the headline rankings.

Eanes Elementary School. A neighborhood K–5 school with consistent academic excellence. Eanes Elementary has earned a top rating on state accountability assessments and is known for active parent involvement, strong enrichment programming, and a community culture that extends beyond classroom hours. The school is within walking or biking distance of many central Westlake Hills neighborhoods.[5]

Hill Country Middle School. Serving grades 6–8, Hill Country MS is part of the Eanes ISD feeder pattern and maintains the district's strong academic trajectory. Notably, Hill Country MS offers an extensive range of elective courses including visual arts, music, and technology, programming that often exceeds what larger district middle schools can offer. The school's athletics program is competitive at the district and regional level.

Westlake High School. Routinely ranked among Texas's top 10 high schools by the state accountability system and by independent ranking organizations. Westlake High offers over 30 Advanced Placement courses, with AP exam pass rates consistently above 75%, well above state and national averages. The school's athletics programs, particularly football, swimming, and golf, have produced state championships and college athletes. Graduates routinely earn admission to highly selective universities, and the school's college counseling resources rival private school programs in other markets.[5]

Buyers relocating from outside Texas often ask whether Eanes ISD ratings are self-reported or independently verified. The Texas Education Agency's public accountability ratings are externally administered, and Eanes ISD's A ratings are consistent and independently verifiable through the Eanes ISD website and TEA public data portals.

Westlake Hills vs. Austin City Limits, Property Tax Implications

One of the most underappreciated financial advantages of buying within the City of Westlake Hills is the property tax structure. Because Westlake Hills is an independent municipality and not part of the City of Austin, homeowners here do not pay Austin city property taxes.

Property tax rates in Westlake Hills typically run in the range of 1.55% to 1.75% of assessed value annually, depending on the precise location and applicable taxing entities (Travis County, Eanes ISD, Travis County Healthcare District, and the City of Westlake Hills general fund). By comparison, homes inside Austin city limits carry the additional City of Austin tax rate of approximately 0.54%, bringing total effective rates to roughly 2.1%–2.3%.[3]

On a $2 million home, the difference is meaningful: a Westlake Hills property might carry $32,000–$35,000 in annual property taxes versus $42,000–$46,000 for a comparable home inside Austin city limits. The savings of $7,000–$11,000 per year compounds significantly over a 5–10 year ownership period. For estate-level properties at $5M or $8M, the annual savings from the Westlake Hills tax structure can exceed $20,000–$30,000 per year.

Note that all Texas homeowners should file for the homestead exemption with Travis Central Appraisal District (TCAD) to cap annual assessed value increases at 10% per year on a primary residence.[3] This cap becomes extremely valuable during periods of rapid appreciation, as Westlake Hills experienced from 2019 to 2022, and protects long-term owners from tax shock when the market runs hot.

What to Know Before Making an Offer in Westlake Hills

Westlake Hills has several property-level characteristics that routinely surprise buyers who are unfamiliar with the market. Understanding these in advance prevents expensive surprises during due diligence.

HOA vs. non-HOA properties. Many streets in Westlake Hills have no HOA whatsoever, they are within a city that enforces its own zoning and tree ordinances, so lot-level deed restrictions may be minimal or non-existent. However, gated communities like Rob Roy and certain newer developments within the 78746 zip code do have active HOAs with monthly or annual dues, architectural review committees, and restrictions on fencing, exterior modifications, and short-term rentals. Always confirm HOA status, dues, and documentation before making an offer.

Septic systems are common. A significant percentage of Westlake Hills properties, particularly those on larger lots and further from Bee Cave Road, are served by private septic systems rather than Austin's municipal sewer. This is not inherently a problem, but it requires diligent inspection. A septic inspection by a licensed inspector (ideally including a camera inspection of the lateral lines) should be a standard component of your due diligence. Septic systems in good condition require pumping every 3–5 years and last 25–40 years with proper maintenance. Failed or undersized systems require replacement at costs of $15,000–$40,000.

Well water vs. city water. Some properties in Westlake Hills, again, typically those on larger lots in the outer portions of the city, are served by private wells rather than municipal water supply. Well water quality in this area of Travis County is generally good but requires annual testing for bacterial content and periodic testing for nitrates and other minerals. City water service is available in most of the more densely developed sections of Westlake Hills along the Bee Cave Road corridor.

Tree ordinances. The City of Westlake Hills enforces a tree ordinance that restricts the removal of protected trees (typically 8-inch trunk diameter and above) without a permit. This ordinance is strictly enforced and can significantly impact your plans to add square footage, a pool, or a detached garage. Always commission an arborist's survey and a review of the tree ordinance before purchasing any property where you anticipate exterior construction or significant landscaping changes.[6]

Impervious cover limits. City of Westlake Hills zoning restricts the percentage of a lot that can be covered by impervious surfaces (structures, driveways, pool decks, patios). This directly affects how large you can build, how much pool deck you can add, and how extensively you can pave. If you are purchasing with plans to maximize the envelope, adding a large structure to a modest existing footprint, confirm the impervious cover limits with the city and your architect before you are under contract.

Frequently Asked Questions About Westlake Hills Homes

What is the average home price in Westlake Hills TX?

As of spring 2026, the median sale price for homes in Westlake Hills is approximately $2.4 million, based on trailing 90-day Austin Board of Realtors MLS data. Entry-level properties, typically older construction on smaller lots, begin around $900,000 to $1.3 million. Mid-range updated homes run $1.5 million to $2.5 million, and luxury or estate properties range from $3 million to $8 million or more. Prices are approximately 12–15% below their 2022 peak, creating a meaningful buying opportunity for qualified buyers with a medium-to-long investment horizon.

Is Westlake Hills in Austin city limits?

No. Westlake Hills is an independent municipality, the City of Westlake Hills, incorporated in 1953 and entirely separate from the City of Austin. It has its own city government, city manager, ordinances, and tax rate. Homes within the City of Westlake Hills do not pay City of Austin property taxes, which typically saves homeowners $2,500 to $11,000 per year depending on assessed value. The city is located in Travis County, southwest of downtown Austin, within the 78746 zip code. Some 78746 addresses are within Austin city limits (particularly near the Barton Creek and Rollingwood edges), so it is important to confirm the exact municipal boundary for any specific property you are considering.

What school district is Westlake Hills in?

Westlake Hills is served by Eanes Independent School District (Eanes ISD), one of the top-rated school districts in Texas. Students attend Eanes Elementary School (K–5), Hill Country Middle School (6–8), and Westlake High School (9–12). Westlake High consistently ranks in the top 10 high schools in Texas by academic performance, AP course offerings, and college readiness. The Eanes ISD school system is the primary driver of demand, and the premium, for Westlake Hills real estate. Buyers should always confirm school assignments for a specific address directly with Eanes ISD, as district boundaries and feeder patterns can occasionally change.

How far is Westlake Hills from downtown Austin?

Westlake Hills is approximately 8 to 12 miles from downtown Austin depending on where within the city you are traveling to and from. The typical drive time via MoPac Expressway (Loop 1) or Bee Cave Road is 15 to 25 minutes outside of rush hour, and 30 to 45 minutes during peak morning and afternoon traffic. Many Westlake Hills residents also commute via Loop 360 (Capital of Texas Highway) to reach the Domain, the Arboretum, or employers in north Austin in 25–35 minutes. The proximity to both downtown Austin and major tech campuses on the northwest corridor is one of Westlake Hills's most valued and durable attributes.
