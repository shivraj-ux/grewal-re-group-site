## Pflugerville Overview: Affordable, Diverse, and Growing Fast in the NE Austin Corridor

Pflugerville sits along the northeast stretch of IH-35 in Travis County, roughly 15 miles from downtown Austin. The city grew from a small agricultural settlement, the Pfluger family farmed the area in the late 19th century, into one of Austin's most active suburban growth corridors, driven by affordability, IH-35 access, and a sustained wave of in-migration from buyers priced out of central Austin and South Austin submarkets. That growth has been significant and sustained: Pflugerville has seen consistent population increases through the 2010s and 2020s, with an estimated population above 65,000 as of 2026, and the 78660 ZIP code now represents one of the highest-volume transaction areas in Travis County outside the city limits. Most of Pflugerville sits in Travis County, though some eastern sections along the SH-130 corridor fall within Williamson County, a jurisdictional detail that matters for property tax purposes.

The city's character is genuinely diverse, one of the most demographically mixed communities in the Austin metro, with substantial Hispanic, South Asian, East African, and African-American populations alongside long-tenured Anglo families and an active influx of tech and professional workers from the broader Austin employment base. That diversity is not incidental; it shows in the restaurant landscape along Pecan Street and Heatherwilde Boulevard, in the community programming at Lake Pflugerville Regional Park, and in the school culture across Pflugerville ISD campuses. Families moving from major metro areas in California, the Pacific Northwest, and the Northeast often remark on how different the social texture feels here compared to other Austin suburbs, and for many, that difference is a feature rather than a concern.

Key corridors in Pflugerville include IH-35 (the commercial and commute spine), Pecan Street (the historic main street and growing mixed-use district), Heatherwilde Boulevard (a major north-south residential and commercial artery), Kelly Lane (connecting to newer residential developments in the northeast), and Dessau Road (running south toward the Austin tech corridor and domain area). The Pflugerville Town Center area provides an anchor for retail, dining, and community activity. Amazon's fulfillment and logistics operations and the broader IH-35 employment corridor to the north, anchored by the Samsung Austin semiconductor campus in nearby Taylor, have made Pflugerville's location increasingly strategic for workers who do not want to pay Round Rock or Cedar Park prices.

## Pflugerville Real Estate Market 2026: Prices, Value Proposition, and What Is Selling

The Pflugerville real estate market in 2026 offers a price range of approximately **$280,000 to $550,000**, making it one of the Austin metro's most accessible entry points with genuine long-term fundamentals behind it[[1]](#src-1). That range covers a wide variety of home types: entry-level three-bedroom homes in established Pflugerville neighborhoods built in the 1990s and early 2000s start in the $280K–$350K band; mid-range four-bedroom homes in master-planned communities along Kelly Lane and Heatherwilde price from $350K to $450K; and larger, newer construction homes in communities like Blackhawk, Falcon Pointe, and Stone Hill Town Center push toward $500K–$550K for well-appointed five-bedroom homes with upgraded finishes and amenity access.

What is selling in 2026 reflects Pflugerville's dual buyer profile. First-time buyers, many of them millennials priced out of Austin proper, are active at the $300K–$380K price point, where Pflugerville offers move-in-ready homes with yards and functional floorplans at price points that require a conventional 30-year mortgage rather than a jumbo loan. This buyer cohort is often looking at 1,500–2,200 square feet, three or four bedrooms, and an established neighborhood with schools already in place. Simultaneously, move-up buyers and relocating families are active in the $400K–$550K tier, where Pflugerville's newer communities offer the community amenities, finishes, and school assignments they want at a significant discount to comparable inventory in Round Rock, Cedar Park, or the Austin city limits.

The headline numbers behind that range: Pflugerville's median sale price sits at approximately **$360,000** as of Q1 2026, up from roughly $335,000 in 2024, with average days on market improving to around 42 days, active inventory tightening toward 680 listings, a list-to-sale price ratio near 97%, and months of supply compressing to approximately 3.1[[1]](#src-1). The $300K–$420K segment is the most competitive in the city, driven by first-time buyers and Samsung and Tesla employee relocations competing for limited inventory, while the $420K–$550K tier remains more buyer-friendly with additional negotiating room, since new construction in that range is still fairly active.

Days on market have moderated from the frenzied 2021–2022 era, and the 2026 Pflugerville market rewards accurate pricing and strategic preparation. Homes priced within 2–3% of current comparable sales are moving in reasonable timeframes; overpriced inventory is sitting longer as buyers have regained leverage they lacked during the pandemic peak[[1]](#src-1). For sellers, this means that presentation, professional photography, and an accurate initial list price matter more than they did three years ago. For buyers, there are genuine negotiating opportunities on homes that have been on market beyond 30 days, a dynamic that did not exist in 2021 and 2022.

## Lake Pflugerville: Swimming, Fishing, Trails, and a Community Amenity Unlike Any Other NE Austin Suburb

Lake Pflugerville Regional Park is 212 acres of open parkland centered on a lake that offers something genuinely unusual in the Austin suburbs: a designated swimming area where residents can actually get in the water. Most Austin-area lakes are either private, reservoir-only (no swimming), or restricted to boat and kayak access. Lake Pflugerville, owned and operated by the City of Pflugerville, maintains a designated swim beach, lifeguard coverage during peak season, and a family-friendly entry point that makes it a practical weekend destination rather than a special-occasion trip[[3]](#src-3).

Beyond swimming, the park offers fishing access to a stocked lake, kayaking and canoeing for non-motorized watercraft on calm water (with seasonal rentals available), a mountain bike trail network through the lakeside terrain that has become popular with the broader Austin riding community, beach volleyball courts at the swim beach, hiking and biking trails that loop around the perimeter, open green space and picnic facilities, a playground, and a disc golf course. The trail network connects to broader greenway infrastructure in the area, making the park a hub for residents who run, walk, or bike as part of their daily routine. Community events, seasonal festivals, fitness programming, and holiday gatherings, use the park as their primary venue, which means Lake Pflugerville functions as a community gathering point that drives civic cohesion in a way that most suburban parks, however well-designed, do not.

The real estate significance is straightforward: properties within walking or biking distance of Lake Pflugerville Regional Park carry a premium over comparable homes further from the park, and that premium has been consistent over time. Buyers specifically seeking lake amenity access without lake-price real estate, the kind of premium you would pay in Lakeway, Hudson Bend, or Lago Vista for a water-adjacent lifestyle, will find Pflugerville's offering genuinely competitive at a fraction of the price. That is not a sales pitch; it is a structural market feature that has driven buyer behavior in this ZIP code for years.

## Employment Corridor: Amazon, Samsung, and NE Austin Tech Access

Pflugerville's northeast Austin location positions its residents within practical daily commute range of one of the most significant employment buildouts in Central Texas over the past decade. The IH-35 corridor north of Austin, running through Pflugerville, Round Rock, and extending toward Taylor and Hutto, has attracted a concentration of industrial, logistics, and semiconductor manufacturing employment that has fundamentally changed the economic geography of the region.

Amazon operates multiple fulfillment, delivery station, and logistics facilities in and around the northeast Austin corridor, employing thousands of workers across full-time, part-time, and seasonal roles at competitive wage levels. For Pflugerville residents, proximity to these facilities translates to short commutes, often 10–15 minutes, for a substantial pool of employment that pays at levels sufficient to support homeownership in Pflugerville's price range. This is not a peripheral observation: it is one of the primary economic engines that has sustained Pflugerville's residential demand through market cycles.

The Samsung Austin semiconductor campus, located in Taylor, approximately 25 miles northeast of Pflugerville via TX-130, represents one of the largest single manufacturing investments in Texas history, with multiple chip fabrication plants under construction and operational phases that are adding thousands of direct jobs and a broader supply chain ecosystem of supporting employers. Engineers, technicians, project managers, and operations staff at Samsung and its supplier network have identified Pflugerville as a practical and affordable home base, contributing to demand in the $380K–$500K price range where professional-class buyers seek value relative to their Austin city-limit counterparts[[3]](#src-3).

Beyond Amazon and Samsung, Pflugerville residents access the broader Austin tech and professional services employment base via the MoPac/IH-35 commute corridor to the Domain, the tech campuses along Burnet Road, and downtown Austin. The Tesla Gigafactory in southeast Austin, Apple's north Austin campus, Dell's Round Rock headquarters, Oracle's Austin offices, and the major healthcare employers along the north Austin medical corridor, St. David's and Ascension Seton among them, are all within a 15–30 minute commute radius, which means a two-earner household with jobs at different employers can often make the combined commute math work from Pflugerville when it fails everywhere else at this price point. Remote and hybrid work has further expanded Pflugerville's buyer pool, workers who are in-office two or three days per week find the 20–30 minute commute to Austin's employment centers entirely manageable relative to the substantial home value differential they capture by purchasing in Pflugerville rather than closer to the city core.

## The SH-130 Commute Advantage: Bypassing IH-35 for Tesla, Samsung, and the Airport

SH-130, the toll road officially designated Texas State Highway 130 and also known as the Pickle Parkway, runs along Pflugerville's eastern edge and is arguably the city's most underrated asset. While the rest of the metro debates IH-35 widening timelines, residents on Pflugerville's east side enjoy largely free-flowing toll road access that bypasses IH-35 congestion entirely for southeast- and northeast-bound commutes, a structural advantage that no amount of IH-35 construction will replicate soon[[5]](#src-5).

The typical drive times tell the story. Pflugerville to the Tesla Gigafactory in southeast Austin runs approximately 18–22 minutes via SH-130 south, consistent most hours of the day, dramatically faster than most north or northwest Austin addresses can manage. Pflugerville to the Samsung campus in Taylor is approximately 20–28 minutes northeast via SH-130 and SH-79, a clean highway run with no signal-heavy surface roads. Austin-Bergstrom International Airport is approximately 20–25 minutes south via SH-130, faster than from most of north and northwest Austin. Round Rock, including the Dell headquarters area, is a short 12–18 minute run north via SH-45 and IH-35, and Apple's north Austin campus is approximately 18–25 minutes west via SH-45 and US-183. Downtown Austin runs 25–40 minutes depending on route and hour, competitive with most Austin suburbs even if not as fast as in-city addresses.

SH-130 does carry a toll, with costs varying by segment and vehicle class; current rate tables are published by TxDOT[[5]](#src-5). For most daily commuters, the monthly toll cost, typically in the $80–$150 range for regular usage, is easily justified by the time savings and reduced vehicle wear compared to stop-and-go IH-35 driving. A TxTag or comparable transponder secures the lowest per-mile rates. For buyers working at Tesla, Samsung, or along the TX-130 logistics corridor, this commute profile is often the deciding factor between Pflugerville and every other north Austin suburb.

## Pflugerville ISD: Three High Schools, a Growing District, and Strong Family Appeal

Pflugerville ISD is the school district serving Pflugerville and surrounding areas in Travis County, with a student population that has grown substantially alongside the city's residential expansion[[2]](#src-2). The district operates three high school campuses, a relatively unusual configuration for a suburban district of its size, and a meaningful one for families with high school-aged students who want choices in school culture, athletics, and academic programming.

**Pflugerville High School** is the district's original high school campus, with a long-tenured community identity and established traditions in athletics, performing arts, and academic programming. PHS carries the history and community connection that comes with being the founding campus, alumni networks, homecoming traditions, and a school culture that generations of Pflugerville families have participated in. For families who value that kind of institutional continuity in a high school experience, Pflugerville High is a genuine draw.

**Weiss High School**, named for educator and community figure Henry Weiss, opened as the district expanded to accommodate growing enrollment on the city's north and east sides. Weiss has built its own athletic and academic identity since opening, competing across UIL classifications in sports that draw strong community followings. For buyers purchasing in the northern and northeastern portions of the 78660 ZIP code, Weiss is typically the assigned campus and has developed a dedicated community around it.

**Hendrickson High School** serves portions of the district's southwest and newer residential developments, with a campus culture that blends strong STEM programming, competitive athletics, and a community that reflects the diversity of the neighborhoods it serves. Hendrickson has established itself as a capable and ambitious campus within the Pflugerville ISD system, with college placement outcomes and extracurricular programming that families arriving from high-performing suburban districts in other states find competitive[[4]](#src-4).

I always encourage buyers to verify current attendance zone assignments for specific addresses directly through the Pflugerville ISD address lookup tool before making purchase decisions based on school assignments. Zone boundaries have shifted in this district as new residential developments come online, and the specific campus serving a given address is the definitive data point, not a neighbor's recollection or a general neighborhood description.

## Typhoon Texas and Community Entertainment: Pflugerville as a Family Destination

Typhoon Texas waterpark in Pflugerville is one of the Austin metro's largest and most active water parks, anchoring a broader entertainment and family-activity ecosystem that gives the city an amenity footprint well above what most comparably sized Texas suburbs can claim. The park features wave pools, water slides across multiple thrill levels, a lazy river, and dedicated areas for younger children, the kind of full-service water park experience that typically requires a day trip or a resort stay, available as a short drive from home for Pflugerville residents.

Beyond Typhoon Texas, Pflugerville's entertainment infrastructure includes the Pflugerville Town Center retail and dining district, a growing restaurant corridor along Pecan Street that reflects the community's culinary diversity, and regular community programming centered on Lake Pflugerville Regional Park. The city hosts seasonal events, the Deutschen Pfest heritage festival, summer outdoor movie events, holiday programming, that sustain a community calendar and give residents recurring reasons to engage with their neighbors and their city.

For families evaluating Pflugerville as a long-term home base, this combination of accessible, affordable entertainment, a swim lake, a major waterpark, a walkable town center, strong park infrastructure, within a city at Pflugerville's price point is genuinely distinctive. Comparable entertainment amenity stacks in Lakeway, Bee Cave, or Cedar Park come paired with real estate prices 30–60% above what buyers pay in Pflugerville for similar square footage.

## Buying Tips for Pflugerville: New Construction vs. Older Inventory, IH-35 Access, and HOA Considerations

Buying in Pflugerville in 2026 involves choices that benefit from clear-eyed evaluation. The city's residential inventory is broadly split between established neighborhoods built primarily from the 1990s through 2010s, and newer master-planned communities developed from 2010 to present, and the two categories involve meaningfully different buyer considerations.

**Older inventory** in established Pflugerville neighborhoods, areas along Heatherwilde Boulevard, near the original town center, and in communities developed before the city's rapid 2010s growth, typically offers larger lots relative to newer development, more mature trees and landscaping, and lower HOA dues or no HOA at all. The trade-off is that older homes may need mechanical updates (HVAC, roof, water heater), and finishes will reflect their era of construction. For buyers comfortable with a moderate renovation program or willing to purchase a home that has been already updated, these neighborhoods offer the strongest price-per-square-foot value in the Pflugerville market.

**New construction** in communities like Blackhawk, Falcon Pointe, and developments along Kelly Lane offers modern floorplans, current energy efficiency standards, builder warranties, and the amenity packages, community pools, splash pads, trails, clubhouses, that master-planned development brings with it. The price premium over older inventory is real but often justifiable for buyers who want low-maintenance living and modern finishes without a renovation project. Builders remain active in Pflugerville as of 2026, and buyers in this segment should understand the distinction between spec homes (available immediately), quick-move-in homes (near completion), and build-to-suit contracts, which involve longer timelines and carry their own negotiating dynamics with builder sales teams.

**IH-35 access and noise** is a due diligence item that some buyers underweight. Properties within a quarter-mile or less of IH-35, particularly those oriented toward the highway, can experience meaningful traffic noise, especially from truck traffic on this major freight corridor. This is not a deal-breaker for many buyers, particularly those commuting on IH-35 daily who value proximity over quiet, but it is worth a site visit at different times of day before committing. Properties one or more blocks off the highway generally experience minimal noise impact.

**HOA structures** in Pflugerville vary widely. Newer master-planned communities carry active HOAs with dues typically ranging from $400 to $900 annually, architectural review requirements, and maintained common areas and amenity centers. Older Pflugerville neighborhoods often have minimal or no HOA, giving owners greater flexibility for outbuildings, fencing, landscaping, and use of their property. Neither structure is inherently superior, the right choice depends on your lifestyle, your tolerance for community governance, and what you plan to do with your home over time.

**Property taxes** deserve line-item attention in a Pflugerville budget. Effective total tax rates in Pflugerville typically run approximately 2.0%–2.4% of appraised value depending on how the taxing jurisdictions overlap for a given parcel, city, county, Pflugerville ISD, and in many newer communities a MUD (municipal utility district) that adds its own levy[[6]](#src-6). Most of the city sits in Travis County, where appraised values are verified through the Travis Central Appraisal District, while some eastern sections along SH-130 fall in Williamson County under the Williamson Central Appraisal District. Verify the specific parcel's taxing entities and combined rate before budgeting, two otherwise similar homes a few blocks apart can carry meaningfully different annual tax bills based on MUD boundaries alone.

## Pflugerville Neighborhoods: Blackhawk, Cele Road, Stone Hill Farms, and the Established Core

Pflugerville's residential map breaks into a handful of distinct sub-areas, and knowing the differences narrows a home search considerably.

**Blackhawk** is the city's premier master-planned community, a large development along the SH-130 corridor in northern Pflugerville that has become the go-to address for buyers seeking new construction with resort-style amenities at accessible price points. The community offers a well-appointed amenity center with a resort-style pool, splash pad, fitness center, and clubhouse, plus miles of internal hike-and-bike trails that link into the broader Pflugerville trail system. Multiple national builders, Centex, Pulte, and D.R. Horton among them, have participated across Blackhawk's phases, providing a range of floorplans, and the community is generally zoned to Hendrickson High School. New construction in Blackhawk typically runs from approximately $340,000 to $520,000, with some older-phase resales available below $320,000, and its SH-130 position makes it especially convenient for Tesla and Samsung-area commuters.

**The Cele Road corridor** on Pflugerville's eastern edge carries much of the city's newest construction, with some sections crossing into Williamson County and direct access to the SH-130 commute routes toward the Samsung Taylor campus. Buyers here trade established landscaping for current floorplans and energy standards at competitive price points.

**Stone Hill Farms** offers established suburban living anchored by proximity to Stone Hill Town Center, where H-E-B, Target, Home Depot, Best Buy, and dozens of restaurants and retailers sit within walking distance or a short drive, one of the strongest retail-adjacency positions in the NE Austin corridor.

**Springbrook and Highland Park** represent the established core: older, well-settled neighborhoods with mature trees, larger lots than typical new construction, and lower price points generally in the $280K–$380K band. These are the neighborhoods where Pflugerville's strongest price-per-square-foot value lives, for buyers who prioritize yard space and location over new finishes. Along **Cameron Road**, the city's southwestern edge transitions into northeast Austin proper, with urban-feel neighborhoods at relatively accessible price points for buyers who want to stay close to the Austin city limits.

## Pflugerville vs. Hutto vs. Manor: NE Austin Affordability Comparison

Buyers exploring northeast Austin's affordability corridor frequently evaluate Pflugerville, Hutto, and Manor on the same shortlist. All three sit within a comparable distance northeast of Austin, all three price below Round Rock and Cedar Park to the northwest, and all three draw buyers seeking to maximize value relative to central Austin alternatives. The distinctions between them are meaningful and worth understanding before committing to one of these markets.

**Pflugerville** is the most established and complete community of the three, an incorporated city with a longer residential history, the largest amenity footprint (Lake Pflugerville, Typhoon Texas, Pflugerville Town Center), three high school campuses within its ISD, and the strongest existing retail, dining, and services infrastructure. Pflugerville's IH-35 frontage and proximity to the Austin city limits give it the best commute positioning of the three for workers whose jobs are in Austin's central employment zones. It is also the most expensive of the three in most price segments, reflecting its superior amenities and location. Buyers willing to pay $350K–$550K will find Pflugerville's combination of community maturity, amenity access, and schools difficult to match in the NE corridor.

**Hutto**, immediately northeast of Pflugerville in Williamson County, offers the deepest affordability in the NE Austin corridor, prices there can push below $280K for three-bedroom homes, and new construction is active at price points that stretch value further than Pflugerville's average[[1]](#src-1). The trade-off is a less mature community footprint, a longer Austin commute (roughly 30–40 minutes versus Pflugerville's 20–30), and a Williamson County location that places homeowners in a different tax jurisdiction with its own property tax dynamics. Hutto ISD has grown rapidly and is investing in capacity, but the district's depth of programming and established identity does not yet match Pflugerville ISD's at this stage of development. For buyers who need to maximize value below $320K and are comfortable with a slightly longer commute and a community still building its identity, Hutto is a legitimate choice. For buyers who can stretch to Pflugerville's price range, the additional amenity and community infrastructure typically justifies the difference.

**Manor**, located in Travis County southeast of Pflugerville along US-290 East and TX-130, offers a different value proposition, good freeway access to the eastern employment corridor and Austin-Bergstrom International Airport, Manor ISD schools, and price points that remain competitive with Hutto at the low end of the NE Austin market. Manor's community commercial infrastructure is more limited than Pflugerville's, and the daily experience of living in Manor involves more dependence on driving to Round Rock, Pflugerville, or central Austin for retail and dining. For buyers whose work is in the eastern corridor, the airport, logistics facilities along TX-130, or southeastern Austin, Manor's location can be a better commute fit than Pflugerville's IH-35 orientation. For buyers oriented toward the northern and central Austin employment zones, Pflugerville's positioning is generally superior.

Buyers also frequently weigh Pflugerville against **Round Rock and Cedar Park** to the northwest, and there the math is different: Round Rock's 2026 median sits around $445,000 and Cedar Park's around $465,000, an $85,000–$105,000 gap over Pflugerville's roughly $360,000 median that buys significantly more house in Pflugerville[[1]](#src-1). The trade-off is honest and worth naming: Pflugerville ISD, while solid and improving, generally rates somewhat below Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD in state accountability scores and third-party ratings[[4]](#src-4). Many buyers in the $350K–$420K band are making a deliberate choice, more home and better employer proximity via SH-130 over marginally higher school district ratings, and for Tesla and Samsung commuters in particular, Pflugerville's location often wins the analysis outright. Buyers for whom district ranking is the single top priority should look at Round Rock ISD-zoned communities and budget for the premium.

The comparison is ultimately about fit rather than ranking. Each of these three communities serves a specific buyer profile, and the best choice depends on your employment location, budget ceiling, school priorities, and tolerance for commute length and community maturity trade-offs.
