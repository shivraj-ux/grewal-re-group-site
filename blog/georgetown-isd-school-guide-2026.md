## Georgetown ISD: A District on the Rise

Georgetown Independent School District serves the City of Georgetown and surrounding unincorporated Williamson County, one of the fastest-growing counties in the United States for a decade running. The district has transformed from a small-town agricultural community into a dynamic suburban system, reflecting the broader story of Georgetown itself: a historic square anchoring explosive residential growth pushing outward in every direction.

For buyers considering Georgetown, understanding GISD is not optional, it's foundational. School boundaries determine property taxes, neighborhood character, and resale value. In a market where competing buyers are sophisticated, knowing which campus serves your street before you write an offer separates skilled purchasers from those who discover surprises at closing.

GISD's rapid enrollment growth reflects the district's appeal. The district has consistently added campuses, programs, and personnel to accommodate the influx of families drawn by Georgetown's combination of affordability relative to central Austin, the charm of the historic town square, and the high quality of life afforded by proximity to Lake Georgetown, the San Gabriel River parks, and easy access to the Austin tech corridor.

## High Schools: Georgetown Eagles & East View Patriots

### Georgetown High School, Home of the Eagles

Georgetown High School is the district's flagship campus and one of the most storied programs in Williamson County. The Eagles football team has long been a point of community pride, filling Georgetown's stadium on Friday nights and drawing alumni from across the region. Beyond athletics, Georgetown HS has invested significantly in its academic offerings, including Advanced Placement coursework, dual enrollment opportunities with Austin Community College, and a well-regarded fine arts program.

The Georgetown High School Performing Arts Center is a crown jewel of the campus, a modern, professional-quality venue that hosts theater productions, choir concerts, band performances, and community events throughout the year. For families who prioritize fine arts alongside athletics, Georgetown HS delivers a well-rounded extracurricular ecosystem.

Georgetown HS also maintains GISD's agricultural programs, reflecting Georgetown's deep rural roots. The school's FFA chapter is one of the strongest in the region, with students competing at district, area, and state levels. This Ag program is not a relic, it's an active, well-funded pathway that connects Georgetown's agricultural heritage to modern career and technical education.

### East View High School, Home of the Patriots

East View High School opened to serve Georgetown's rapidly growing western and northwestern areas, particularly the master-planned communities that have emerged along Ronald Reagan Boulevard and the TX-29 corridor. As a newer campus, East View has benefited from modern facilities, updated technology infrastructure, and a faculty that blends veteran educators with energetic newcomers.

East View's student body reflects Georgetown's new demographic reality: a diverse, growing population of families relocating from across Texas and the country, drawn by the district's reputation and the community's quality of life. The Patriots program has built competitive athletics quickly and continues to develop its fine arts and CTE programming to match Georgetown HS.

For buyers in Wolf Ranch, Cimarron Hills, and newer subdivisions on Georgetown's western edge, East View is typically the assigned high school, though boundary verification with GISD is always required before purchase.

## Middle Schools: Georgetown MS & Forbes MS

### Georgetown Middle School

Georgetown Middle School serves grades 6–8 for students in the established, central portions of Georgetown. The campus has strong academic programs including pre-AP coursework, fine arts ensembles, and competitive athletics that prepare students for the transition to Georgetown High School.

### Forbes Middle School

Forbes Middle School is the district's newer middle school campus, built to serve the enrollment surge driven by Wolf Ranch and other master-planned communities on Georgetown's expanding western flank. Forbes feeds primarily into East View High School and has quickly established itself as a strong academic and extracurricular program. Its campus design incorporates collaborative learning spaces and updated STEM labs that reflect modern educational priorities.

Middle School Quick Reference

- **Georgetown MS:** Grades 6–8 · Feeds Georgetown HS · Central Georgetown

- **Forbes MS:** Grades 6–8 · Feeds East View HS · West Georgetown / Wolf Ranch corridor

## Elementary Schools & Feeder Patterns

Georgetown ISD operates numerous elementary campuses spread across the district's expansive geography. Understanding your elementary feeder pattern, and how it connects to middle school and high school assignments, is critical for long-term planning.

### Wolf Ranch Feeder Pattern

Wolf Ranch is Georgetown's signature master-planned community, developed by Johnson Development and representing one of the most ambitious residential projects in Williamson County's history. As Wolf Ranch's population has grown, GISD has added elementary capacity specifically to serve this community. Wolf Ranch Elementary School serves the community's youngest students, feeding into Forbes Middle School and typically on to East View High School.

The Wolf Ranch feeder pattern is notable for its relatively new facilities and its demographically diverse student population reflecting the community's deliberate design as an all-ages, all-stages neighborhood. Buyers in Wolf Ranch should confirm their exact campus assignment with GISD at the time of purchase, as boundary adjustments have occurred as the community has expanded.

### Georgetown Village Feeder Pattern

Georgetown Village is one of Georgetown's established master-planned communities closer to the historic core of the city. Students in Georgetown Village typically feed through the established Georgetown Middle School pathway and on to Georgetown High School. This feeder pattern offers continuity with the district's longest-standing programs and strongest alumni network.

## Specialized Programs: Dual Language, Ag/CTE, Fine Arts

### Dual Language Program

Georgetown ISD operates a dual language immersion program at designated elementary campuses, providing students structured instruction in both English and Spanish. The program follows a research-backed model in which students develop bilingual literacy simultaneously, with long-term outcomes that include academic and cognitive advantages. Demand for dual language placement is high, and GISD uses a lottery system for enrollment at non-boundary campuses. Families interested in dual language should contact GISD's curriculum department as early as possible in the enrollment cycle.

### Agricultural & CTE Programs

Georgetown's agricultural heritage is not merely historical, it is actively celebrated and supported through GISD's robust Ag and Career & Technical Education programs. Georgetown High School's FFA chapter competes at the highest levels of Texas competition, with students raising livestock, competing in agricultural mechanics, judging contests, and leadership events. The district's Ag program operates with dedicated facilities, a full-time Ag faculty, and community partnerships that give students real-world agricultural experience.

Beyond agriculture, GISD's CTE pathways include healthcare, business, technology, and construction trades, giving students stackable credentials and industry certifications alongside their high school diplomas. For families who value career readiness alongside college preparation, GISD's CTE programs are a meaningful differentiator.

### Fine Arts

Fine arts are central to GISD's identity. Both high school campuses maintain competitive band, choir, orchestra, and theater programs. Georgetown High School's Performing Arts Center provides a professional-grade venue that elevates the student experience and connects school performances to the broader Georgetown community. Middle and elementary campuses feed talent into these programs through progressive fine arts instruction beginning in the earliest grades.

## GISD Boundary Map: Georgetown vs. Round Rock ISD

One of the most common points of confusion for buyers in northern Williamson County is the boundary between Georgetown ISD and Round Rock ISD. Several major corridors and communities sit near the boundary line, and a single street can separate a Georgetown ISD address from a Round Rock ISD address. This distinction matters enormously, both districts are well-regarded, but their feeder patterns, campus cultures, and home value premiums differ in ways that affect real estate decisions.

The Williamson County Appraisal District ([wcad.org](https://wcad.org)) provides address-level school district confirmation that buyers can use to verify their property's district assignment before closing. I strongly recommend using both WCAD's tool and a direct confirmation from GISD's enrollment office for any Georgetown or northern Williamson County purchase.

Key areas where the GISD/RRISD boundary creates buyer confusion include:

- Portions of the Georgetown 78628 zip code that cross into Round Rock ISD territory

- New subdivisions along Ronald Reagan Boulevard where master plans straddle district lines

- Sun City Georgetown, the Del Webb active-adult community, which lies within GISD boundaries (relevant for resale value even though residents are primarily empty-nesters)

- Portions of the Highway 29 corridor east of Georgetown's city limits

## Charter School Options in Williamson County

Families in Georgetown have access to several charter school options operating in Williamson County, providing alternatives to traditional GISD campuses. Charter schools in Texas are publicly funded and tuition-free, though enrollment is typically by lottery and is not guaranteed by address.

Well-known charter networks operating in or near Georgetown include KIPP Texas, Harmony Public Schools, and several locally-based options. Each charter has its own curriculum focus, admission timeline, and lottery process. Charter enrollment does not change the school district designation on your property, a home zoned to GISD remains a GISD home regardless of where your child actually attends school.

For families considering charter schools, the practical advice is: purchase the home in the district that best supports your long-term resale goals, then pursue charter enrollment independently. GISD and charter enrollment are separate processes.

## How Wolf Ranch Reshaped GISD Capacity

Wolf Ranch represents the most significant single driver of Georgetown ISD enrollment growth in the past decade. Johnson Development's master plan encompassed thousands of residential lots and, by design, drew thousands of school-aged children into GISD's system. The district responded with new elementary capacity, boundary realignments, and Forbes Middle School's development to absorb and serve this growing population.

The interplay between Wolf Ranch's development timeline and GISD's campus construction schedule is a lesson in infrastructure planning. At various points, enrollment pressure outpaced facility availability, leading to portable classrooms and temporary boundary adjustments. As the community has matured and GISD's capital improvement projects have caught up, the system has stabilized, but buyers should always verify current conditions at the time of purchase.

Wolf Ranch's amenity package, resort pool, parks, trails, and the Wolf Ranch Town Center retail district, combined with GISD's improving school infrastructure has made it one of the most sought-after communities for young families in Williamson County.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Georgetown ISD 2026

**Q: How many students are enrolled in Georgetown ISD? :**

Georgetown ISD serves over **14,000 students** across its campuses in Williamson County, Texas. The district has experienced rapid enrollment growth driven by major master-planned communities like Wolf Ranch, making it one of the fastest-growing districts in the state. This growth trajectory is expected to continue as northern Williamson County continues to absorb Austin metro area families.

**Q: What are the main high schools in Georgetown ISD? :**

Georgetown ISD operates **two high schools**: Georgetown High School (the Eagles), the district's flagship campus with a storied football tradition, FFA program, and Performing Arts Center; and East View High School (the Patriots), a newer campus serving the growing west Georgetown corridor. Both offer advanced coursework, dual enrollment opportunities with Austin Community College, and robust extracurricular programs.

**Q: What feeder patterns serve Wolf Ranch in Georgetown ISD? :**

Students living in the Wolf Ranch master-planned community typically feed into **Wolf Ranch Elementary**, then into **Forbes Middle School**, and ultimately to **East View High School** depending on their specific address. Boundaries shift as the district adds capacity, so always verify current zoning with GISD directly at [georgetownisd.org](https://georgetownisd.org) before purchasing. Do not rely on the community's marketing materials or neighborhood word-of-mouth.

**Q: Does Georgetown ISD offer a dual language program? :**

Yes. Georgetown ISD operates a **dual language immersion program** at select elementary campuses that provides structured instruction in both English and Spanish. The program prepares bilingual learners for an increasingly global workforce and has documented academic benefits including stronger literacy outcomes across both languages. Contact GISD's curriculum department for current campus assignments and lottery enrollment timelines.

**Q: How does living in Georgetown ISD affect home values compared to neighboring districts? :**

Georgetown ISD-zoned homes span a broad price spectrum, from the mid-$300s in established central Georgetown neighborhoods to well over $2 million in luxury communities like Cimarron Hills. The district's growing reputation and the quality-of-life appeal of Georgetown itself have supported consistent appreciation. When comparing districts, confirm boundary addresses with the [Williamson County Appraisal District](https://wcad.org) before purchasing, and work with a knowledgeable local agent who can show you comparable sales by district zone.
