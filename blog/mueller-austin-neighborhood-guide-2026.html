History & Origin

From Airport Tarmac to Urban Village: The Mueller Story

Robert Mueller Municipal Airport served Austin from 1930 until 1999, when the city transitioned operations to Austin-Bergstrom International Airport in Southeast Austin. What remained was 711 acres of flat, centrally located land, a once-in-a-generation redevelopment opportunity that city planners, community members, and Catellus Development Corporation transformed into the Mueller neighborhood we know today.

The master plan, shaped in part by extensive community input gathered through the Mueller Neighborhood Association and the city's planning process, set ambitious goals: a walkable, mixed-income, mixed-use urban village that would serve as a national model for sustainable infill development. Ground broke in 2006, and the community has been growing ever since.

Today Mueller is home to more than 6,000 residents, with additional phases still under construction. The neighborhood is managed through the Mueller Community Association, which oversees parks, community programming, and the vibrant Aldrich Street corridor.

City of Austin Connection: Mueller's development was governed by a development agreement with the City of Austin, which required the 25–32% affordable housing component and extensive parkland dedication, provisions that remain legally binding and distinguish Mueller from typical market-rate developments.

Community & Amenities

What Makes Mueller Austin Special

Aldrich Street District

The Aldrich Street commercial corridor is Mueller's downtown-in-miniature: a pedestrian-friendly strip lined with locally owned restaurants, coffee shops, boutiques, a cineplex (Alamo Drafthouse), and professional services. Designed with wide sidewalks, street trees, and ground-floor retail that activates every block, Aldrich Street is what makes Mueller feel like a real neighborhood rather than a subdivision.

Town Park and Ella Wooten Pool

Mueller's central Town Park anchors the neighborhood socially and spatially. The park offers open lawns, playgrounds, a splash pad, and sports courts. Adjacent to the park, Ella Wooten Pool, operated by the City of Austin Parks and Recreation Department, provides a beloved public swimming option during Austin's long summers.

Mueller Farmers Market

Every Sunday morning, the Mueller Farmers Market draws residents and visitors from across Austin. The market consistently features 80+ vendors selling produce, baked goods, prepared foods, and crafts, making it one of the largest and most beloved farmers markets in the city. For Mueller residents, it doubles as a weekly community gathering.

Thinkery Children's Museum

Families with young children prize Mueller's proximity to the Thinkery, Austin's premier children's science and creativity museum. The facility offers interactive exhibits on STEM, arts, and outdoor learning, a significant quality-of-life asset for households with school-age children.

Dell Children's Medical Center

Dell Children's Medical Center of Central Texas, part of the Ascension Seton network, is located within Mueller and serves as one of the neighborhood's largest employers. Its presence adds economic stability and walkable employment that benefits the entire community.

Sustainability & Design

Mueller's Sustainability Features and Tech-Forward Culture

Mueller was designed from the ground up with sustainability as a core principle, not an afterthought. The neighborhood's infrastructure choices reflect a genuine commitment to environmental performance that attracts buyers who care about these issues.

  • Solar Ready Construction: Many Mueller homes were built with solar-ready roof structures and electrical panels, significantly reducing the cost of retrofit installations.
  • Rainwater Collection: The community's green infrastructure includes bioswales, rain gardens, and permeable paving that manage stormwater on-site and reduce runoff into Austin's creek systems.
  • EV Charging: Community parking areas and many individual homes include Level 2 EV charging infrastructure, reflecting Mueller's tech-forward resident base.
  • Accessible Design: The street grid and pedestrian infrastructure were built to ADA standards throughout, with curb cuts, tactile paving, and accessible park facilities.
  • Tree Canopy: Mueller's master plan required extensive street tree planting; the maturing canopy now provides meaningful shade and urban heat island mitigation.
  • Energy Star Homes: Many Mueller single-family homes carry Energy Star certification, with superior insulation, windows, and HVAC performance.

Mueller's resident community skews toward Austin's technology sector workforce, given proximity to employers in the 183 Tech Corridor, UT Austin, and Dell Medical School. This demographic brings high expectations for digital infrastructure, fiber internet, smart home compatibility, and co-working spaces are all well-established in the neighborhood.

Mueller's central location is one of its most compelling selling points. Positioned approximately 3 miles northeast of downtown Austin, residents enjoy faster commute times than most Austin neighborhoods while still living in a quiet, walkable community.

Community Diversity

Mueller's 32% Affordable Housing Mandate

Unlike most Austin neighborhoods, where affordability is a political aspiration frequently unmet, Mueller has a legally binding affordable housing commitment baked into its development agreement with the City of Austin. Approximately 25–32% of Mueller's residential units are income-restricted, managed primarily by Foundation Communities and other nonprofit partners.

This means Mueller is a genuinely mixed-income community: teachers, nurses, artists, and service workers live alongside tech executives and physicians in the same walkable blocks. Many Mueller residents cite this as a feature, not a bug, it creates the kind of authentic urban community feel that wealthier homogeneous neighborhoods cannot replicate.

For Affordable Housing Applicants: Income-restricted units in Mueller are administered through Foundation Communities and the City of Austin's Housing Department. Eligibility is based on Area Median Income (AMI) thresholds. Learn more at City of Austin Housing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Mueller Austin: Your Questions Answered

What are home prices like in Mueller Austin in 2026? +

Mueller Austin home prices range from approximately $450,000 for smaller condos and townhomes to $1.1 million or more for larger single-family bungalows and premium units. The neighborhood's master-planned design and walkability command a premium over comparable Austin ZIP codes. Buyers should work with a local agent familiar with Mueller's HOA structure and specific pocket pricing by block.

Which schools serve Mueller Austin? +

Mueller is served by Austin ISD: Blanton Elementary School (PK–5), Lamar Middle School (6–8), and McCallum High School (9–12). All three are well-regarded campuses within AISD's district boundaries for the 78723 ZIP code. Always verify current boundaries at austinisd.org before making a purchase decision.

How walkable is Mueller Austin? +

Mueller consistently earns a Walk Score above 70, classified as "Very Walkable", making it one of Austin's most pedestrian-friendly neighborhoods. Aldrich Street's retail corridor, the weekly farmers market, Thinkery children's museum, and Town Park are all reachable on foot from most homes. The neighborhood also earns high Bike Scores given its internal trail network and connections to Austin's wider bikeway system.

What is the affordable housing component at Mueller? +

Mueller was master-planned with a 25–32% affordable housing mandate, one of the largest such commitments in Austin's history. The Foundation Communities and Catellus Development Corporation partnership has delivered hundreds of income-restricted units alongside market-rate homes. Eligibility is based on Area Median Income thresholds set by the City of Austin's Housing Department.

How far is Mueller from downtown Austin? +

Mueller is approximately 3 miles northeast of downtown Austin, making it a quick 10–15 minute drive or a bikeable commute via the Lance Armstrong Bikeway and other protected paths. The neighborhood's location on Airport Boulevard also provides easy access to MoPac and US-183 for commuters heading to other parts of the metro area.

Buyer's Perspective

Is Mueller Right for You? A Practical Buyer's Checklist

Mueller is an exceptional neighborhood, but it is not the right fit for every buyer. Use this checklist to assess fit before committing significant time to touring:

  • You value walkability: Mueller's design rewards residents who want to walk or bike for daily errands. Car-centric buyers may find the trade-off not worth the price premium.
  • You want community: Mueller has an unusually active neighborhood association, regular events, and a genuine small-town-within-the-city feel. Introverts who prefer anonymity may find it intense.
  • Schools matter to your decision: McCallum and Blanton are well-regarded, but confirm current ratings and boundary lines with AISD directly.
  • You can absorb HOA fees: Mueller's community association fees fund the parks, pool, and event programming that make the neighborhood special. Budget accordingly.
  • You're a long-term holder: Mueller's infrastructure investment and central location make it a strong long-term hold; short-term flippers should be aware of the premium entry price.
  • Mixed-income community aligns with your values: The 32% affordable housing mandate is a legal requirement; buyers who are not comfortable in a diverse economic community should look elsewhere.
