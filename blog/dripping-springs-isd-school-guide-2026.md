## Dripping Springs ISD: The Hill Country's Premier District

Dripping Springs Independent School District occupies a unique position in the Austin metro's educational landscape: it is small enough to maintain tight-knit campus cultures and small class sizes, yet academically robust enough to compete with the region's largest and most prestigious districts. Serving a community that blends rural Hill Country character with the sophistication of Austin's western suburbs, DSISD has built a reputation as one of the most desirable public school systems in Central Texas.

The district's consistent **TEA "A" accountability rating** is not an accident, it reflects deliberate investment in teacher quality, curriculum rigor, parental engagement, and student support. For buyers comparing DSISD to neighboring Hays CISD or Austin ISD, the academic differentiation is real and measurable, and it is priced into the real estate market accordingly.

Dripping Springs ISD serves the city of Dripping Springs and surrounding Hays County communities, including Driftwood, Wimberley-adjacent rural areas, and portions of the 78737 zip code corridor that extends toward southwest Austin. Understanding which addresses fall within DSISD boundaries, and which do not, is one of the most critical due diligence steps any buyer in this market can take.

## Dripping Springs High School, Home of the Tigers

Dripping Springs High School is the district's sole high school campus and the pride of the community. The Tigers compete at the 5A level in the UIL, fielding competitive programs across football, basketball, baseball, softball, soccer, track, tennis, and cross country. The athletic culture at DSHS is strong without overshadowing the district's commitment to academic excellence, a balance that distinguishes DSISD from many suburban Texas districts.

Academically, Dripping Springs HS offers a full suite of Advanced Placement courses and has developed strong dual enrollment partnerships with Austin Community College, allowing students to earn college credit at no cost while still in high school. This dual enrollment pathway is a practical financial benefit for families, students may arrive at college with a semester or more of transferable credits already completed.

### STEM Programs at Dripping Springs HS

DSHS has invested significantly in STEM education, reflecting both community demand and the career pathways relevant to the Austin tech ecosystem that many graduates enter. Programs include:

- **Engineering and Robotics:** Competitive robotics teams, engineering coursework, and project-based STEM electives

- **Computer Science:** AP Computer Science Principles and AP Computer Science A, aligning with Austin's technology workforce needs

- **Dual Enrollment STEM:** Partnership with Austin Community College for STEM dual credit in math, science, and technology fields

- **Science Research:** Independent and mentored research opportunities for advanced students

- **Environmental Science:** Unique Hill Country context gives DSHS students real-world ecosystem labs in their own backyard

The district's commitment to STEM is backed by a parent community that includes engineers, tech executives, and entrepreneurs from Austin's technology sector who have chosen Dripping Springs for its quality of life and school quality.

### Fine Arts: Theater, Band, and Beyond

Dripping Springs High School has a strong fine arts program that competes at the regional and state level in UIL music and theater competitions. The school's marching band, concert ensembles, and theater department draw significant parental support and community attendance. For families who place fine arts education on equal footing with athletics and academics, DSHS delivers.

Theater productions at DSHS range from classic dramatic works to contemporary musicals, with production quality that reflects a well-funded and community-supported arts program. Students interested in technical theater, stage management, and production design find robust opportunities alongside traditional performance roles.

## Middle School: Sycamore Springs MS

Sycamore Springs Middle School serves DSISD's 6th through 8th grade students, functioning as the academic and social bridge between the district's elementary campuses and Dripping Springs High School. The campus maintains the district's "A" TEA rating and is known for strong pre-AP coursework, competitive athletics, and fine arts programs that prepare students for the competitive high school environment.

Sycamore Springs benefits from DSISD's culture of strong parental involvement. The campus PTA and booster organizations are active fundraising bodies that supplement state and district funding with resources for technology, fine arts, and facility improvements. This community investment model is one of the defining characteristics of DSISD and contributes meaningfully to the district's outcomes.

Class sizes at Sycamore Springs remain manageable by Austin metro standards, another benefit of DSISD's smaller scale compared to the large suburban districts surrounding Austin. Students are not anonymous numbers in oversized classrooms; they are known by their teachers and supported as individuals.

## Elementary Campuses: Small Classes, Big Results

DSISD's elementary campuses are the foundation of the district's academic excellence. Each campus maintains DSISD's district-wide culture of high expectations, parental engagement, and individualized student attention. The district's small class sizes at the elementary level, a direct result of DSISD's smaller overall enrollment compared to large suburban districts, give students meaningful teacher-to-student ratios that support early literacy and numeracy development.

## Belterra and Caliterra: DSISD's Showcase Communities

### Belterra (78737)

Belterra is one of the premier master-planned communities in Hays County, located in the 78737 zip code where southwest Austin blends into Hill Country terrain. Developed by Newland Communities, Belterra offers a full suite of amenities including resort-style pools, a fitness center, parks, and trail systems, all set against the limestone hills and cedar canopy of the Texas Hill Country.

Belterra students are served by **Rooster Springs Elementary**, then **Sycamore Springs Middle School**, and on to **Dripping Springs High School**. This clear, established feeder pattern is one of Belterra's most significant selling points for families, buyers know exactly which campuses their children will attend from purchase day through graduation.

Home prices in Belterra reflect the DSISD premium, with current listings and recent sales spanning approximately $600,000 to $900,000 for production homes, with custom lots and larger properties trading higher. The combination of Belterra's amenities, the Hill Country setting, and the DSISD school zone creates persistent demand that has supported appreciation through multiple market cycles.

### Caliterra (78620)

Caliterra is a newer master-planned community in the 78620 zip code, developed along the Pedernales River and offering a nature-forward living experience that emphasizes outdoor recreation and community connection. The development's river access, trails, and Hill Country topography attract buyers who want suburban amenities with a genuinely natural setting.

Caliterra students are zoned within DSISD, with feeder pattern assignments to be confirmed directly with the district as the community continues to develop and new phases absorb additional students. Buyers in Caliterra's earliest phases should verify current campus assignments, as DSISD may adjust boundaries as the development matures.

Home prices in Caliterra range broadly depending on the phase and product type, from the mid-$500s for production homes to well over $1 million for estate lots and custom builds along the river corridor.

## DSISD's Culture of Parental Involvement

One of the most frequently cited strengths of Dripping Springs ISD is its culture of extraordinary parental involvement. The district's parent organizations, PTAs, booster clubs, and campus foundations, are among the most active and well-funded in the Austin metro area, raising hundreds of thousands of dollars annually that supplement state funding for technology, facilities, fine arts programs, and teacher appreciation.

This parental engagement culture is self-reinforcing: families who value education move to DSISD, their active engagement improves school quality, improved school quality attracts more engaged families, and the cycle continues. It is a positive feedback loop that explains in part why DSISD consistently outperforms districts with similar or larger budgets.

For buyers considering DSISD, this culture is not merely a nice-to-have, it is a structural advantage built into the community that has real effects on educational outcomes and real estate values. Communities with strong parental involvement culture tend to produce better-resourced campuses and stronger neighborhood cohesion, both of which support property values over time.

## Dual Enrollment with Austin Community College

Dripping Springs High School maintains a robust dual enrollment partnership with **Austin Community College (ACC)**, allowing qualified juniors and seniors to earn transferable college credit while completing their high school diploma requirements. This program is one of the most significant practical benefits DSHS offers to college-bound students.

Dual enrollment courses are offered in a range of disciplines including:

- English composition and literature

- Mathematics (College Algebra, Pre-Calculus, Calculus)

- Natural sciences (Biology, Chemistry)

- History and government (satisfying Texas college core requirements)

- STEM pathways aligned with Austin's technology workforce needs

Students who fully utilize DSHS's dual enrollment opportunities can arrive at four-year universities with 12–30+ transferable credit hours, potentially eliminating a full semester or more of college tuition costs. For Dripping Springs families who understand this financial benefit, dual enrollment is among the most compelling arguments for DSISD's value.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Dripping Springs ISD 2026

**Q: What TEA rating does Dripping Springs ISD hold in 2026? :**

Dripping Springs ISD consistently earns an **"A" accountability rating** from the Texas Education Agency (TEA), making it one of the highest-rated suburban districts in the Austin metro area. This rating reflects strong academic achievement, student progress, and closing of achievement gaps across all campuses. Verify the most current rating at [tea.texas.gov](https://tea.texas.gov).

**Q: What zip codes are served by Dripping Springs ISD? :**

DSISD primarily serves zip codes **78620** (Dripping Springs proper, Caliterra), **78619** (Driftwood and rural Hays County), and portions of **78737** (Belterra and the southwest Austin/Hays County boundary area). Not all addresses in these zip codes are DSISD-zoned, always confirm your specific address with DSISD's enrollment office at [drippingspringsisd.net](https://drippingspringsisd.net) and with [Hays CAD](https://hayscad.net).

**Q: Do homes in Belterra and Caliterra feed into Dripping Springs ISD? :**

Yes. **Belterra** (78737) and **Caliterra** (78620) are both zoned within Dripping Springs ISD boundaries. Belterra students typically attend Rooster Springs Elementary, then Sycamore Springs Middle School, and on to Dripping Springs High School. Caliterra's feeder assignments should be confirmed directly with DSISD as the community continues to develop. Always verify the specific address, not just the community name, before purchasing.

**Q: What STEM programs does Dripping Springs High School offer? :**

DSHS offers robust STEM programming including engineering and robotics, AP Computer Science, dual enrollment STEM courses through Austin Community College, and science research opportunities. The school's STEM culture is reinforced by a parent community with deep roots in Austin's technology sector. Students can earn significant college credit in STEM fields through dual enrollment before graduating high school.

**Q: How do home prices in Dripping Springs ISD compare to AISD and Hays CISD? :**

DSISD-zoned homes typically command a **premium over comparable Hays CISD-zoned properties**, reflecting the district's consistently high TEA ratings. Median home values in DSISD-boundary areas range approximately **$550,000 to $900,000**, with luxury properties in rural Hays County trading well above $1 million. DSISD properties tend to hold value through market cycles better than average, given the district's stable "A" rating and strong community demand. Verify current values with Hays CAD at [hayscad.net](https://hayscad.net).
