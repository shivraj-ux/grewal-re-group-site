Best Private Schools Austin 2026: A Complete Guide for Luxury Home Buyers

The best private schools in Austin for 2026 include St. Andrew's Episcopal School, St. Stephen's Episcopal School, Regents School of Austin, Brentwood Christian School, St. Michael's Academy, and Austin Waldorf School. Tuition ranges from roughly $12,000 to $35,000+ per year depending on grade level and institution. For luxury buyers, the chosen private school often determines which Austin neighborhood makes the most sense, school proximity and commute time are among the top five factors cited by relocating families with children.

Austin's private school landscape has grown substantially over the past decade, keeping pace with the city's population boom and the influx of families from California, New York, and other high-cost markets who arrive expecting elite educational options. Whether you are relocating for work, retiring early to Austin's climate, or simply seeking a lifestyle upgrade, understanding the private school ecosystem is inseparable from making a smart luxury real estate decision.

This guide covers Austin's leading private schools for the 2026 academic year, their academic philosophies, tuition structures, admissions processes, and geographic locations. Most importantly, it explains how your choice of school will shape your neighborhood search.

Why School Choice Drives Luxury Home Buying Decisions in Austin

In most markets, luxury buyers weigh views, finishes, lot size, and walkability. In Austin, families with school-age children add one more non-negotiable: proximity to their chosen school. Traffic on MoPac, 360, and 183 can turn a 10-mile commute into a 45-minute ordeal. Parents who have already paid $25,000–$35,000 in annual tuition are not eager to spend an additional two hours per day in the car.

The practical result: private school enrollment decisions directly influence neighborhood selection. Families at St. Andrew's Episcopal School in Central Austin cluster in Tarrytown, Rosedale, Bryker Woods, and Bouldin Creek. Families at St. Stephen's Episcopal School in West Austin gravitate toward Westlake Hills, Rollingwood, and West Lake Hills estates. Regents School of Austin families often buy in Southwest Austin and Barton Creek.

Agent Insight: In my experience working with over 100 luxury transactions across Austin, school proximity ranks in the top three must-haves for roughly 70% of families with school-age children. I always ask about school plans before we narrow the search geography.

Beyond commute, private school choice signals a lifestyle orientation. Classical Christian school families often prioritize neighborhoods with strong community ties and proximity to like-minded families. Montessori and Waldorf families tend to value walkable, eclectic neighborhoods. Episcopal school families often look for established prestige neighborhoods with mature trees and historic character. None of this is absolute, but it patterns strongly enough to guide an experienced agent's approach to the neighborhood conversation.

St. Andrew's Episcopal School, Austin's Most Prestigious K-12

St. Andrew's Episcopal School is widely regarded as Austin's most academically rigorous and socially prominent private school. Located in Central Austin near Tarrytown, the school serves students from Pre-K through 12th grade on a sprawling, beautifully maintained campus.

Academic Profile

St. Andrew's offers an advanced college-preparatory curriculum with a robust AP program, strong arts and athletics, and an unusually diverse enrichment calendar. The school consistently places graduates at top-tier universities including Ivy League institutions, Stanford, Duke, and the University of Texas flagship honors programs. The student-teacher ratio is low, and the faculty includes a high proportion of advanced degree holders.

Tuition and Financial Aid

Annual tuition at St. Andrew's exceeds $30,000 per year for most grade levels, with Upper School tuition at the top of that range. The school offers need-based financial aid, and families are encouraged to apply regardless of income expectation. The aid application process runs concurrently with the admissions application. Learn more at standrews.org.

Location and Real Estate Implications

The St. Andrew's campus sits in the heart of Central Austin, making the surrounding neighborhoods, Tarrytown, Old Enfield, Bryker Woods, and Rosedale, the natural landing zones for enrolled families. These are among Austin's most expensive ZIP codes, with single-family luxury homes regularly trading between $1.5M and $5M+. Tarrytown in particular carries a significant "St. Andrew's premium" that has persisted through multiple market cycles.

Admissions Process

Admissions at St. Andrew's is selective and early. The school begins accepting applications in the fall (September–November) for the following academic year. Required components include:

  • Online application and non-refundable fee
  • Current school transcript and teacher recommendations
  • Student shadow day on campus
  • Parent interview
  • Standardized assessment (ISEE or SSAT for grades 5 and up)
  • Kindergarten applicants participate in a play-based developmental assessment

Decisions are typically released in February–March for fall enrollment. The school maintains a waitlist, and waitlist movement does occur, particularly for grades 1–3 and 9–10.

St. Stephen's Episcopal School, West Austin's Boarding Option

St. Stephen's Episcopal School occupies a distinctive niche in Austin's private school market: it is the city's only school offering a meaningful boarding program alongside its day school program. Set on a gorgeous 394-acre campus in West Austin, one of the most beautiful private school campuses in Texas, St. Stephen's serves grades 6 through 12.

Academic and Residential Programs

St. Stephen's combines rigorous college-preparatory academics with an experiential, outdoor-oriented ethos. The school's campus includes hiking trails, a working farm, art studios, and athletic facilities that would rival many small colleges. The boarding program primarily serves grades 9–12 and draws students from across Texas, the nation, and internationally.

For relocating families, the boarding option provides a strategic bridge: parents who have accepted an Austin job offer but haven't yet completed their home purchase can enroll a high-schooler as a boarder while the family completes their move timeline. This is an underutilized option that I often discuss with executive-level clients relocating from out of state. Visit ststephens.edu for enrollment details.

Tuition and Financial Aid

Day school tuition ranges from approximately $28,000 to $35,000 annually depending on grade level. Boarding tuition is higher, covering room and meals. The school offers merit and need-based aid, and the financial aid team is known to be accessible and realistic about award structures.

Location and Neighborhood Impact

The St. Stephen's campus is accessible from West Austin via Loop 360 and Bee Caves Road. Families enrolled at St. Stephen's frequently purchase in Westlake Hills, Rollingwood, Rob Roy, Lost Creek, and Barton Creek. These neighborhoods sit in the $1.5M–$8M+ tier and are among Austin's most consistently appreciating luxury submarkets.

Regents School of Austin, Classical Christian Education

Regents School of Austin offers one of Austin's most intellectually distinctive educational models: classical Christian education rooted in the trivium (Grammar, Logic, and Rhetoric stages), with a strong emphasis on Western civilization, Socratic discussion, and Latin study. The school has grown significantly over the past decade as families have sought alternatives to both conventional private schools and public education.

Academic Philosophy

Regents does not follow a conventional AP-driven curriculum. Instead, students engage deeply with primary texts, great books, classical literature, and foundational scientific and philosophical works. The pedagogical aim is to produce students who can think, argue, and write at a high level rather than students who have mastered standardized test preparation. College placement is strong, with graduates attending highly selective institutions as well as strong regional universities. Learn more at regentsschool.com.

Tuition and Access

Tuition at Regents ranges from approximately $15,000 to $25,000 per year, making it one of the more accessible options among Austin's prominent private schools without sacrificing academic seriousness. The school offers financial aid for qualifying families.

Location and Neighborhood Patterns

The Regents campus sits in Southwest Austin, drawing heavily from neighborhoods like Circle C Ranch, Barton Creek, Shady Hollow, and Manchaca Road corridor communities. These neighborhoods offer excellent value in the $600K–$2M range for families prioritizing acreage, newer construction, and community character over Central Austin prestige addresses.

Brentwood Christian School, North Austin K-12

Brentwood Christian School is a long-established North Austin private school serving Pre-K through 12th grade. With a solid reputation for academics, strong athletic programs, and a welcoming community culture, Brentwood Christian is a popular choice for families in North Austin and the Rundberg to Parmer Lane corridor.

Academic Offerings

Brentwood offers a college-preparatory curriculum with AP courses, dual-enrollment options, and a robust fine arts and athletics program. Class sizes are intentionally small, and the school emphasizes character formation alongside academic achievement. The student body is diverse and has grown substantially as North Austin has densified with tech workers from nearby campuses.

Tuition Structure

Annual tuition at Brentwood Christian ranges from approximately $12,000 to $18,000 depending on grade level, placing it among the most financially accessible full-spectrum private schools in Austin. Financial assistance is available through the school's aid program.

Neighborhood Implications for Buyers

Brentwood Christian families frequently purchase in Gracywoods, Milwood, Northwest Hills, Allandale North, Anderson Mill, and Jollyville, all established North Austin neighborhoods that offer spacious lots, mature trees, and convenient access to Domain-area tech employment. The price range is typically $600K–$1.5M, with some newer construction available in the lower tier.

St. Michael's Academy, South Austin Catholic Education

St. Michael's Academy is Austin's premier Catholic secondary school, serving grades 6 through 12 in South Austin. As the successor institution to a long tradition of Catholic education in the Austin Diocese, St. Michael's combines faith formation with serious academics and competitive athletics.

Academic and Faith Profile

The school offers a rigorous curriculum with AP and honors coursework, strong STEM programming, and an active campus ministry program. Graduate placement includes Texas state universities, Texas A&M, and selective private institutions. The Catholic identity is central but the school welcomes students of all faith backgrounds who are comfortable in the Catholic educational environment.

Tuition and Financial Aid

Annual tuition ranges from approximately $13,000 to $20,000, with parish-affiliated families sometimes qualifying for a modest discount. The Diocese of Austin offers scholarship programs for qualifying Catholic families, and the school provides need-based assistance.

South Austin Real Estate Connection

St. Michael's South Austin location draws families from Barton Hills, Bouldin Creek, Slaughter Lane corridor, Manchaca, and Sunset Valley. South Austin has experienced some of the strongest appreciation in the metro over the past five years, with luxury single-family homes in Barton Hills trading at $1.5M–$3M and newer construction along the Slaughter Lane corridor offering more accessible entry points.

Austin Waldorf School, Alternative Education in North Austin

Austin Waldorf School offers an internationally recognized Waldorf educational philosophy emphasizing whole-child development, arts integration, and a spiraling curriculum that mirrors developmental stages from early childhood through high school. The school serves Pre-K through 12th grade in North Austin.

What Makes Waldorf Distinctive

Waldorf education de-emphasizes standardized testing and screen-based learning in early grades, focusing instead on handwork, storytelling, movement, and artistic expression as vehicles for cognitive development. In the upper grades, this foundation supports rigorous academic work. Waldorf graduates are known for creativity, self-direction, and unusual depth of engagement with ideas.

For luxury buyers, Austin Waldorf School represents a specific lifestyle orientation, one that often pairs with interest in walkable neighborhoods, farm-to-table food culture, and outdoor recreation. Families who choose Waldorf tend to look at Hyde Park, Brentwood, North Loop, Rosedale, and the 183/Lamar corridor.

Tuition

Annual tuition at Austin Waldorf ranges from approximately $16,000 to $24,000, with sliding-scale and need-based aid available. The school is a member of the Association of Waldorf Schools of North America (AWSNA), ensuring alignment with international Waldorf standards.

More Notable Austin Private Schools

Hyde Park Baptist School

Hyde Park Baptist School is a K-12 Christian school in Central Austin serving the Hyde Park neighborhood and surrounding areas. With tuition in the $10,000–$16,000 range, it is among the more accessible private options in Central Austin. The school has a long community presence and strong arts and music programming. Its Central Austin location makes it convenient for families in Hyde Park, Mueller, Cherrywood, and North Loop.

The Rawson-Saunders School, Dyslexia-Focused Education

The Rawson-Saunders School is a specialized K-8 institution serving students with dyslexia and language-based learning differences. It is one of Austin's most important specialized educational resources, and one that profoundly shapes home buying decisions for families with children who have been diagnosed with dyslexia or related learning profiles. The school uses the Orton-Gillingham methodology and serves students from across the metro, drawing families to Central and Northwest Austin neighborhoods for proximity to the campus.

Acton Academy Austin, The Micro-School Concept

Acton Academy pioneered the "micro-school" concept and originated in Austin before franchising nationally. The Austin flagship serves a small student population using a self-directed learning model anchored in real-world challenges, Socratic discussion, and project-based exploration. Students are called "heroes" rather than students, and the pedagogical philosophy draws on the work of Daniel Pink and Sal Khan. Acton is not for every family, it requires a high degree of student self-motivation and parental buy-in to a non-traditional structure, but it attracts entrepreneurial, tech-forward families who often purchase in East Austin, South Congress, and the 78704 corridor.

AIM Academy

AIM Academy provides a project-based, college-preparatory education with an emphasis on entrepreneurship, STEM, and design thinking. It draws students from across Austin and is particularly popular with families in the tech community who have relocated from Bay Area schools with strong project-based learning cultures. The campus serves upper elementary through high school students.

Montessori Schools Across Austin

Austin has a robust Montessori ecosystem spanning multiple campuses and providers. Key institutions include Casa di Mir Montessori (North Austin), Brown Barge Montessori (partial private enrollment), and several AMI/AMS-credentialed private Montessori programs. Montessori education typically covers ages 3–12 (or extends through middle school in some programs), making it a common choice for families seeking an alternative to conventional elementary education before transitioning to a more traditional high school pathway.

Navigating the Austin Private School Admissions Process

Austin's private school admissions landscape is more competitive than most relocating families expect. Here is what you need to know before beginning the process:

Timeline

  • September–October: Most schools open applications for the following academic year. Campus tours and open houses run heavily through this period. Attend as many as possible before applying.
  • October–November: Submit applications, request teacher recommendation letters from current schools, and schedule required assessments.
  • November–January: Shadow days, parent interviews, and student assessments take place.
  • February–March: Admissions decisions are released. Schools notify in a coordinated window to give families time to compare offers before the deposit deadline.
  • March–April: Deposit deadlines. Families typically have 2–4 weeks to confirm enrollment.

Financial Aid

Apply for financial aid regardless of your income expectation. Austin's private schools allocate meaningful aid budgets, and the range of families who qualify is wider than most assume. Aid applications typically use the School and Student Services (SSS) platform managed by NAIS. Filing requirements include tax returns, W-2s, and sometimes business financials. The aid committee's decisions are confidential and separate from the admissions committee's decisions at most schools.

What Schools Look For

Austin private school admissions committees evaluate academic readiness, character, community fit, and, in most cases, the family's understanding of and alignment with the school's mission. Families who have done their homework, attended open houses, and can speak genuinely about why a specific school fits their child's learning profile are consistently more successful than those who treat it as a generic application process.

Key Tip: If you are relocating to Austin mid-year, contact schools directly to ask about mid-year enrollment availability. Many schools have limited mid-year spots, and waitlists move more often than families realize.

Private vs. Public Schools in Austin: A Luxury Buyer's Perspective

Austin's public school system is not monolithic. The metro area spans more than a dozen independent school districts (ISDs), and the variation in quality, resources, and academic culture between districts, and between campuses within districts, is enormous. Families who prioritize public education often conduct an equally sophisticated school research process, targeting specific ISDs and even specific feeder patterns.

The key public school districts that shape luxury home buying decisions in Austin include:

  • Eanes ISD (Westlake Hills, Rollingwood, West Lake Hills), consistently ranked among Texas's highest-performing districts
  • Austin ISD, massive and variable; some campuses are excellent, others face resource challenges; Casis Elementary, Bryker Woods, and Barton Hills are standouts
  • Lake Travis ISD, strong academics in the Bee Cave/Lakeway corridor; growing rapidly
  • Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD, both highly rated in the north suburbs

For families committed to private school from the outset, the public district is less relevant, but it does affect resale value. Homes in Eanes ISD command a meaningful premium even if the current owner has no school-age children, because future buyers will include families who do. This is worth understanding when evaluating comparable properties.

For further research on Texas school performance data, consult tea.texas.gov, the Texas Education Agency's public accountability portal includes campus-level ratings, demographic data, and performance trends.

Private School Tuition as a Cost of Living Factor in Austin

Families relocating from high-cost-of-living markets sometimes underestimate the tuition factor. In markets like New York City, San Francisco, and Los Angeles, private school tuition of $30,000–$50,000 per year is normalized. In Austin, the range is somewhat lower, but it remains a material line item in the household budget, particularly when combined with property taxes, which are higher in Texas than in many origin markets.

A useful framework for luxury buyers: in Texas, you pay no state income tax, but property taxes typically run 1.8%–2.5% of appraised value annually. On a $2.5M home, that is $45,000–$62,500 per year in property taxes. Add $30,000–$65,000 in private school tuition for two children, and the annual cost picture looks very different from the headline "no income tax" narrative. A qualified financial planner and a knowledgeable real estate agent should both be part of your relocation team.

Total Cost Framework: Property tax + HOA + private school tuition = Austin's true cost of luxury living. Many relocating families are surprised that their all-in housing and education costs are comparable to their origin market, even without state income tax.

Frequently Asked Questions: Austin Private Schools

What are the best private schools in Austin for 2026?

The top private schools in Austin for 2026 include St. Andrew's Episcopal School (K-12, $30K+/year, Central Austin), St. Stephen's Episcopal School (grades 6–12 with boarding option, West Austin), Regents School of Austin (classical Christian, PK-12, approximately $15,000–$25,000/year), Brentwood Christian School (PK-12, North Austin, approximately $12,000–$18,000/year), and St. Michael's Academy (Catholic, grades 6-12, South Austin). Each offers distinct academic philosophies, affiliations, and tuition structures suited to different family priorities.

How much does private school tuition cost in Austin?

Austin private school tuition ranges widely. Regents School of Austin runs approximately $15,000–$25,000 per year, while St. Andrew's Episcopal School exceeds $30,000 per year. Most schools offer financial aid and need-based scholarships, so families should apply early and request aid applications alongside admissions materials. Boarding school options at St. Stephen's carry additional room-and-board costs. Contact each school's admissions and financial aid offices for current year figures.

Does private school choice affect which neighborhood a family buys in Austin?

Absolutely. Luxury buyers with children in private schools consistently prioritize proximity to their chosen campus. Families enrolling at St. Andrew's in Central Austin tend to look at Tarrytown, Rosedale, or Bouldin Creek. St. Stephen's families gravitate toward West Austin and Westlake Hills. Regents families often land in Southwest Austin and the Barton Creek area. School commute time is among the top five factors cited by relocating families with children in my experience across 100+ Austin luxury transactions.

What is the admissions process like for Austin private schools?

Most Austin private schools begin accepting applications in September–October for the following academic year. The process typically involves a campus tour, student shadow day, parent interview, teacher recommendation letters, and standardized assessment (ISEE or SSAT for upper grades). Kindergarten admissions may include a play-based developmental assessment. Decisions are usually released February–March. Apply to multiple schools and begin the process in early fall to meet all deadlines comfortably.

Which Austin private schools offer boarding options?

St. Stephen's Episcopal School in West Austin is the most prominent Austin private school offering a boarding program, primarily serving students in grades 9–12. This makes it attractive to families relocating to Austin who want to establish residency before their full household move, or for students whose parents travel internationally for business. The boarding program draws students from across Texas, nationally, and internationally, creating a diverse residential campus community.
