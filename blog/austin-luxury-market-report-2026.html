Austin Luxury Real Estate Market Report 2026

By Shivraj Grewal, CLHMS Guild · CNE | Updated May 2026 | ~20 min read

Austin's luxury real estate market, broadly defined as properties priced $1 million and above, has matured into one of the most compelling high-end markets in the Sun Belt. In 2026, the $1M–$3M segment is characterized by increasing inventory, sophisticated buyers, and an emphasis on lifestyle, design, and smart-home technology. At the ultra-luxury level ($5M+), Austin now competes directly with markets like Nashville, Denver, and Scottsdale for relocating executives, entrepreneurs, and generational wealth. This report provides a comprehensive analysis of every price tier, the buyers behind them, and the communities where Austin luxury is most concentrated.

Defining Austin's Luxury Real Estate Tier

The concept of "luxury" in real estate is inherently market-relative. In Manhattan, the luxury threshold begins north of $5M. In Houston, it might start at $750,000. In Austin, the industry broadly defines luxury as properties priced $1 million and above, with meaningful distinctions among the $1M–$2M entry luxury tier, the $2M–$3M mid-luxury segment, the $3M–$5M premium tier, and the $5M+ ultra-luxury category.

As recently as 2018, the million-dollar home threshold in Austin was rare enough to be newsworthy. Today, hundreds of Austin homes are listed above $1M at any given time, and the luxury segment represents a meaningful, and growing, share of overall market activity. This shift reflects Austin's transformation into a global-caliber city, the arrival of tech executive wealth, and the steady appreciation of well-located real estate over the past decade.

According to data from the Texas A&M Real Estate Center and local MLS records, the $1M+ market in Austin has grown from approximately 2% of all transactions in 2018 to over 8% in 2026, a fourfold increase in market share in under a decade.

Inventory by Luxury Price Bracket

One of the defining characteristics of the Austin luxury market in 2026 is the meaningful differentiation in inventory levels across price brackets. Unlike the sub-$500K market, where supply remains tight, the luxury segment has seen a gradual normalization of available inventory that gives buyers more time and options than they had during the frenzy of 2021–2022.

The data above reflects a clear pattern: as price increases, inventory in terms of months of supply increases, and average days on market lengthens dramatically. This is not unique to Austin, it reflects the fundamentally thin buyer pools at the top of any market, but it has important implications for luxury sellers who must commit to a long-horizon marketing strategy and precise pricing discipline.

TCAD and Luxury Valuation

Travis Central Appraisal District (traviscad.org) appraisals for luxury properties can diverge significantly from market value, sometimes by 20–35% in either direction. This creates both protest opportunities for homeowners and due-diligence requirements for buyers. At Compass RE Texas, every luxury transaction includes an independent valuation analysis to ensure our clients are protected.

Who Is Buying Austin Luxury Real Estate in 2026?

Understanding the luxury buyer pool is essential for both sellers setting expectations and buyers understanding their competition. Austin's $1M+ buyer landscape is diverse but can be segmented into several primary profiles:

Tech Executives and Senior Engineers

The largest and fastest-growing segment of the Austin luxury buyer pool. These are individuals relocating from the Bay Area, Seattle, and New York following company expansions, acquisitions, or personal life decisions. They often arrive with:

  • Substantial equity from California or Washington real estate (often $1M–$2M in net proceeds)
  • Strong income from stock compensation, RSUs, and annual bonuses
  • Sophisticated research habits, they have done extensive online diligence before their first showing
  • Strong preferences for smart home technology, energy efficiency, and contemporary architecture
  • School district as a primary driver, the Eanes ISD (Westlake) is a perennial top choice

Entrepreneurs and Business Founders

Austin's robust startup ecosystem has created a class of local wealth, founders, operators, and investors who built companies here and are now upgrading from the $600K–$900K range into genuine luxury. This buyer typically:

  • Has lived in Austin for 5–10 years and has existing neighborhood preferences
  • Values community connections, recognizable street addresses, and architectural distinction
  • May be experiencing a liquidity event (acquisition, IPO, private equity recap) and timing their purchase accordingly
  • Is less dependent on mortgage financing and more likely to offer cash or large down payments

Out-of-State Relocators

Beyond the tech-sector migration, Austin continues to attract high-net-worth individuals from across the country seeking Texas's favorable tax environment. With no state income tax, no estate tax, and relatively competitive property tax management for high-value properties, Texas offers meaningful financial advantages for wealth preservation. This segment includes:

  • Retired or semi-retired executives from finance and private equity (often NY or CT)
  • Entertainment industry figures and creative professionals from California
  • Political and policy figures drawn to Austin's growing influence in national discourse
  • Generational wealth relocators establishing Texas domicile for estate planning purposes

Local Move-Up Buyers

A meaningful but often overlooked segment of Austin luxury buyers are existing Austin homeowners who purchased in the $500K–$850K range in 2018–2021, have accumulated significant equity, and are ready to move up. This buyer understands Austin deeply, moves quickly when they find the right property, and often sells their existing home concurrently, creating timing complexity that expert agents navigate through bridge financing, leaseback arrangements, and contingency management.

Hottest Luxury Zip Codes in Austin 2026

Austin's luxury market is geographically concentrated. While high-end properties exist throughout the metro, the overwhelming majority of $1M+ activity is centered in four primary zip codes, each with a distinct character and appeal:

78703 · Tarrytown / Clarksville / Old West Austin

Tarrytown: Old Austin Prestige

Tarrytown is the gold standard of Austin real estate, a neighborhood of grand trees, gracious lots, and homes that range from lovingly restored 1940s Tudors to architecturally striking contemporary estates. Proximity to Lake Austin, downtown Austin, and top private schools makes 78703 perennially desirable. Lot sizes in Tarrytown typically run 8,000–15,000 sq ft, and teardown lots, rare but available, command $600K–$1.2M. Finished luxury homes here sell from $1.5M for smaller remodeled properties to $8M+ for estate-class new construction. Days on market in Tarrytown average 55–80 days, shorter than the metro luxury average, reflecting the neighborhood's durable demand.

78746 · Westlake Hills / West Lake Hills / Rollingwood

Westlake: Schools, Views, and Status

If there is a single zip code that defines luxury family real estate in Austin, it is 78746. The Eanes Independent School District, consistently rated among the top school districts in Texas, is the primary draw for families relocating with school-age children. Westlake Hills offers elevated terrain with Hill Country views, private lots, mature landscaping, and a suburban sanctuary feel just minutes from downtown. The luxury price range spans $1.2M for older ranch-style homes on modest lots to $15M+ for hilltop estates with panoramic views and resort-caliber amenities. Westlake is the most consistently liquid luxury submarket in Austin, well-priced homes here find buyers relatively quickly even in softer market conditions.

78738 · Bee Cave / Lakeway / Rough Hollow

Bee Cave & Lakeway: New Luxury at Scale

The 78738 zip code has emerged as the primary destination for new construction luxury in the Austin metro. Master-planned communities like Rough Hollow, Serene Hills, and Flintrock at Hurst Creek offer resort-style amenities, community yacht clubs, lazy rivers, tennis complexes, and miles of hiking trails, that appeal to buyers seeking lifestyle infrastructure alongside architectural quality. Prices here range from $900K for smaller attached villas to $4M+ for custom lakefront estates. The mix of established luxury builders (Drees, Grand Haven, Sitterle) and custom builders gives buyers meaningful options at every point in the price spectrum.

78732 · Lake Travis / Steiner Ranch / River Place

Lake Travis Area: Waterfront and Hill Country Estate Living

The Lake Travis corridor, encompassing communities from Steiner Ranch through Lakeway to Volente and Jonestown, is defined by its relationship with Lake Travis and the surrounding Hill Country terrain. While distinct from Lake Austin (the urban lake within the city limits), Lake Travis offers accessible waterfront living at a broader range of price points, from $1.5M lakefront homes to $6M+ custom estates on private coves. The area's appeal is amplified by the Marble Falls Road corridor's resort infrastructure, including private marinas, golf clubs, and spa facilities.

Days on Market: The Luxury Timeline

One of the most important calibration exercises for any luxury seller or buyer is understanding the realistic timeline for a transaction in their price bracket. Unlike the under-$750K market, where 30-day transactions are common, luxury real estate operates on fundamentally different timescales.

The chart above illustrates the core reality: a home in the $1M–$2M range averages 72 days on market before going under contract. At $2M–$3M, that stretches to 95 days. At $3M–$5M, it is 118 days. And at $5M+, the average is 165 days, more than five months from list to contract.

These timelines have significant implications:

  • For sellers: Pricing accurately from day one is essential. Luxury homes that go through multiple price reductions develop a market stigma that extends timelines further and gives buyers negotiating leverage.
  • For buyers: Patience and preparation matter. Having financing pre-arranged (or proof of funds for cash), knowing your must-haves, and being ready to move when the right property appears are critical.
  • For both parties: The quality of representation matters enormously. An experienced luxury agent with deep local market knowledge, professional staging connections, and an active off-market network can meaningfully shorten timelines for sellers and expand options for buyers.

New Luxury Construction Trends in Austin 2026

Austin's luxury new construction market in 2026 reflects the convergence of elevated land costs, sophisticated buyer demand, and the influence of global architectural trends. Several themes dominate new luxury construction across the metro:

Indoor-Outdoor Living as a Non-Negotiable

Austin's climate, warm nine months of the year, has made seamless indoor-outdoor living not a luxury feature but a baseline expectation in the $1M+ market. New construction luxury homes routinely feature:

  • Multi-panel sliding or stacking glass doors spanning 30–60 feet of exterior wall
  • Covered outdoor living rooms with full outdoor kitchens, built-in grills, refrigerators, and ice makers
  • Pool and spa combinations as near-universal inclusions, with heated spas, baja shelves, and tanning ledges standard
  • Shade structures including pergolas, motorized screens, and outdoor fans rated for Texas summers
  • Fire features, fire pits, fire bowls, and linear fireplace inserts, extending the outdoor season

Smart Home Technology Integration

The luxury buyer of 2026, particularly the tech executive segment, has elevated expectations for home automation. Leading systems seen across Austin luxury listings include:

  • Control4 and Crestron: Whole-home automation platforms managing lighting, AV, climate, security, and shading from a single interface
  • Lutron Homeworks: The gold standard for residential lighting control, with scene-based programming and occupancy sensing throughout
  • Savant: Preferred by design-forward buyers for its intuitive app interface and Apple ecosystem integration
  • Surveillance and access: Enterprise-grade camera systems, biometric entry, smart locks, and remote monitoring as baseline features
  • Whole-home audio: Distributed audio systems (Sonos, Origin Acoustics) allowing independent zone control throughout interior and exterior spaces

Energy Independence and Resilience

Following the February 2021 winter storm that exposed vulnerabilities in Texas's electrical grid, energy resilience has moved from nice-to-have to essential in Austin luxury construction. Standard features now include:

  • Whole-home standby generators (22kW–48kW) with automatic transfer switches
  • Tesla Powerwall or similar battery backup systems for solar integration
  • Rooftop solar arrays sized for meaningful offset (10kW–20kW systems common)
  • Spray foam insulation and high-performance windows for extreme temperature resilience
  • Tankless water heater systems with recirculation for instant hot water throughout

Wellness and Health-Focused Amenities

Post-pandemic luxury buyers have permanently elevated wellness as a design priority. Austin luxury homes increasingly include:

  • Dedicated home fitness rooms with rubber flooring, mirrored walls, and equipment alcoves
  • Infrared saunas, cold plunge pools, and steam showers in primary suite bathroom suites
  • Air purification and filtration systems throughout, MERV-13 or HEPA-level filtration
  • Circadian lighting systems that adjust color temperature throughout the day
  • Meditation or yoga rooms with cork flooring and acoustic treatment

Lake Austin Waterfront: Austin's Most Exclusive Real Estate

Within the broader Austin luxury market, Lake Austin waterfront properties occupy a category of their own. Lake Austin, not to be confused with Lake Travis, is a narrow, winding reservoir on the Colorado River that runs through west Austin and into the city of West Lake Hills. The lake is characterized by:

  • Extreme scarcity of private waterfront parcels, fewer than 300 true waterfront properties on the entire lake
  • Heavy regulation governing new construction, docks, and shoreline modifications
  • Proximity to downtown Austin (15–25 minutes depending on access point)
  • No public boat ramps, effectively a private, uncrowded lake for waterfront residents
  • Year-round usability due to Austin's mild climate

These characteristics create a sustained premium. Lake Austin waterfront properties command 40–80% above comparable non-waterfront properties in the same neighborhood. A 3,500 sq ft home in Tarrytown might sell for $2.5M–$3.5M. A comparable home with Lake Austin frontage and a private dock will command $4.5M–$7M, and architecturally significant estates on larger lots have transacted above $12M–$15M.

The turnover rate for Lake Austin waterfront is extremely low, perhaps 15–25 properties change hands annually. When a true waterfront property comes available, qualified buyers need to move decisively. This is a market segment where agent network and off-market relationships provide disproportionate advantage.

The Ultra-Luxury Segment: $5M and Above

Austin's $5M+ market, the ultra-luxury tier, has emerged as a credible destination for generational real estate wealth over the past five years. While Austin cannot yet compete with established ultra-luxury markets like Los Angeles, Manhattan, or Palm Beach on transaction volume, it offers a compelling value proposition for its price tier: extraordinary architectural design, expansive land, and lifestyle amenities that rival any market in the country, at a fraction of the price per square foot.

Ultra-Luxury Property Characteristics

Properties at $5M and above in Austin typically share several distinguishing features:

  • Lot size: 1–5 acres within the city or 5–50 acres on the outskirts, land remains Austin's scarcest luxury commodity
  • Architectural distinction: Custom design by named architects (Dick Clark + Associates, Clayton Korte, Michael Hsu for interior) rather than production builders
  • Primary suite suites: 1,500–2,000 sq ft primary suites with dual bathrooms, dual closets, private sitting rooms, and dedicated coffee stations are expected
  • Entertainment infrastructure: Home theaters, wine cellars (often climate-controlled walk-in caves rather than wine rooms), game rooms, and dedicated bar spaces
  • Guesthouse or casita: Separate guest accommodations, full apartments with kitchen, bedroom, and bath, for privacy and flexibility
  • Multi-car garages: 4–8 car garages with epoxy floors, EV charging, and lifts for car collectors
  • Water features: Beyond pools, full waterfall grottos, lazy rivers, swim-up bars, and resort-quality hardscape

Ultra-Luxury Buyer Psychology

The $5M+ buyer in Austin is unlike any other buyer in the market. They are rarely in a hurry. They are buying a home that must justify its premium on every dimension, architecture, setting, privacy, technology, and resale potential. They have likely seen dozens of properties (often virtually) before scheduling a showing. They may ask for weeks or months between first viewing and offer, and their offers, when they come, are often cash with minimal contingencies.

Marketing a $5M+ property in Austin requires a fundamentally different strategy than marketing a $1.5M home. The buyer pool is global in scope (reaching Austin via Compass's national and international networks), deeply private, and relationship-driven. The National Association of Realtors (nar.realtor) data consistently shows that luxury buyers rank agent expertise and reputation as the most important selection criteria, above price and above marketing budget.

Frequently Asked Questions: Austin Luxury Market 2026

What is the luxury real estate market like in Austin in 2026? +

The Austin luxury real estate market in 2026, defined as homes priced $1 million and above, features increasing inventory, longer average days on market (72–165 days depending on price bracket), and a sophisticated buyer pool of tech executives, entrepreneurs, and out-of-state relocators. The $1M–$2M segment is the most active, with balanced supply and demand. The $2M–$3M tier leans slightly toward buyers. The $3M–$5M and $5M+ segments are patient markets where expert positioning and network relationships determine outcomes. Cash transactions are particularly common above $2M (approximately 38% of transactions).

Which Austin zip codes have the most luxury real estate activity in 2026? +

The most active luxury zip codes in Austin in 2026 are 78703 (Tarrytown/Clarksville), 78746 (Westlake Hills), 78738 (Bee Cave/Lakeway), and 78732 (Lake Travis/Steiner Ranch). Each offers distinct characteristics, Tarrytown's walkable urban prestige, Westlake's Eanes ISD school district reputation, Bee Cave's new construction resort communities, and Lake Travis's waterfront lifestyle. Within the City of Austin, 78704 (South Congress) and 78702 (East Austin) have growing $1M+ activity driven by appreciation in those neighborhoods.

How long do luxury homes stay on the market in Austin? +

Austin luxury homes average the following days on market in 2026: $1M–$2M: 72 days · $2M–$3M: 95 days · $3M–$5M: 118 days · $5M+: 165 days. Well-priced, professionally staged, and actively marketed luxury homes can significantly outperform these averages. Properties that are overpriced, poorly photographed, or under-marketed may sit considerably longer, accumulating market time that ultimately hurts their negotiating position. Expert pricing and marketing strategy from day one is the single most impactful variable within a seller's control.

Who is buying luxury real estate in Austin in 2026? +

Austin's luxury buyer pool in 2026 is primarily composed of: tech executives and senior engineers relocating from California and Washington with substantial equity; entrepreneurs and founders monetizing liquidity events in Austin's startup ecosystem; out-of-state relocators from NY, NJ, CT, and IL attracted by Texas's no-income-tax advantage; and local move-up buyers leveraging equity from earlier Austin purchases. A meaningful share of luxury transactions, approximately 38% above $2M, involve cash purchases. School district quality (particularly Eanes ISD for Westlake) is a primary driver for family buyers.

What is the premium for Lake Austin waterfront property in 2026? +

Lake Austin waterfront properties command a 40–80% premium above comparable non-waterfront properties in the same zip code. True Lake Austin frontage, distinct from Lake Travis or other area lakes, is extremely scarce, with fewer than 300 privately owned waterfront parcels on the entire lake. Prices range from approximately $3M for modest waterfront cottages to $15M+ for architecturally significant estates with private docks, boat houses, and resort-level outdoor amenities. Turnover is extremely low (15–25 transactions annually), making agent network and off-market access critical for buyers pursuing this segment.
