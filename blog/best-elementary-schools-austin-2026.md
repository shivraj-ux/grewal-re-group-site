# Best Elementary Schools Austin 2026: The Neighborhood-by-Neighborhood Guide for Families

The best elementary schools in Austin for 2026 span multiple districts: Eanes ISD's Eanes Elementary, Bridge Point Elementary, and Cedar Creek Elementary consistently rank among Texas's highest-performing campuses. Within Austin ISD, Casis Elementary (Tarrytown), Bryker Woods Elementary (Rosedale), Barton Hills Elementary (Zilker), Brentwood Elementary, Gullett Elementary, and Mathews Elementary are standout campuses. In the northern suburbs, Round Rock ISD's Deep Wood Elementary and Laurel Mountain Elementary are highly sought after. Elementary school quality is among the most powerful drivers of neighborhood home values and buyer demand in the Austin luxury market.

When families relocating to Austin ask me "where should we live?", what they are really asking, more than half the time, is "where can my kids go to a great elementary school?" Elementary school quality is the invisible foundation of Austin's neighborhood premium structure. It drives buyer demand, sustains values through market cycles, and creates the community character that makes certain neighborhoods feel irreplaceable.

This guide covers the Austin area's best elementary schools for 2026, organized by district and neighborhood, with context on what makes each campus distinctive, how school boundaries affect property values, and how to research schools beyond the ratings.

## Eanes ISD, Austin's Premier Elementary School District

Eanes Independent School District

West Austin · Westlake Hills · Rollingwood · West Lake Hills · Bee Cave (partial)

Eanes ISD is the gold standard for public elementary education in the Austin metro. A small, geographically defined district covering the affluent West Austin communities of Westlake Hills, Rollingwood, and surrounding areas, Eanes consistently ranks among the top school districts in Texas and nationally. Learn more at [eanesisd.net](https://www.eanesisd.net).

### Eanes Elementary School

Eanes Elementary serves the core Westlake Hills and Rollingwood communities, the most centrally located of the Eanes ISD elementary campuses. The school benefits from an exceptionally active parent community, strong extracurricular support through the Eanes ISD Education Foundation, and consistently high TEA ratings. Class sizes are manageable, and the school's demographics reflect the high household income of the surrounding neighborhoods.

Homes zoned to Eanes Elementary sit primarily in the $1.2M–$5M range, with the median sale price well above the Austin metro average. The Eanes ISD premium is one of the best-documented school-value relationships in Austin real estate, buyers consistently pay above-market prices to access the district, and those premiums have proven durable across multiple market cycles.

### Bridge Point Elementary School

Bridge Point Elementary serves the eastern portion of the Eanes ISD attendance zone, including neighborhoods along Bee Caves Road and portions of West Lake Hills. It is the largest of the Eanes elementary campuses by enrollment and is known for strong STEM enrichment programming alongside the district's foundational academic excellence.

The surrounding neighborhoods, including estates along Lake Austin Boulevard, and newer developments in the Davenport Ranch area, attract buyers in the $1.5M–$6M+ range. Bridge Point's location near the 360/Bee Caves intersection makes it convenient for families with parents working in both Central Austin and the Domain tech corridor.

### Cedar Creek Elementary School

Cedar Creek Elementary serves the southernmost reaches of the Eanes ISD attendance zone, including portions of the Bee Cave Road corridor and some of the estate communities approaching the Hill Country. The campus is newer than some of its Eanes ISD siblings and has developed a strong reputation for fine arts programming alongside core academics.

Buyers targeting Cedar Creek Elementary often look at properties in the $900K–$3M range, somewhat lower entry points than Eanes or Bridge Point zones, making it one of the more accessible entry points into the Eanes ISD premium while still offering the full district benefit.

**The Eanes ISD Premium:** Homes in Eanes ISD routinely sell at a 15–25% premium over comparable homes in adjacent AISD zones, according to market analysis of Travis CAD sales data. This premium has persisted through the 2008, 2020, and 2022–2023 market corrections, suggesting it represents structural demand rather than speculative excess.

## Austin ISD, The Best Elementary Campuses

Austin ISD is the largest school district in the Austin metro, serving over 70,000 students across dozens of elementary campuses. The district's quality varies enormously by campus, some elementaries are among the best in Texas, while others face significant resource and performance challenges. Buying in AISD requires campus-level research, not just district-level assessment.

### Casis Elementary, Tarrytown

Casis Elementary is Austin ISD's most storied and prestigious elementary school, serving the Tarrytown neighborhood, one of Austin's wealthiest and most sought-after residential areas. The school has historically ranked at the top of AISD elementary performance metrics, supported by a deeply engaged parent community and one of the highest household income feeder demographics in the district.

Tarrytown homes zoned to Casis Elementary represent some of Austin's highest-value residential real estate, with single-family homes regularly trading between $1.5M and $5M+ for estates. The "Casis zone premium" within Tarrytown is real and measurable, properties on the Casis side of a zoning boundary routinely price higher than comparable homes just outside the attendance area.

Casis also has one of the most active parent-teacher organizations in AISD, which funds teacher enrichment, campus improvements, and family programming well above what district allocations support.

### Bryker Woods Elementary, Rosedale

Bryker Woods Elementary serves the Rosedale neighborhood, Central Austin's quiet, tree-lined enclave of architecturally interesting bungalows, Tudor cottages, and mid-century homes. The school consistently earns strong TEA ratings and is known for a warm campus culture, engaged parent community, and strong arts programming.

Rosedale and the broader Bryker Woods neighborhood attract buyers seeking Central Austin charm at a slight discount to Tarrytown, with homes typically in the $900K–$2.5M range. The elementary school is a core part of the neighborhood's appeal, contributing to Rosedale's resilience as a buyer's target through multiple market cycles.

### Barton Hills Elementary, Zilker / Barton Hills

Barton Hills Elementary serves one of Austin's most beloved residential neighborhoods: the Barton Hills/Zilker area immediately south of Barton Springs Road. The school is known for its community-oriented culture, strong outdoor education programming (benefiting from proximity to Barton Creek Greenbelt), and a parent community that has deeply invested in the campus.

Barton Hills the neighborhood has become one of Austin's most competitive buyer markets, with homes typically in the $1M–$3.5M range and intense demand from families who want Central Austin proximity combined with access to the Greenbelt lifestyle. The elementary school is consistently cited as a top reason families choose the neighborhood over nearby alternatives.

### Brentwood Elementary, North Central Austin

Brentwood Elementary serves the Brentwood neighborhood in North Central Austin, one of the most popular "urban village" neighborhoods for young families seeking the character of older Austin at more accessible price points. The school has seen consistent improvement in academic metrics in recent years, supported by a growing and engaged parent community as the neighborhood has gentrified.

Brentwood neighborhood homes are typically in the $700K–$1.8M range, making Brentwood Elementary one of the most accessible "highly regarded" AISD elementaries for buyers whose budgets cap below the Tarrytown/Westlake tier. Explore AISD campus data at [austinisd.org](https://www.austinisd.org).

### Gullett Elementary, Allandale

Gullett Elementary serves a portion of the Allandale neighborhood in Northwest AISD, drawing from a community of well-educated, civic-minded families. The school has a strong reputation for academic culture and parent involvement. Allandale homes in the Gullett zone are typically in the $700K–$1.5M range, offering a compelling value proposition for families who cannot stretch to Casis or Westlake pricing.

### Mathews Elementary, Northwest Hills

Mathews Elementary serves the Northwest Hills neighborhood, a mid-century modern enclave of ranch-style homes on large lots that has seen significant buyer interest over the past five years as younger families discover its combination of strong schools, mature landscaping, and relative affordability compared to other AISD prestige zones. Homes in the Mathews zone are typically $700K–$1.6M, with some larger lots exceeding $2M.

Round Rock · Pflugerville · Hutto · parts of Cedar Park and Brushy Creek

Round Rock ISD is one of Texas's most consistently high-performing larger school districts, regularly earning strong state accountability ratings across the majority of its campuses. The district has benefited from sustained investment in facilities, teacher compensation, and academic programming as the Round Rock/North Austin corridor has absorbed enormous population growth. ### Deep Wood Elementary

Deep Wood Elementary serves a portion of the Brushy Creek area, drawing from established neighborhoods with strong community character. The school has earned consistent TEA recognition and is served by a parent body that actively participates in campus programming. The surrounding neighborhoods offer homes in the $500K–$1.1M range, an attractive value proposition for families who want strong public elementary education without the Westlake price premium.

### Laurel Mountain Elementary

Laurel Mountain Elementary serves neighborhoods in the Cedar Park/Round Rock border area, a zone of strong residential growth that has attracted tech workers from nearby Apple, Dell, and other major employers. The school has earned strong TEA ratings and benefits from the high educational attainment of the surrounding parent community. Home prices in the Laurel Mountain zone typically range from $550K to $1.2M, with some newer construction available at the lower end.

**Northern Suburbs Value:** Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD offer strong public elementary education at a meaningful price discount relative to Eanes ISD and select AISD zones. For families with $600K–$1.1M budgets, these districts frequently represent the best quality-per-dollar outcome in the Austin metro.

## How to Find Your Zoned Elementary School in Austin

Zoning research is one of the most important steps in the Austin homebuying process for families with school-age children. Here is a systematic approach:

- **Start with the district's school finder tool.** Austin ISD has an address-based school finder at [austinisd.org](https://www.austinisd.org). Eanes ISD, Round Rock ISD, and Leander ISD all offer similar tools on their district websites.

- **Use Travis CAD to identify the ISD.** Visit [traviscad.org](https://www.traviscad.org) and look up any property by address. The property record will show the ISD to which the property is assessed. This is a fast way to confirm district assignment before researching specific campuses.

- **Verify with the district directly.** For any property where school zoning is a significant factor in your purchase decision, call the district's student services or enrollment office and give them the specific address. Zoning maps can have errors, and digital tools sometimes lag behind boundary changes.

- **Ask your real estate agent.** An experienced Austin buyer's agent should be able to confirm zoning for any address on your shortlist and flag cases where a block-level boundary change could affect your assumptions.

**Critical Rule:** Never assume a home's school zone based on its neighborhood name alone. Austin ISD attendance boundaries are frequently not aligned with neighborhood names or geographic features. A street can change school zones at mid-block. Always verify by address.

## Open Enrollment and Magnet Elementary Programs in Austin ISD

Austin ISD offers a range of enrollment options beyond your neighborhood attendance zone school. These programs can significantly expand your child's educational options regardless of where you live in the district.

### Dual Language Immersion Programs

Austin ISD operates Spanish/English dual language immersion elementary programs at multiple campuses across the district. These programs are among the most oversubscribed specialty programs in AISD, families should expect a lottery process with long waitlists for the most desirable campuses. The dual language programs do not require attendance zone residency; any AISD family can apply.

French/English dual language immersion is also available at select AISD campuses, serving primarily the Central Austin community and attracting international families, particularly those from France, Canada, and Francophone Africa, who have settled in Austin's tech and academic sectors.

### Fine Arts Magnet Programs

AISD's elementary fine arts magnet programs offer dedicated instruction in visual arts, music, drama, and dance for students who demonstrate artistic aptitude or passion. These programs operate as district-wide magnets with lottery-based admission, and they have developed strong reputations for producing students who continue in arts disciplines through middle and high school.

### STEM and Project-Based Learning Programs

Several AISD elementary campuses have adopted project-based learning models or STEM-specific programming as a whole-campus approach rather than a magnet overlay. Families interested in these approaches should research specific campuses rather than relying on categorical descriptions, as implementation quality varies significantly.

### How Open Enrollment Works

Within AISD, families living in the district may apply for open enrollment slots at campuses other than their attendance zone school, subject to available capacity. Open enrollment applications typically open in January for the following academic year. Magnet programs have their own application cycle and lottery. Note that transportation is generally not provided for open enrollment students attending a campus outside their attendance zone.

## What TEA Ratings Mean, And What They Don't Tell You

The Texas Education Agency (TEA) issues annual accountability ratings for every Texas public school campus. Understanding what these ratings measure, and what they omit, is essential for any family using them to guide a school decision or a real estate purchase.

### What TEA Ratings Measure

The TEA Accountability System evaluates campuses in three primary domains:

- **Student Achievement (35%):** Measures the percentage of students meeting or exceeding grade-level expectations on STAAR assessments across reading/language arts, mathematics, science, and social studies. Also includes graduation rates for high schools.

- **School Progress (35%):** Evaluates how much students grow academically year-over-year (growth domain) and how the campus compares to peers with similar demographic characteristics (relative performance domain).

- **Closing the Gaps (30%):** Measures performance equity across student groups, including economically disadvantaged students, special education students, English learners, and racial/ethnic subgroups. Campuses with performance gaps face potential rating reductions regardless of their overall scores.

An "A" rating (90–100 points) indicates high performance across all three domains. A "B" (80–89) indicates good performance with some room for improvement. A "C" (70–79) meets minimum standards. "D" and "F" ratings trigger state intervention protocols. Visit [tea.texas.gov](https://tea.texas.gov) for current campus-level ratings and detailed performance data.

### What TEA Ratings Don't Capture

TEA ratings are a useful but incomplete picture. Here is what they systematically miss:

- **Campus culture and climate.** Whether a school is a joyful, safe, and nurturing place to learn is not captured in standardized testing data.

- **Teacher tenure and stability.** Campuses with low teacher turnover consistently outperform their peer institutions, but this metric is not directly reflected in the accountability system.

- **Extracurricular programming quality.** Fine arts, athletics, STEM clubs, and enrichment opportunities vary enormously between campuses at the same rating level.

- **Parent community engagement.** The strength of the parent-teacher organization and the parent community's investment in the school is one of the strongest predictors of student outcomes, and entirely invisible in the TEA rating system.

- **Principal leadership quality.** Effective principals drive school culture, teacher retention, and academic excellence. Leadership quality is not rated, but it is transformative.

## How to Evaluate an Elementary School Beyond the Ratings

A campus visit is the single most valuable tool available to a family evaluating elementary schools. Here is what to look for:

### Before You Visit

- Check TEA ratings at [tea.texas.gov](https://tea.texas.gov) and GreatSchools ratings at [greatschools.org](https://www.greatschools.org) for baseline context

- Review the campus's STAAR performance trends over 3–5 years, a single year's rating can be misleading; trends tell the real story

- Ask the district or check the TEA data portal for teacher turnover rates at the campus level

- Search local parent forums and neighborhood Facebook groups for unfiltered perspectives from current families

### During Your Campus Visit

- **Walk the hallways during instruction time.** Are students engaged? Are classrooms calm and purposeful? Is student work displayed with evident care?

- **Speak with the principal.** A confident, clear-eyed principal who can articulate the school's priorities and challenges is a green flag. Evasiveness or over-polished PR language is a yellow flag.

- **Ask about teacher tenure.** How many teachers have been at the school for 5+ years? High tenure correlates strongly with instructional quality and campus stability.

- **Observe the lunchroom and playground.** Social environment matters enormously for elementary-age children. Look for positive peer interactions and attentive supervision.

- **Ask about the PTA/PTO.** A strong parent organization that raises meaningful funds for the campus and engages broadly with the community is a powerful positive indicator.

### Questions to Ask

- What is the school's philosophy around reading instruction? (Science-of-reading-aligned approaches have strong evidence behind them.)

- How does the school communicate with parents about individual student progress, beyond report cards?

- What happens when a student needs additional support? What when a student is academically advanced?

- What extracurricular programs are available, and how are they funded?

- What is the staff turnover rate over the past three years?

## How Elementary School Quality Drives Neighborhood Home Values in Austin

The relationship between elementary school quality and residential property values in Austin is strong, well-documented, and commercially significant. Understanding this relationship helps buyers make informed decisions and helps sellers understand their home's full value proposition.

### The Eanes ISD Premium

The clearest example in the Austin market is Eanes ISD. Homes in the Eanes district consistently sell at a premium to comparable homes just outside the boundary. Market analyses using Travis CAD sales data and neighborhood comparable studies have estimated this premium at 15–25% in normal market conditions. The premium has proven remarkably durable, it persisted through the 2008 financial crisis, the COVID market disruption, and the 2022–2023 rate-driven correction.

### The Casis Elementary Premium Within AISD

Within Austin ISD, the Casis Elementary attendance zone in Tarrytown commands a measurable premium over comparable properties in adjacent zones. Buyers who understand this can use the school boundary as a guide when evaluating whether a Tarrytown property is priced appropriately, and sellers in the Casis zone can legitimately market their school assignment as a value-added feature. Check property and ISD data at [traviscad.org](https://www.traviscad.org).

### The Broader Principle

School quality drives neighborhood demand because it affects not just the current buyer but also the pool of future buyers. Investors, downsizers, and even buyers without children pay attention to school ratings because they know future buyers will. This forward-looking dynamic is part of why school-zone premiums tend to be durable rather than cyclical.

For luxury buyers in particular, school quality is often a screening criterion rather than a trade-off. A family with a $2M budget has many Austin options, what narrows the search is combining school quality with neighborhood character, commute, and lifestyle preferences. Guiding that narrowing process is one of the most valuable things an experienced agent does.

## Top School Questions From Families Relocating to Austin

In my work with relocating families across 100+ Austin luxury transactions, certain school-related questions come up consistently. Here are the most common, with honest answers:

#### Q: Can I get my kids into a better school by renting first and then buying in the same district?

Yes, renting first is a legitimate strategy. Enrollment in most Austin area public schools requires proof of residency, a lease or utility bill in the district. You can rent in your target attendance zone, enroll your child, and then purchase in the same zone without interrupting enrollment. Just confirm with the school that their residency verification process accepts rental documentation.

#### Q: What happens if we buy near a school boundary and it changes?

Attendance boundaries in Austin ISD and other area districts do change, though not frequently. If you are purchasing specifically because of a school zone, ask your agent to verify that the boundary has been stable and to check district planning documents for any announced or upcoming rezoning. AISD's master facility planning documents are publicly available and can indicate whether a boundary review is anticipated.

#### Q: Are Texas public schools very different from California or New York schools we are used to?

There are real differences. Texas uses TEKS (Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills) standards rather than Common Core, and the STAAR assessment system differs from what students experience in California or New York. In the top-performing Austin area districts, the academic rigor and college preparation quality are comparable to high-performing districts in any state. In lower-performing zones, there can be meaningful quality gaps. This is why campus-level research matters more than state-level generalizations.

#### Q: Is there open enrollment between Austin-area districts?

Inter-district open enrollment policies vary. Some districts accept out-of-district students on a space-available basis; others do not. Contact the target district's enrollment office directly to ask about inter-district transfer options and any applicable fees or conditions.

#### Q: Should we consider private elementary school instead?

Private elementary is a strong option for families who want a specific academic philosophy, religious affiliation, or class size, or who are buying in a zone where the public elementary does not meet their expectations. See our companion guide on [Best Private Schools Austin 2026](best-private-schools-austin-2026) for a full treatment of Austin's private school landscape. For many families, the combination of a Westlake home in Eanes ISD and public elementary education represents the best value, outstanding public schools without private tuition, with a home that appreciates in one of Austin's strongest submarkets.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Austin Elementary Schools

**Q: What are the best elementary schools in Austin for 2026?:**

The best elementary schools in Austin for 2026 include Eanes ISD campuses (Eanes Elementary, Bridge Point Elementary, Cedar Creek Elementary) in the Westlake area and AISD standouts such as Casis Elementary (Tarrytown), Bryker Woods Elementary (Rosedale), Barton Hills Elementary (Zilker), Brentwood Elementary, Gullett Elementary, and Mathews Elementary. In the northern suburbs, Round Rock ISD's Deep Wood Elementary and Laurel Mountain Elementary, and several Leander ISD STEM-focused campuses, are highly sought after. TEA ratings and campus-level research at tea.texas.gov are the best starting points for verifying current performance.

**Q: How do I find my zoned elementary school in Austin?:**

Use the Austin ISD school finder at austinisd.org for AISD properties, or contact Eanes ISD, Round Rock ISD, or Leander ISD directly for properties in those districts. Travis CAD's property search at traviscad.org can help you identify which ISD a property is assigned to. Always verify zoning directly with the district for any address where school assignment is a significant factor in your purchase decision, digital tools can lag behind boundary changes. Your real estate agent should also be able to confirm zoning before you write a contract.

**Q: Does elementary school quality affect home values in Austin?:**

Yes, significantly. Homes zoned to highly rated elementary schools, particularly Casis Elementary in Tarrytown and all schools in Eanes ISD, command measurable premiums over comparable homes in adjacent zones. The Eanes ISD premium is estimated at 15–25% over comparable AISD properties in normal market conditions, and this premium has proven durable through multiple market cycles. Within AISD, the Casis Elementary zone carries its own premium within the Tarrytown neighborhood. School quality affects not just current buyer demand but also future resale potential, which is why school-zone research matters for investors and buyers without children as well as for families.

**Q: What do TEA school ratings mean for Austin parents?:**

The Texas Education Agency (TEA) issues annual accountability ratings for every Texas public school campus. Ratings range from A to F across three domains: Student Achievement (35%), School Progress (35%), and Closing the Gaps (30%). An "A" rating indicates high performance across all three domains. However, TEA ratings measure population-level academic performance and do not capture campus culture, teacher tenure, extracurricular quality, principal leadership, or parent community strength. Use TEA ratings as a starting filter, then supplement with campus visits, teacher tenure data, and conversations with current families. Find current ratings at tea.texas.gov.

**Q: What magnet and dual language elementary programs does Austin ISD offer?:**

Austin ISD offers several specialty elementary programs including Spanish/English dual language immersion at multiple campuses, French/English dual language immersion, fine arts magnets, and STEM-focused programming. Magnet programs typically require a separate application and lottery process and are open to any AISD family regardless of attendance zone. Spanish dual language programs are the most oversubscribed, apply early, as waitlists are long. Application windows typically open in December–January for the following academic year. Contact Austin ISD's School Choice office at austinisd.org for current program offerings and application timelines.
