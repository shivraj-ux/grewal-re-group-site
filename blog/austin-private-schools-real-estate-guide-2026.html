Austin Private Schools & Real Estate Guide 2026

By Shivraj Grewal, CLHMS Guild May 9, 2026 14 min read
Proximity to top-rated private schools in Austin directly impacts home values, neighborhoods within a short commute of elite campuses like St. Andrew's Episcopal School command 10–20% price premiums over comparable homes without strong school access. For relocating families and investors alike, understanding the private school landscape is one of the most powerful tools for identifying high-demand Austin zip codes in 2026. This guide covers nine leading Austin private schools, their tuition ranges, and the neighborhoods where buying near them makes the strongest investment case.

How Private Schools Drive Austin Home Values

Austin's rapid population growth has intensified competition for seats at the city's most respected private schools. As families from across the United States and abroad relocate to the region, drawn by tech sector employment at Apple, Tesla, Samsung, and Dell, the calculus of neighborhood selection increasingly begins with the school directory, not the MLS listing.

Research from the National Association of Realtors consistently identifies school quality as one of the top three factors in home purchase decisions nationally. In Austin, where public school quality varies dramatically by zip code and private enrollment has grown more than 18% over the past five years, that dynamic is especially pronounced.

The mechanism is straightforward: premium private schools attract premium buyers, who sustain premium prices. Homes in the 78746 zip code (West Lake Hills / Westlake), which provides the easiest access to St. Andrew's Episcopal School, routinely trade at or above $1.5M, outperforming the broader Austin metro median by a factor of nearly three.

Top Private Schools in Austin 2026

Austin's private school landscape spans classical academies, faith-based institutions, progressive Montessori and Waldorf campuses, and university-preparatory day schools. Here is a detailed profile of the nine schools most frequently cited by relocating families working with our team.

St. Andrew's Episcopal School

West Austin · PK–12 · sas.org
$28,000–$35,000/yr

Austin's most prestigious day school, consistently ranked among the top private schools in Texas. Strong college-placement record with Ivy League acceptances. Draws families to the 78746 zip code from across the state and nationally.

The Khabele School

Central Austin · PK–8 · khabeleschool.org
$18,000–$24,000/yr

Known for progressive pedagogy and a strong diversity commitment. Families from Bouldin Creek, Travis Heights, and Barton Hills seek proximity to this Central Austin anchor institution.

Hyde Park Baptist School

Hyde Park · PK–12
$10,000–$14,000/yr

A faith-based option with competitive academics in the heart of the Hyde Park and Bryker Woods corridor. One of Austin's oldest private schools with a loyal multi-generational family base.

St. Michael's Catholic Academy

North Central Austin · 9–12
$14,000–$18,000/yr

A highly regarded Catholic high school with strong athletics and college-prep academics. Serves families across North Austin neighborhoods from Allandale to Great Hills.

Regents School of Austin

Cedar Park · PK–12 · regentsschool.com
$13,000–$17,000/yr

A classical Christian school in Cedar Park drawing families to the Northwest Austin and Leander corridor. Strong humanities and Latin curriculum; popular with families who also value newer construction homes under $700K.

Austin Waldorf School

Oak Hill / Southwest Austin · PK–12
$13,000–$19,000/yr

One of the few full Waldorf PK–12 programs in Texas. Attracts families to the Oak Hill corridor and Dripping Springs areas. Strong arts integration and nature-based early childhood curriculum.

Brentwood Christian School

North Austin · PK–12
$10,000–$15,000/yr

One of Austin's most established faith-based schools. Located in North Austin near the 183/MoPac corridor, drawing families from Allandale, Crestview, and Windsor Park neighborhoods.

Saint Louis King of France

Pflugerville/NE Austin · PK–8
$8,000–$12,000/yr

A Catholic elementary school in the northeastern Austin metro. One of the more affordable private options, drawing families from Pflugerville, Manor, and northeast Austin, areas with strong value-buy opportunities in 2026.

Headwaters School

South Austin / Buda · PK–8
$12,000–$16,000/yr

A progressive school emphasizing place-based and project-based learning. South Austin families from Travis Heights, Galindo, and Manchaca Road corridors frequently cite Headwaters as a primary school choice.

Westlake Hills: Austin's Premier Private School Corridor

The 78746 zip code, encompassing West Lake Hills, Rollingwood, and areas of western Austin, represents the strongest correlation between private school access and home values in the entire Austin metro. The primary driver: St. Andrew's Episcopal School, which sits on a 41-acre campus straddling the West Austin hillside.

Families willing to pay St. Andrew's tuition, which can reach $35,000 per year at the upper school level, are typically also willing to pay a significant premium for a home that eliminates the cross-town commute. The result is a sustained floor on home prices that defies broader market corrections.

In 2025–2026, median closed prices in 78746 held above $1.5M even as other Austin-area zip codes saw 5–10% corrections. Inventory remains constrained because current owners, many of whom relocated specifically for school access, are reluctant to leave during their children's enrollment years, further supporting prices.

What to Expect When Buying in 78746

Buyers in this corridor should anticipate competitive offers on well-positioned properties, particularly those within 1.5 miles of the St. Andrew's campus. Homes on large lots with Hill Country views command the highest premiums. The neighborhood's limestone terrain also means many properties feature pools, outdoor living spaces, and architectural designs oriented toward the landscape.

The Texas Education Agency reports that Eanes Independent School District, the public school district serving most of 78746, is among the highest-rated districts in Texas, providing a "double premium" for buyers: excellent public AND private school access in the same zip code.

Central Austin's Private School Options

Central Austin's Hyde Park, Bryker Woods, Bouldin Creek, and Travis Heights neighborhoods offer a different proposition: walkable, architecturally rich urban neighborhoods where private school access is a 5–15 minute drive rather than a defining neighborhood characteristic.

The Khabele School draws progressive families to central zip codes including 78703, 78704, and 78705. Hyde Park Baptist School anchors a more traditional faith-based community in the 78751/78752 corridor. Both schools serve families who prioritize urban proximity and the cultural richness of Central Austin over the suburban seclusion of the Westlake hills.

Home prices in Central Austin private school zones typically range from $700K to $1.4M depending on lot size, proximity to amenities, and architectural character. Older bungalows on larger lots along the Shoal Creek and Bull Creek corridors represent among the highest-demand segments of this market.

North Austin & Cedar Park Private Schools

The northern suburbs, Cedar Park, Round Rock, Leander, and northwest Austin, have developed a robust private school ecosystem in the past decade, driven by the massive residential buildout along the 183A toll corridor. Regents School of Austin in Cedar Park anchors the classical Christian school community, while Brentwood Christian serves families in the North Austin neighborhoods along MoPac.

The value proposition in this corridor is compelling for price-conscious buyers: homes priced $450K–$700K with access to well-regarded private schools and strong public school districts (Leander ISD and Round Rock ISD). New construction options are plentiful, and the tech corridor between Apple's campus and the Domain area reduces commute friction for families working in the technology sector.

The Texas Education Agency rates multiple campuses in Leander ISD and Round Rock ISD as among the best in Central Texas, reinforcing value stability even for buyers who eventually transition from private to public education.

Choosing Between Public and Private School Zones

For relocating families, the fundamental question is whether to prioritize public school district quality (where the school assignment comes with the home) or private school proximity (where you pay tuition regardless of your address, but proximity reduces friction).

For most relocating families, the optimal strategy is to identify a shortlist of private schools that match their educational values, then map those schools against neighborhoods that also fall within strong public school districts. The intersection of those two factors, strong public school district AND proximity to preferred private schools, defines the highest-demand, most resilient price zones in the Austin market.

Resources like Niche.com's private school rankings and the City of Austin's education resources page offer useful starting points for families researching school quality before relocating.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do private schools increase home values in Austin?

Yes. Proximity to top-rated private schools in Austin typically commands a 10–20% premium over comparable homes in adjacent neighborhoods without strong school access. In Westlake Hills and West Lake Hills, where St. Andrew's Episcopal School draws elite families, median home prices regularly exceed $1.5M. The premium is durable because it reflects both school quality and the sustained demographic pull of high-income, school-focused families who choose these neighborhoods precisely for educational access.

What are the top private schools in Austin for 2026?

Top Austin private schools for 2026 include St. Andrew's Episcopal School (West Austin/Westlake), The Khabele School (Central Austin), St. Michael's Catholic Academy (North Austin), Regents School of Austin (Cedar Park), Austin Waldorf School (Oak Hill/SW Austin), Hyde Park Baptist School (Central Austin), Brentwood Christian School (North Austin), Saint Louis King of France School (Pflugerville area), and Headwaters School (South Austin). Each serves different educational philosophies and price points, ranging from $8,000 to $35,000 in annual tuition.

What is the average tuition for Austin private schools?

Austin private school tuition ranges widely. Elementary-level programs typically cost $10,000–$22,000 per year, while prestigious college-preparatory secondary programs like St. Andrew's Episcopal School can run $28,000–$35,000 per year. Waldorf and faith-based schools often fall in the $10,000–$18,000 range. Financial aid and scholarship programs are available at most schools; families should contact institutions directly regarding assistance.

Which Austin neighborhoods are closest to the best private schools?

Westlake Hills and West Lake Hills (78746) sit closest to St. Andrew's Episcopal School. Hyde Park and Bryker Woods offer access to Hyde Park Baptist School and The Khabele School. Circle C Ranch and Barton Creek provide reasonable commutes to multiple South and West Austin private schools. Cedar Park provides primary access to Regents School of Austin. For families open to new construction at more accessible price points, Northwest Austin between MoPac and 183A combines proximity to Brentwood Christian and Regents with strong public school district backing from Leander ISD.

Is it worth buying a home near an Austin private school if my child is already enrolled elsewhere?

From a pure investment standpoint, yes. Homes near top Austin private schools tend to hold value and appreciate more consistently, even if you never use those schools yourself. The buyer pool for these properties includes domestic and international families who prize educational access, sustaining demand regardless of market cycles. When you eventually sell, your home will appeal to a motivated, financially qualified buyer pool who are specifically searching for school-adjacent properties.
