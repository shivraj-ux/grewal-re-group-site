Pflugerville TX Real Estate Guide 2026

By Shivraj Grewal, CLHMS Guild  •  May 9, 2026  •  13 min read

Pflugerville, TX is the Austin metro's best-kept affordability secret, a fast-growing northeast suburb located just 15 miles from downtown Austin, offering home prices from $280,000 to $550,000 in an area directly served by the SH-130 toll road and SH-45.

With Tesla Gigafactory approximately 20 minutes south via SH-130, Samsung Taylor about 25 minutes northeast, and Apple's north Austin campus approximately 20 minutes west, Pflugerville sits at the crossroads of Central Texas's most powerful job growth corridor, at price points that most Austin suburbs can no longer match.

This guide covers Pflugerville ISD school ratings, master-planned communities (Blackhawk, Cele Road), Lake Pflugerville recreation, the SH-130 commute advantage, market trends for 2026, and how Pflugerville compares to nearby suburbs on value, schools, and employer access.

Why Pflugerville Offers Austin's Best Value

In a metro where Austin proper median prices have climbed well above $550,000 and suburbs like Round Rock and Cedar Park now frequently run $450,000+, Pflugerville has maintained a compelling price advantage that makes it one of the most rational housing choices in Central Texas for 2026 buyers.

Located 15 miles northeast of downtown Austin along the SH-130 and SH-45 corridors in Travis County (with some eastern sections in Williamson County), Pflugerville offers the Austin metro's combination of:

  • Price: Median ~$360,000 in 2026 Q1, among the lowest of any established Austin suburb
  • Location: 15 miles to downtown Austin, 20 minutes to Tesla, 25 minutes to Samsung Taylor, 20 minutes to Apple
  • New construction volume: Consistent high permit activity in Blackhawk, Cele Road corridor, and east Pflugerville
  • Recreation: Lake Pflugerville City Park, swimming, kayaking, mountain biking, beach volleyball
  • Retail: Stone Hill Town Center (Target, Home Depot, Best Buy, Costco access, dozens of restaurants)
  • SH-130 access: A genuine commute game-changer for southeast Austin and Taylor-area workers

The Pflugerville value case is especially compelling for employees of the major semiconductor, tech, and logistics employers who have flooded the northeast Austin / Taylor / Round Rock corridor with jobs over the past five years. A household earning $120,000–$180,000 from Samsung, Tesla, or Amazon can afford a genuinely comfortable new-construction home in Pflugerville, often impossible in Round Rock, Bee Cave, or Cedar Park at equivalent price points in 2026.

Pflugerville ISD Schools

Pflugerville Independent School District serves the city and surrounding area with approximately 25,000+ students across more than 30 campuses. It is one of the larger districts in the Austin metro and has been growing rapidly to accommodate the area's population expansion. Key campuses buyers research include:

  • Hendrickson High School, Located in the northern Pflugerville area, Hendrickson serves many of the newer master-planned community neighborhoods including Blackhawk. Known for competitive athletics and expanding academic programs.
  • Pflugerville High School, The district's original flagship campus serving established neighborhoods in the city core. Comprehensive UIL programs and deep community connections.
  • Connally High School, Serves western and southern portions of the district, including areas bordering Manor and the SH-130 corridor. Smaller campus with a close-knit community feel.
  • Elementary and middle schools, The district operates multiple elementary and two main middle school campuses (Pflugerville MS, Kelly Lane MS, Park Crest MS) with more facilities being planned as enrollment grows.

An honest assessment: Pflugerville ISD is a solid, improving district, but it is generally rated somewhat lower than adjacent Round Rock ISD to the north or Leander ISD to the northwest, districts with stronger test scores, more highly rated campuses, and a history of high parental engagement. Full current ratings and accountability data are available at pflugervilleisd.net.

Buyers for whom school district ratings are the primary decision driver may find better options in RRISD-zoned communities slightly north of Pflugerville. However, for families prioritizing affordability, Pflugerville ISD provides a solid education with dedicated teachers and good extracurricular programs, and the district is actively investing in facilities and programming as its tax base grows.

Private school options accessible from Pflugerville include Regents School of Austin (west Austin, approximately 35 minutes), various faith-based academies in Round Rock, and the growing network of charter school options within the district area.

Major Employers Driving Pflugerville Demand

Pflugerville's housing demand in 2026 is being driven in large part by employment growth in one of the most remarkable employer concentration zones in American economic history, the northeast Austin / Taylor / Round Rock semiconductor and technology corridor. The numbers are staggering:

Major Employers Accessible from Pflugerville (2026)

  • Tesla Gigafactory Texas (Del Valle / SE Austin), Approximately 20 minutes south via SH-130. Austin's most visible recent mega-employer, with 20,000+ jobs and growing. SH-130 makes Pflugerville dramatically more accessible to Tesla workers than most of north Austin.
  • Samsung Austin Semiconductor (Taylor campus, $17B+ expansion), Approximately 25 minutes northeast via SH-130. Samsung's massive Taylor fab expansion has created thousands of high-paying semiconductor manufacturing and engineering jobs, and Pflugerville provides the most affordable housing at closest proximity for many of these workers.
  • Amazon, Multiple fulfillment, distribution, and delivery station facilities throughout the greater Austin metro, with several directly accessible from Pflugerville via SH-130 and SH-45.
  • Apple (North Austin campus, 3 Apple Way), Approximately 20 minutes west of Pflugerville. Apple's 5,000+ employee north Austin campus includes its Mac Pro manufacturing facility and growing corporate presence.
  • Dell Technologies (Round Rock headquarters), Approximately 15 minutes northwest. Dell remains one of the region's largest employers with thousands of corporate and engineering roles in Round Rock.
  • Oracle (Austin campus), Approximately 25 minutes west. Oracle relocated its headquarters to Austin and maintains a major office complex in north Austin.
  • Healthcare employers, St. David's Medical Center (north Austin), Ascension Seton, and numerous medical office complexes are accessible within 20–30 minutes, making Pflugerville a practical option for Austin healthcare workers.

No other Austin suburb at Pflugerville's price point places residents this close to this concentration of high-wage employment. For a household where one partner works at Tesla and the other at Samsung Taylor, Pflugerville may be the only Austin suburb where the combined commute math actually works, and where new construction at $350,000–$450,000 still exists. This is the core of Pflugerville's 2026 value thesis.

Lake Pflugerville & Recreation

Lake Pflugerville is one of the city's most beloved assets, a 180-acre reservoir managed by the City of Pflugerville as a public park that punches well above its weight in amenities for a city of its size. The lake offers:

  • Swimming beach, A designated swim area with lifeguards during season, making Lake Pflugerville one of the few public swimming spots in the Austin metro outside of Barton Springs Pool
  • Kayaking and canoeing, Non-motorized watercraft are welcome; the calm lake is ideal for kayak and canoe recreation, with rentals available seasonally
  • Mountain biking trails, A challenging and well-maintained mountain bike trail network through the lakeside terrain, popular with the growing Austin mountain biking community
  • Beach volleyball courts, Multiple courts at the beach area draw competitive and recreational players throughout the year
  • Fishing, Bass, catfish, and sunfish fishing from the bank or non-motorized boats; no Texas fishing license required for the lake's catch-and-release areas
  • Hike and bike trails, Multiple miles of paved and natural surface trails connecting the lake to adjacent parkland and neighborhoods
  • Picnic areas and playgrounds, Extensive covered pavilions, picnic tables, and children's play equipment serving the family demographic that dominates Pflugerville's buyer pool

Lake Pflugerville is genuinely one of the Austin metro's hidden recreational gems. For families with children, or active adults who prioritize outdoor access, the lake provides year-round recreational value that makes Pflugerville punch well above its median price point in lifestyle quality. Typhoon Texas waterpark, located in Pflugerville off SH-45, provides additional family entertainment and is among the larger waterparks in the Central Texas region.

Blackhawk & Master-Planned Communities

Blackhawk is Pflugerville's premier master-planned community, a large development along the SH-130 corridor in northern Pflugerville that has become the go-to address for buyers seeking new construction with resort-style amenities at accessible price points. Key features of Blackhawk include:

  • Amenity center and resort pool, A well-appointed community clubhouse, resort-style pool with lazy river elements, splash pad, and fitness center
  • Trail network, Miles of internal hike-and-bike trails connecting neighborhoods throughout the community and linking to the broader Pflugerville trail system
  • Multiple builders, Centex, Pulte, D.R. Horton, and other national builders have participated in Blackhawk's phased development, providing a range of floor plans and price points
  • Hendrickson HS zoning, Blackhawk is generally zoned to Hendrickson High School within Pflugerville ISD
  • SH-130 proximity, The community's position along the SH-130 corridor makes it especially convenient for Tesla and Samsung-area commuters

Home prices in Blackhawk generally run from approximately $340,000 to $520,000 for new construction, with some resales below $320,000 for older phases. The community has matured well, with established landscaping, good commercial buildout along adjacent corridors, and a strong HOA that maintains community standards.

Other Notable Pflugerville Neighborhoods

Beyond Blackhawk, Pflugerville offers a variety of established and growing communities:

  • Cele Road Corridor, Newer construction along the eastern SH-130 edge, with access to Williamson County in some sections and proximity to Samsung Taylor commute routes
  • Stone Hill Farms, An established neighborhood anchored by proximity to Stone Hill Town Center, HEB, Target, Home Depot, Best Buy, Chick-fil-A, and dozens of additional retailers all within walking distance or a short drive
  • Springbrook, An older, well-established neighborhood with mature trees, generally lower price points ($280K–$380K), and walkable access to local parks and schools
  • Highland Park, Established single-family community with larger lots than typical new construction, popular with buyers who prioritize yard space over new finishes
  • Cameron Road area, The southwestern edge of Pflugerville transitions into northeast Austin proper, with urban-feel neighborhoods at relatively accessible price points

Pflugerville's real estate market in 2026 reflects the broader Austin metro pattern, a normalization from the 2020–2022 peak, followed by a gradual tightening as employment growth (particularly from Samsung Taylor and Tesla) sustains demand. The affordability advantage is drawing buyers from more expensive Austin suburbs, adding buyer-side pressure in the $300,000–$450,000 range.

SH-130 Commute Advantage

The SH-130 toll road, officially Texas State Highway 130, also known as the Pickle Parkway, is Pflugerville's most underrated asset and one of the strongest arguments for the city's long-term value. While the rest of Austin debates I-35 widening timelines, Pflugerville residents on the eastern side of the city enjoy largely free-flowing toll road access that bypasses I-35 congestion entirely for southeast and northeast-bound commutes.

Key SH-130 commute scenarios from Pflugerville (based on 2026 typical travel times per TxDOT data and regional analysis):

  • Pflugerville to Tesla Gigafactory (Del Valle): Approximately 18–22 minutes via SH-130 south, consistent most hours of the day, with minimal I-35 exposure. This is dramatically faster than most north or northwest Austin addresses.
  • Pflugerville to Samsung Taylor campus: Approximately 20–28 minutes northeast via SH-130 and SH-79, a clean highway run with no traffic signal-heavy surface roads.
  • Pflugerville to Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (ABIA): Approximately 20–25 minutes via SH-130 south, faster than most of northwest and north Austin addresses.
  • Pflugerville to Round Rock (Dell HQ / downtown Round Rock): Approximately 12–18 minutes north via SH-45 and I-35, a short, reliable commute.
  • Pflugerville to downtown Austin: Approximately 25–40 minutes via SH-130 to I-35 south, or I-35 direct, competitive with many other Austin suburbs, though not as fast as in-city addresses.
  • Pflugerville to Apple north Austin campus: Approximately 18–25 minutes via SH-45 west and SH-183, a manageable commute using mostly limited-access roads.

SH-130 carries a toll, costs vary by segment and vehicle class. Current toll rate tables are available at the TxDOT website. For most commuters, the monthly toll cost ($80–$150/month for typical daily usage) is easily justified by the time savings and reduced vehicle wear compared to stop-and-go I-35 commuting. TxTag or EZ TAG transponders provide the lowest per-mile rates.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pflugerville TX Real Estate

Is Pflugerville TX a good place to buy a home in 2026?

Yes, Pflugerville offers one of the strongest value propositions in the Austin metro in 2026. With a median price of approximately $360,000, it is significantly more affordable than Round Rock ($445K), Cedar Park ($465K), or central Austin ($550K+). The city provides good quality of life with Lake Pflugerville, Stone Hill Town Center retail, and excellent employer proximity for Tesla, Samsung, Amazon, and Apple employees. The primary trade-off is a somewhat lower school district rating versus higher-priced RRISD or LISD alternatives.

What are home prices in Pflugerville TX in 2026?

Pflugerville home prices in 2026 range from approximately $280,000 to $550,000 for most neighborhoods, making it one of the most affordable established Austin-area suburbs. The 2026 Q1 median sale price is approximately $360,000. New construction in Blackhawk and the Cele Road corridor typically runs $340,000–$520,000. Verify current appraised values at traviscad.org for Travis County parcels or wcad.org for Williamson County sections.

How are Pflugerville ISD schools rated?

Pflugerville ISD is a solid, large, and diverse district serving 25,000+ students. High schools include Hendrickson HS, Pflugerville HS, and Connally HS. The district is improving but is generally rated somewhat lower than neighboring Round Rock ISD or Leander ISD in third-party ratings and state accountability scores. Full current data is available at pflugervilleisd.net. For buyers where school district ranking is the top priority, RRISD-zoned communities in nearby Round Rock may be a better fit, with correspondingly higher home prices.

What is the SH-130 commute from Pflugerville?

SH-130 (Pickle Parkway) runs along Pflugerville's eastern edge and provides a critical advantage: it bypasses I-35 congestion entirely for southeast and northeast commutes. From Pflugerville, typical SH-130 commute times include Tesla Gigafactory (~20 min south), Samsung Taylor (~25 min northeast), Austin airport (~22 min south), and Round Rock (~15 min north). SH-130 carries a toll, current rates at txdot.gov. For employees of Tesla and Samsung, Pflugerville's SH-130 access is a major differentiator versus other Austin suburbs.

What is Blackhawk in Pflugerville?

Blackhawk is Pflugerville's premier master-planned community, a large development along SH-130 in northern Pflugerville featuring resort-style amenities (pool, clubhouse, fitness center, trails), multiple national builders (Centex, Pulte, D.R. Horton), and Hendrickson HS zoning. Home prices typically range from $340,000 to $520,000 for new construction, with older-phase resales available from the mid-$300s. Blackhawk is the most sought-after address in Pflugerville for buyers prioritizing community amenities and new construction quality.

How does Pflugerville compare to Round Rock and Cedar Park?

Pflugerville's median price ($360K) is approximately $85,000–$105,000 below Round Rock ($445K) and Cedar Park ($465K). The trade-offs: Pflugerville ISD is rated somewhat lower than Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD. However, Pflugerville's location provides significantly better commute access to Tesla Gigafactory and Samsung Taylor, making it the more practical choice for many tech and semiconductor workers. For buyers prioritizing the highest school ratings, Round Rock ISD communities are worth the premium. For buyers prioritizing employer proximity and housing value, Pflugerville often wins the analysis.
