Rough Hollow Lakeway Real Estate 2026: Resort Living on Lake Travis

Private yacht club, lazy river, resort pools, and Lake Travis ISD schools, Rough Hollow is Lakeway's most amenity-rich master-planned community, with homes ranging from $700K to $2.5M+ and a lifestyle that consistently draws buyers from coastal markets looking for something they can't replicate anywhere else in Austin.

By Shivraj Grewal · Grewal RE Group · Lakeway, TX · ~8 min read

Rough Hollow is not a typical Austin suburb. It is a master-planned resort community on the north shore of Lake Travis, designed from the ground up to deliver a lifestyle that most buyers in this price range have to choose between: excellent schools, genuine water access, resort-caliber amenities, and newer construction with Hill Country views. Within the City of Lakeway's 78734 ZIP code, Rough Hollow occupies its own distinct tier, and in 2026, it continues to draw steady buyer demand from relocating families, remote workers, and investors who understand what the community's private marina represents in terms of long-term value retention.

If you're evaluating Rough Hollow against other Lake Travis corridor options, or trying to understand what separates it from surrounding Lakeway neighborhoods, here is a grounded, practical look at what to expect from the market and the community in 2026.

Rough Hollow Overview: A Resort Community on Lake Travis

Rough Hollow sits along Highlands Blvd and Rough Hollow Pkwy on the north shore of Lake Travis, just off RR 620 in Lakeway, roughly 25 miles west of downtown Austin via RR 620 and Loop 360[4]. The community was developed with a clear identity: a resort-lifestyle address that provides residents with private lake access, world-class amenities, and HOA-maintained standards, all within a top-tier school district. That identity has remained consistent since the community's first phases broke ground, and newer construction phases have continued to raise the bar on finish levels and architectural quality.

The geography itself is a genuine asset. The Lake Travis shoreline, managed in part by the Lower Colorado River Authority (LCRA)[3], defines the community's southern boundary, and Rough Hollow's elevation along the Highland Village plateau provides sweeping Hill Country views that carry real dollar value in the local market. The result is a community that looks and feels like a destination rather than a subdivision, a distinction that matters to buyers arriving from coastal markets where resort communities command prices two to three times higher for comparable amenities.

The community's street grid is organized around Highlands Blvd and its tributaries, with Rough Hollow Pkwy serving as the primary internal corridor connecting residential sections to the yacht club, amenity centers, and the Highland Village commercial node nearby. Bee Creek Rd to the north provides an additional connection to the broader Lakeway and Bee Cave road network.

Rough Hollow Real Estate Market in 2026: Price Tiers and Inventory

Home prices in Rough Hollow in 2026 span a meaningful range, approximately $700,000 to $2.5 million and above, driven primarily by three variables: lot orientation (lake view versus lakefront versus interior), home size and finish level, and proximity to the yacht club and amenity centers[1]. Understanding these tiers before beginning a search will save time and sharpen your offer strategy.

Interior and Hill Country view homes ($700K–$1.1M) represent the community's entry tier. These are typically well-appointed single-family homes of 2,500–3,800 square feet on interior lots, benefiting from all community amenities and Lake Travis ISD schools without carrying the premium of a water-facing position. For buyers whose priority is the school district, resort amenities, and newer construction rather than a personal water view from the living room, this tier delivers exceptional value relative to comparable new construction communities in the Austin metro.

Lake-view homes ($1.1M–$1.8M) occupy lots positioned on the community's elevated ridgelines and slopes, where lake and Hill Country panoramas are visible from primary living spaces and outdoor areas. These homes are among the most competitive in the community's market, view lots at this price point in the Lake Travis corridor are genuinely limited in supply, and they hold value through market cycles with more consistency than interior product. Days on market for accurately priced lake-view homes in early 2026 has been running in the 30–50 day range[1].

Lakefront and large custom homes ($2M–$2.5M+) define Rough Hollow's upper market. True lakefront lots, those with direct Lake Travis frontage and private dock potential, are the scarcest inventory in the community and in the broader Lakeway market. Custom homes in this tier feature finishes, floor plans, and outdoor living spaces that reflect the full intent of the community's design standards. New construction in this tier, including semi-custom builds on remaining premium lots, continues to come to market in 2026 as later phases of the community complete development.

New construction activity remains meaningful in Rough Hollow in 2026, with national and regional builders offering product across multiple sub-communities within the Rough Hollow umbrella. Builder incentives, including rate buydowns and design center credits, are negotiable depending on phase inventory levels, and a knowledgeable buyer's agent can meaningfully improve outcomes in builder negotiations.

Rough Hollow Yacht Club and Marina: What Residents Actually Get

The Rough Hollow Yacht Club and Marina is the amenity that distinguishes this community from virtually every other master-planned development in the Austin area. The facility is private and residents-only, not a public marina, not a shared-access dock, but a genuine yacht club on Lake Travis with boat slips, kayak and paddleboard storage, a waterfront pavilion, and event and gathering space that makes the lake a practical part of daily life rather than a distant feature[3].

For buyers relocating from coastal markets, San Diego, Seattle, South Florida, the Bay Area, the yacht club is often the single most recognizable point of comparison. In those markets, private marina access at comparable residential price points would cost significantly more. In Rough Hollow, marina membership and the associated lifestyle is embedded in the community's HOA structure, which is a meaningful part of the community's value proposition.

Boat slips at the marina are allocated separately from HOA membership and are subject to availability and a waiting list at various times depending on demand and slip turnover. Buyers for whom marina slip access is a non-negotiable should verify current slip availability before writing an offer, and should work with an agent who can help navigate that process. Kayak and paddleboard storage through the Watersports Barn is available to all residents and provides everyday lake access without requiring a boat slip assignment.

The marina also hosts a calendar of community events throughout the year, regattas, sunset cruises, holiday gatherings, that contribute to the social character of Rough Hollow and reinforce why residents describe it as a true community rather than just a neighborhood.

Resort Amenities: What the HOA Delivers on a Daily Basis

Beyond the marina, Rough Hollow's amenity package is what justifies the HOA dues structure for most residents, and what separates the community from Lakeway neighborhoods that share the same school district but not the same lifestyle infrastructure. The amenity campus includes a resort-style lazy river, multiple pools (including adult-only and family pools), a children's splash pad, and outdoor gathering areas that together create a destination residents use regularly rather than occasionally.

The community fitness center is full-service, free weights, cardio equipment, group fitness programming, at a level that competes with standalone commercial gym memberships and eliminates a separate monthly expense for health-conscious residents. The Highland Lakes Club, the community's main amenity hub, serves as an events and social programming center year-round, with facilities available for private events, community gatherings, and club activities.

The trail network within Rough Hollow connects to Lakeway's broader hike-and-bike system, with miles of maintained paths suitable for running, cycling, and walking with direct access to Lake Travis shoreline segments[3]. For remote workers and retirees who organize their days around active outdoor routines, the trail system is a daily amenity that appears in nearly every resident's account of why they chose Rough Hollow over comparable communities.

HOA dues in Rough Hollow vary by sub-community and phase, but buyers should budget for dues in the range of $150–$300 per month for standard community membership, which covers amenity access, common area maintenance, and security. Additional fees apply for boat slip leases, private events, and certain club amenities. Reviewing the full fee schedule and governing documents for a specific property before making an offer is essential, HOA financials and reserve fund health vary across phases.

Schools: Lake Travis ISD from Elementary Through High School

Rough Hollow is served by Lake Travis Independent School District, consistently recognized as one of the top school districts in Texas[2]. The feeder pattern for most of the community runs through Rough Hollow Elementary, a campus located within or adjacent to the community, to Hudson Bend Middle School, and then to Lake Travis High School on RR 620. This three-campus progression means that for most families in Rough Hollow, the same peer group stays together from kindergarten through graduation.

Lake Travis High School holds strong Texas Education Agency accountability ratings and is well-regarded for college preparation, AP and dual enrollment programming, athletics (the Cavaliers are a consistent presence in UIL playoff competition), and performing arts. For families relocating from states with high income taxes, the combination of Lake Travis ISD academic outcomes and Texas's absence of state income tax reliably changes the cost-of-living comparison once the full math is run.

Rough Hollow Elementary, as a campus designed to serve a community of this caliber, benefits from strong parent involvement and a student body drawn predominantly from households that chose this address specifically for school quality. Hudson Bend Middle School serves the broader Lakeway and Four Points corridor and is the largest middle campus in Lake Travis ISD, its extracurricular depth and academic programming reflect the district's investment in a highly engaged family base.

A practical note: school zone boundaries within Lakeway and the broader 78734 ZIP code can vary by specific parcel address. Always verify assigned campuses for a property you are seriously considering, the Lake Travis ISD website maintains an address-specific school locator, and confirming this before offer submission avoids surprises at closing.

Highland Village and the Local Amenity Ecosystem

Day-to-day life in Rough Hollow is supported by the Highland Village commercial node at the intersection of RR 620 and Bee Creek Rd, a neighborhood-scale retail corridor within minutes of the community's residential sections. Highland Village includes a grocery anchor, restaurants ranging from quick-service to sit-down dining, medical and dental offices, a pharmacy, and personal services that cover the majority of weekly errands without requiring a drive toward the Hill Country Galleria in Bee Cave or further into Austin.

The RR 620 corridor itself is among the most important arterials in the Lake Travis area, connecting Rough Hollow and Lakeway north toward the Hwy 183 tech corridor, south toward Hwy 71 and Bee Cave, and east toward Loop 360 and Austin proper. Commutes to downtown Austin run approximately 30–40 minutes under normal traffic conditions; commutes to the Domain and the tech employment corridor along Hwy 183 are typically 35–50 minutes depending on departure time[4]. For the substantial portion of Rough Hollow residents who work partially or fully remotely, these commute windows are academic much of the week, but buyers who require daily downtown office presence should factor peak-hour conditions into their decision.

The City of Lakeway also maintains community parks, a recreation center, and civic programs that supplement Rough Hollow's private amenity package. The broader Lake Travis recreational economy, boat rentals, lakeside dining at various lake-adjacent restaurants accessible by water, live music venues along the RR 620 and Hudson Bend corridors, adds a lifestyle dimension that the immediate community alone does not capture.

Buying Tips: HOA Dues, Marina Slips, Lot Orientation, and Flood Zones

Buying in Rough Hollow involves a specific set of due diligence items that differ meaningfully from purchasing in a standard Austin suburban neighborhood. Working through these with a knowledgeable agent before making an offer prevents costly surprises.

HOA dues and governing documents: Rough Hollow is structured as a master HOA with multiple sub-community HOAs for specific phases. The total monthly obligation for a given property may include both the master HOA fee and a phase-level fee. Review governing documents, recent meeting minutes, and the reserve fund study before any offer, underfunded reserves in a community with this level of amenity infrastructure can translate to special assessments down the line.

Marina slip costs and availability: Boat slip leases at the Rough Hollow Yacht Club are separate from HOA dues and are priced based on slip size and demand. Availability fluctuates, and at various points in the community's history, waitlists have been active for desirable slip categories. If marina slip access is a priority, verify current availability directly with the HOA before entering a purchase contract, and understand that slip assignment is not guaranteed at closing unless separately contracted.

Lot orientation and view premiums: In a community where lake view and lakefront lots command premiums of $200K–$600K+ over comparable interior product, understanding exactly what you are buying, and whether a specific view corridor will remain unobstructed as the community builds out, requires on-the-ground knowledge of which lots in later phases might affect existing sight lines. A buyer's agent with direct Rough Hollow experience can walk you through this before you write an offer[1].

Flood zones and LCRA lake levels: Properties at lower elevations near the Lake Travis shoreline require careful flood zone verification. Lake Travis water levels fluctuate seasonally and over multi-year drought cycles; the LCRA manages lake levels under a highland lakes management protocol that affects both recreational access and flood risk at various elevations[3]. Review FEMA flood map designations and obtain flood insurance quotes for any property with shoreline proximity before proceeding.

Property taxes: The effective property tax rate in Lakeway's 78734 ZIP code typically runs in the range of 1.8%–2.2% all-in, depending on whether a property falls within a Municipal Utility District or special district boundary. On a $1.5M home, that translates to an annual tax obligation of $27,000–$33,000, a figure that should be explicitly modeled in your cost-of-ownership analysis before offer submission.

Rough Hollow vs. Lakeway vs. Lago Vista: Comparing the Lake Travis Corridor

Buyers evaluating lake-lifestyle communities along the Lake Travis corridor frequently compare Rough Hollow, broader Lakeway, and Lago Vista. Each serves a distinct buyer profile, and the differences are consequential enough to matter in a serious purchase decision.

Rough Hollow is the most amenity-intensive and the most comprehensively managed option on the corridor. Its private yacht club, resort amenity campus, newer construction across all price tiers, and immediate access to Rough Hollow Elementary give it a distinct profile for buyers who want the full resort-lifestyle package embedded in their HOA. The trade-off is that the HOA cost structure is higher than standard Lakeway neighborhoods, and the community carries a premium price-per-square-foot that reflects the amenity investment.

Broader Lakeway, the established neighborhoods within the City of Lakeway outside of the Rough Hollow master-plan boundary, offers more variety in housing stock, including a meaningful inventory of 1980s–2000s-era homes at lower price-per-foot that appeal to buyers who want Lake Travis ISD schools and Lakeway's civic infrastructure without Rough Hollow's HOA structure and associated costs. Lake access in standard Lakeway is primarily through city parks and public boat launches rather than a private marina, which is a meaningful lifestyle distinction for boating-focused buyers.

Lago Vista, located further west along the north shore of Lake Travis in Williamson County, occupies a different tier entirely. Home prices are substantially lower (typically $350K–$900K for most inventory), the school district (Lago Vista ISD) is smaller than Lake Travis ISD, and the community's infrastructure is less developed. For buyers whose primary driver is affordability and lake proximity rather than school district quality or resort amenities, Lago Vista can offer compelling value. However, the commute east toward Austin tech employment is longer, and the day-to-day amenity ecosystem requires more driving than either Rough Hollow or central Lakeway.

For buyers in the $900K–$2.5M range who are prioritizing lake access, school quality, and a resort lifestyle that works on a daily basis rather than just on weekends, Rough Hollow is the most complete answer the Lake Travis corridor offers. It earns its premium because the components that make up that premium, private marina, resort amenity campus, Lake Travis ISD feeder, are genuinely rare in combination at this geography and price point.
