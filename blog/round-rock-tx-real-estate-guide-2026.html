Round Rock TX Real Estate Guide 2026

A complete buyer's guide to Round Rock's neighborhoods, schools, home prices, employer access, and long-term investment case, everything you need before you make an offer in 2026.

By Shivraj Grewal · CLHMS Guild · TREC #736060 · May 8, 2026 · Round Rock, TX

Round Rock, Texas delivers something the broader Austin market rarely offers in 2026: strong school districts, genuine new construction, tech-corridor employment access, and a median home price that still sits below the metro average. If you are buying in the Austin area and have not fully evaluated Round Rock, not just as a backup to Austin but as a primary target, this guide walks through everything that matters: the neighborhoods worth knowing, the school district advantage, the employer proximity story, the new construction landscape, and the long-term appreciation data that makes the case for Round Rock as the metro's most overlooked value play.

Round Rock in 2026, Austin's Fastest-Growing Suburb

Round Rock has grown to a population of over 140,000 residents, making it the second-largest city in Williamson County and one of the fastest-growing suburban cities in Texas over the past decade. Situated 20 miles north of downtown Austin via I-35, Round Rock occupies one of the most strategically positioned zip codes in the metro, close enough to Austin's employment core to commute, far enough away to afford significantly more house per dollar.

The city's economic foundation rests on Dell Technologies' global headquarters, which employs tens of thousands in Round Rock and drives substantial demand for housing throughout the city. But the employer story in 2026 goes well beyond Dell: Apple's Austin campus, the Samsung semiconductor fab in Taylor (15 minutes east), Amazon's North Austin distribution and office operations, and Google's Domain presence all sit within a 25-minute radius of most Round Rock neighborhoods, creating one of the best multi-employer access positions in the entire metro.

Market fundamentals in 2026 show a stabilized but active market. The median home price sits at $390,000, a notable $36,000 below the Austin proper median of $426,000 and roughly in line with the broader Travis County average of $389,000. Days on market average 74, and inventory has expanded to approximately 6.8 months, which favors buyers relative to the tight-inventory conditions of 2021–2022. For buyers who want negotiating room and time to evaluate their options, Round Rock's current conditions represent a material improvement over peak market dynamics.[1]

Round Rock Neighborhoods, Where to Buy

Round Rock is not a monolithic suburb, it contains a genuine mix of housing eras, styles, and price points, and understanding the distinctions between neighborhoods matters when you are competing for inventory or evaluating long-term appreciation.

Old Town Round Rock is the city's historic core, centered around Mays Street and Georgetown Avenue. Bungalows and craftsman-style homes dating from the 1920s through 1960s sit on tree-lined streets within walking distance of restaurants, breweries, and the Georgetown Street commercial corridor. Prices range from $300,000 to $550,000. Lots tend to be generous, and the walkability is the best in the city. Buyers here often prize the character and location over newer finishes, and they are right to.

Teravista is a master-planned community on the east side of Round Rock built around a public golf course. The community is mature, with homes ranging from $380,000 to $650,000 and amenities including pools, trails, and community centers. The golf course creates premium lots that command meaningful premiums. Teravista feeds Round Rock ISD, and commute access via Toll 130 is a practical advantage for Samsung Taylor workers.

Forest Creek is another golf course community, located in the northwest quadrant near McNeil High School. Homes run $400,000 to $700,000, with the upper end sitting on the course. The community is established and well-maintained, with mature trees throughout, something buyers coming from new construction communities often underestimate as a value driver.

Mayfield Ranch on the western edge of Round Rock offers newer construction from the 2010s at $380,000 to $580,000. The community feeds Vista Ridge and Cedar Ridge high schools in Round Rock ISD and has easy 183A access for Cedar Park and Northwest Austin commuters.

Paloma Lake in the northeast features a community lake, pools, and a mix of resale and newer construction at $360,000 to $580,000. It is a strong value option for families who want the master-planned amenity package at a slightly lower price than Teravista or Forest Creek.[3]

Round Rock ISD, The Academic Advantage

Round Rock Independent School District is the primary reason most families choose Round Rock over comparable-priced alternatives in the metro. With over 43,000 students across 50+ campuses, RRISD is large enough to offer specialized programs while maintaining district-wide quality standards that consistently outperform the statewide average.[2]

The district's four comprehensive high schools, Round Rock High School, Cedar Ridge High School, McNeil High School, and Stony Point High School, all receive GreatSchools ratings between 7 and 9 out of 10. Cedar Ridge is the district's designated International Baccalaureate (IB) campus, offering the full IB Diploma Programme for motivated students seeking college-equivalent coursework. All four high schools run robust STEM programs with dual-enrollment pathways through Austin Community College.

The district's 95% graduation rate places it well above the Texas state average of 90%, and its college-readiness metrics, as measured by SAT/ACT performance and AP exam pass rates, are consistently among the top 10% of Texas districts by size. For buyers comparing Round Rock ISD to Austin ISD, the school quality gap is significant: RRISD averages 8.5 out of 10 versus AISD's 6.4 average across comparable school types.

When families in this price band evaluate Round Rock ISD against Eanes ISD, the gold standard for Austin-area school districts, RRISD competes remarkably well. Eanes ISD commands a substantial home price premium ($700K+ median) while Round Rock ISD delivers most of the academic outcomes at less than 60% of the cost. For value-conscious families, this is perhaps the strongest argument for Round Rock in the entire Austin metro analysis.

Dell and Tech Employer Proximity, The Commute Story

The Austin metro's technology employment base is not concentrated downtown, it is distributed across a north-south corridor from Samsung's Taylor fab to Apple's Cupertino-on-I-35 campus. Round Rock sits at nearly the geometric center of this corridor, which is what makes its employer access so strategically strong for 2026 buyers.[6]

Dell Technologies' global headquarters is located in Round Rock proper, the company's campus is 15 minutes from most Round Rock neighborhoods. Apple's Austin campus at 12545 Riata Vista Circle is approximately 25 minutes south via 183A. The Samsung Advanced Logic Chip fab in Taylor, one of the largest semiconductor investments in U.S. history at $17 billion, sits approximately 20 minutes east on Toll 130, making Round Rock one of the best-positioned residential markets for the thousands of high-paying engineering and operations jobs that fab is creating.

Amazon's North Austin fulfillment and office presence is 25 minutes down 183. Google's Domain office is 22 minutes away. For buyers who work at any combination of these employers, or who are considering the metro partly based on optionality across multiple tech employers, Round Rock's position is difficult to beat. No other Austin-area suburb at this price point sits within a 25-minute radius of this many major tech employers simultaneously.

New Construction in Round Rock 2026

One of Round Rock's defining advantages over Austin proper in 2026 is the active new construction market. Where central Austin has effectively exhausted its infill capacity, Round Rock has multiple active master-planned communities and production builder subdivisions generating fresh inventory across price bands that would be impossible to replicate inside the Austin city limits.

Active builders in Round Rock and its immediate periphery include Taylor Morrison, David Weekley, Meritage Homes, Pulte, and Perry Homes. Price ranges for new construction generally run $350,000 to $550,000 for standard production builds, with custom and semi-custom options extending to $700,000 and above in communities like Forest Creek's premium sections.

Buyers pursuing new construction in Round Rock should pay close attention to Municipal Utility District (MUD) designations. Outer suburban areas, particularly those under development in the northeast and east, often sit within MUD districts that add $0.40 to $0.70 per $100 of assessed value to the effective tax rate. On a $500,000 home, this can add $2,000 to $3,500 annually to your tax burden. MUDs are legal mechanisms to finance the infrastructure costs of new development, and they typically carry sunset provisions of 15 to 25 years, but buyers should fully understand the MUD status of any new construction before signing a contract.[5]

Builder incentives in 2026 are meaningfully better than 2021–2022 conditions. With inventory elevated relative to peak, builders are offering mortgage rate buydowns (often 1–2 points), closing cost contributions of $10,000–$20,000, and design center upgrades at no cost. A buyer's agent with active builder relationships in Round Rock can negotiate additional incentives that are not publicly advertised, this is one of the practical value-adds of working with an advisor who transacts regularly in the Williamson County new construction market.

Round Rock Investment, Long-Term Appreciation Story

For buyers evaluating Round Rock not just as a primary residence but as a long-term wealth-building asset, the historical appreciation data makes a compelling case. Round Rock home values have increased approximately 94% since 2015, a figure that closely tracks but slightly lags Austin proper's more volatile appreciation curve, and that is actually a feature, not a bug, for risk-conscious investors.[4]

Austin proper's appreciation from 2015 to 2026 included extreme peaks in 2021–2022 followed by a meaningful correction in 2023–2024. Round Rock's appreciation curve was steadier, it captured most of the upside with significantly less downside volatility. For buyers who experienced the 2023 correction in Austin-proper portfolios, Round Rock's relative stability is a material consideration.

The rental market in Round Rock is also strong, driven by continuous inflows of tech workers who prefer to rent while evaluating the market, UT students and medical residents whose housing needs flow north of Austin, and the steady demand created by Dell, Samsung, and Apple employees on assignment or relocation. Single-family residences in Round Rock currently generate cap rates in the 4.5% to 5.5% range, well above the 3.0% to 4.0% cap rates achievable in Austin 78704 or 78702. For investors, this means Round Rock properties pencil out as income-producing assets in ways that central Austin properties often do not at current price levels.

Population growth projections from the City of Round Rock and Williamson County suggest continued expansion through 2030, with infrastructure investments in transportation and commercial development that should sustain demand. The city's fiscal position remains strong, and its economic development incentive program continues to attract employer relocations that drive residential demand. Round Rock is not a contrarian bet, it is a well-understood growth story that still has meaningful upside at current prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Round Rock TX a good place to live?

Yes. Round Rock consistently ranks among the best places to live in Texas. With a population over 140,000, a highly rated school district, low crime relative to Austin, abundant parks, and a booming employment base anchored by Dell Technologies, it offers suburban quality of life with strong urban-area access. The median home price of $390K in 2026 makes it one of the most attainable markets in the Austin metro for families seeking space, schools, and proximity to tech employers.

What is the average home price in Round Rock TX in 2026?

The median home price in Round Rock, TX is approximately $390,000 as of May 2026, according to Austin Board of Realtors data. This represents a meaningful discount compared to the broader Austin metro median of $426,000. Days on market currently average 74, and inventory stands at approximately 6.8 months, conditions that give buyers time and negotiating leverage not available in earlier years.

How far is Round Rock from Austin?

Round Rock is approximately 20 miles north of downtown Austin via I-35. Under normal conditions, the drive takes about 30–40 minutes; during rush hour it can extend to 50–60 minutes, especially on I-35. Many Round Rock residents use the 183A tollway or Mopac as alternative corridors. Major employers in the northwest corridor, Apple, Dell, Samsung, are even closer, with commute times of 15–25 minutes from most Round Rock neighborhoods.

What school district is Round Rock TX in?

The majority of Round Rock falls within Round Rock Independent School District (Round Rock ISD), one of the highest-rated public school districts in Central Texas. The district serves over 43,000 students across 50+ campuses, with high schools including Round Rock HS, Cedar Ridge HS, McNeil HS, and Stony Point HS, all rated 7 to 9 out of 10 on GreatSchools. Cedar Ridge offers an International Baccalaureate (IB) program and the district boasts a 95% graduation rate. A small number of properties on the outer edges may fall within Hutto ISD or Georgetown ISD, so buyers should always verify the exact school assignment before submitting an offer.
