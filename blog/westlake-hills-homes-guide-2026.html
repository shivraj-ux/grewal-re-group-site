Westlake Hills Homes for Sale Guide 2026

By Shivraj Grewal  •  May 9, 2026  •  12 min read

Westlake Hills is Austin's premier luxury enclave, an independent city west of downtown where Eanes ISD's #1-ranked schools, hill country views, larger lots, and the absence of Austin city property taxes converge to create some of the region's most consistent demand and highest home values. Median prices range from $1.5M to $3M+, and estate properties regularly exceed $5M. If you are a buyer prioritizing top-tier schools, privacy, and long-term appreciation, Westlake Hills belongs at the top of your Austin home search.

Why Westlake Hills Commands Premium Prices

Westlake Hills is not merely a neighborhood, it is an incorporated city of approximately 3,500 residents nestled in the Texas Hill Country just six to eight miles west of downtown Austin. This distinction matters enormously for buyers. Because Westlake Hills maintains its own city government, homeowners are not subject to Austin city property taxes, a structural advantage that can save owners $5,000–$12,000+ annually on a luxury home, depending on the appraised value.

The combination of prime location, school district quality, generous lot sizes, and dramatic Hill Country topography creates a market where demand consistently outpaces supply. Technology executives relocating from Silicon Valley, San Francisco, and Seattle routinely identify Westlake Hills as their first choice in the Austin metro. The terrain provides natural privacy buffers, and the views from ridge-line and canyon-rim properties are genuinely irreplaceable assets.

Major employers accessible from Westlake Hills include Apple's Austin campus (Parmer Lane corridor via MoPac), Dell Technologies, Tesla's Gigafactory (southeast Austin), Oracle's new campus, and dozens of high-growth technology startups concentrated in North Austin. Commute times by car to these campuses typically run 20–40 minutes during off-peak hours, without passing through downtown gridlock via Loop 360.

Beyond tech, Westlake Hills attracts executives in finance, healthcare, law, and private equity who value discretion, quality public education, and neighborhood stability. The city's strict development standards and low density keep character intact while supporting steady long-term appreciation.

Eanes ISD: The #1 Driver of Westlake Hills Values

If there is one single factor that separates Westlake Hills from comparable luxury markets across Austin, it is the Eanes Independent School District. Eanes ISD is consistently ranked the #1 school district in the Austin metropolitan area by sources including Niche.com and the Texas Education Agency. The district's performance on standardized assessments, graduation rates, college acceptance rates, and Advanced Placement participation places it among the top two percent of all Texas school districts.

For buyers with school-age children, access to Eanes ISD is not merely a preference, it is a primary financial decision. Properties that are zoned to Eanes ISD carry a measurable premium over comparable properties in neighboring districts. Research consistently shows that exceptional school districts protect and enhance property values through economic downturns, as the demand base of school-driven buyers is more inelastic than the broader market.

School Attendance Zones in Westlake Hills

Buyers purchasing within Westlake Hills city limits are generally zoned to the following Eanes ISD campuses:

  • Eanes Elementary School, Grades PK–5. Located on Eanes School Road. Consistently high academic performance and a strong parent community.
  • Hill Country Middle School, Grades 6–8. Situated in the heart of the community. Strong STEM, arts, and athletics programming.
  • Westlake High School, Grades 9–12. Ranked among Texas's top public high schools; known for competitive athletics (state championships in multiple sports), robust AP and dual-enrollment programs, and a nationally recognized theatre department.

It is important to note that attendance zones can shift over time, and certain parcels in unincorporated Travis County adjacent to Westlake Hills may feed into different elementary campuses within Eanes ISD. Always verify the assigned campus through the eanesisd.net school finder using the specific property address before making a purchase commitment.

Westlake Hills Neighborhood Breakdown

Westlake Hills is not a single subdivision, it encompasses a range of micro-neighborhoods, custom home streets, and platted sections, each with distinct character, price ranges, and buyer profiles. Understanding these distinctions is critical for buyers comparing properties marketed broadly as "Westlake Hills."

City of Westlake Hills (Incorporated)

The incorporated city limits cover the core of what most buyers mean when they reference Westlake Hills. Properties within city limits carry official Westlake Hills addresses and benefit from the city's own zoning code, which tends to be more protective of lot sizes, heritage tree canopy, and overall density than unincorporated Travis County. Many streets are private, with architectural covenants and deed restrictions that govern exterior materials, setbacks, and accessory structures. Minimum lot sizes in many sections run from half an acre to two-plus acres, preserving a rural character unusual this close to a major urban center.

Unincorporated Travis County Near Westlake Hills

A meaningful portion of the area marketed as "Westlake Hills" to buyers is technically in unincorporated Travis County, carrying a "Westlake Hills" or "Austin" mailing address but governed by Travis County rather than the City of Westlake Hills. These properties may still feed into Eanes ISD and enjoy similar lifestyle advantages, but zoning, code enforcement, and deed restriction administration can differ. Property tax rates and homestead exemption applications should be verified through the Travis Central Appraisal District (traviscad.org). Buyers should also confirm whether a given parcel is within the City of Westlake Hills ETJ (Extra-Territorial Jurisdiction) for future development and annexation considerations.

Deed Restrictions vs. No Deed Restrictions

Westlake Hills contains a patchwork of deed-restricted and non-deed-restricted areas. In deed-restricted sections, homeowners associations or recorded covenants govern uses, exterior finishes, landscaping standards, and sometimes lot subdivisions. In areas without deed restrictions, primarily older custom home streets platted before HOA-driven development became standard, buyers have more flexibility for additions, detached structures, and ADUs, but also less protection from neighboring property changes. Understanding this distinction before purchasing is essential, particularly for buyers planning future renovations or additions.

Gated Communities & Private Estates

Several gated and master-planned communities are located in or adjacent to Westlake Hills, offering additional security, amenity infrastructure, and community covenant protections:

Barton Creek

The Barton Creek master-planned community encompasses numerous gated sections (Barton Creek Sections A through I and additional phases) on the southwest fringe, centered around the Barton Creek Country Club and its multiple championship golf courses. Lots range from half an acre to several acres, and homes typically run $1.8M to $8M+, with exceptional custom estates well above that. The community primarily falls within Eanes ISD and offers one of Austin's most established luxury golf, tennis, and social club lifestyles. The Hill Country scenery and protected greenbelt borders make many lots feel genuinely private.

Davenport Ranch

Davenport Ranch is a deed-restricted community situated along the Colorado River (Lake Austin) corridor on the west side, blending influences of River Place and the broader Westlake Hills area. The neighborhood features a private country club option and is characterized by mature tree cover and homes from the 1980s–2000s that are frequently updated, expanded, or replaced with new construction. The community's proximity to Lake Austin and Loop 360 makes it highly sought after. Price range: $1.5M–$4M+ for established homes; new construction can reach $6M+ for well-positioned lots.

Rob Roy on the Lake

Rob Roy is an exclusive, low-density gated enclave on Lake Austin's south shore featuring some of the most coveted waterfront estates in the entire Austin market. Properties rarely change hands, and when they do, prices start at $4M and frequently exceed $10M–$15M for deep-water lakefront estates. Eanes ISD attendance zones typically apply, and the neighborhood's association maintains strict standards for architectural consistency and property maintenance.

Property Tax Advantage

One of the most financially significant and frequently misunderstood advantages of Westlake Hills is the property tax structure. Austin homeowners pay the City of Austin tax rate (approximately $0.44–$0.48 per $100 of assessed value in recent budget cycles) layered on top of Travis County, Eanes ISD, and other applicable levies. Westlake Hills homeowners replace the Austin city rate with the much smaller City of Westlake Hills municipal rate, historically in the range of $0.07–$0.10 per $100, producing a meaningfully lower combined effective rate.

On a $2.5M assessed value, the effective annual tax difference can represent $9,000–$12,000 in savings, a figure that compounds significantly over a 10-year holding period. Always verify current adopted rates through the Travis Central Appraisal District and consult your CPA for a property-specific analysis.

Market Trends 2026

The Westlake Hills luxury market entering 2026 reflects Austin's broader bifurcation: overall inventory across the metro has grown from 2022–2023 lows, but truly desirable properties in top Eanes ISD-zoned locations remain supply-constrained. Well-priced homes between $1.5M and $2.5M are seeing median days on market of 30–60 days, with multiple-offer situations still occurring for move-in-ready homes on large lots with views or premium finishes.

Above $3.5M, the market is more buyer-friendly, with sellers showing more flexibility on terms and pricing as the luxury buyer pool at that level is narrower. Properties above $6M, particularly estate-scale homes on large acreage with lake access or exceptional views, can take 90–180 days or more to find the right buyer, but when they do transact, values hold firm due to genuine scarcity.

New Construction vs. Established Estates

Buyers in Westlake Hills face a meaningful strategic choice: pursue an established estate (often with larger lots, mature heritage trees, original character, and renovation upside) or target new construction. New construction in Westlake Hills tends to concentrate on tear-down lots or custom homesites in newer sections. Contemporary spec homes feature open floor plans, resort-style pools, integrated smart-home systems, and chef kitchens that appeal to tech-sector buyers accustomed to high-specification environments.

Established estates, particularly those with original 1970s–1990s architecture on one-acre-plus lots, offer buyers the opportunity to customize finishes, add value through strategic renovation, or commission a full reconstruction that maximizes the land. For sophisticated buyers willing to manage a renovation or build process, the established estate pathway can produce superior long-term returns on invested capital.

Why Tech Executives Choose Westlake Hills

Westlake Hills has become a primary destination for C-suite technology executives and founders relocating to Austin from California, New York, Seattle, and globally. The neighborhood's alignment with tech-buyer priorities is precise: privacy and security (gated options, large lots, low foot traffic), top-tier public education eliminating the need for private school tuition, proximity to Austin's tech employment corridor without downtown congestion, the property tax advantage that reads well in LCOE analyses, and the lifestyle signaling of an established, prestige address. Hill Country views and access to Lake Austin recreation complete the value proposition.

How to Search for Westlake Hills Homes Effectively

Searching for Westlake Hills homes requires precision. Many listing portals conflate the incorporated city, unincorporated Travis County, and other nearby communities under a single "Westlake Hills" label. For a truly accurate search, buyers should filter by city of Westlake Hills boundaries, school zone (Eanes ISD specifically), and MLS area designations. Working with an agent who specializes in this corridor, and who has access to off-market and pocket listings, is the most reliable path to seeing the full inventory before it goes public.

Key considerations when evaluating any Westlake Hills property:

  • Confirm Eanes ISD campus assignment via the district's official address lookup tool at eanesisd.net
  • Verify city jurisdiction (incorporated Westlake Hills vs. unincorporated Travis County) via traviscad.org
  • Review deed restrictions and HOA documents thoroughly, many parcels have layered covenants that survive ownership transfers
  • Assess tree canopy and heritage tree (8-inch+ diameter) counts, as removal requires city or county permits and can significantly affect construction plans
  • Evaluate access roads, some Westlake Hills streets become challenging in heavy rain; local knowledge matters
  • Understand flood zone designations, portions of Westlake Hills near creeks and canyon bottoms carry flood zone overlay requirements; verify with FEMA flood maps and a qualified inspector

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the median home price in Westlake Hills in 2026?

Median home prices in Westlake Hills range from $1.5M to $3M+, with luxury estates and gated community properties often exceeding $5M. Entry points under $1.5M are rare and typically require significant renovation investment. Price per square foot averages approximately $650–$720 for well-located, updated properties as of 2026. New construction custom homes with premium Hill Country views and finishes can approach or exceed $800/sqft.

Is Westlake Hills in the Eanes ISD school district?

Yes. The City of Westlake Hills falls entirely within the Eanes Independent School District, consistently ranked #1 in the Austin metro. Schools include Eanes Elementary, Hill Country Middle School, and Westlake High School. Always verify your specific address at eanesisd.net before purchasing, as zones do occasionally update.

Do Westlake Hills homeowners pay Austin city taxes?

No. Westlake Hills is its own incorporated city and homeowners do not pay Austin city property taxes, producing a meaningfully lower effective tax rate than comparable Austin addresses. On a $2.5M assessed value, annual savings can exceed $9,000–$12,000. Verify current rates and your specific parcel's appraisal through traviscad.org.

How far is Westlake Hills from downtown Austin?

Westlake Hills is approximately 6–8 miles west of downtown Austin. Off-peak drive time is typically 15–20 minutes via Loop 360 or MoPac (Loop 1). During morning and evening rush hours, allow 25–40 minutes. Many Westlake Hills residents commute north to tech campuses via Loop 360 to US-183, bypassing downtown congestion entirely.

What are the best gated communities in Westlake Hills?

Top gated and private communities in the Westlake Hills area include Barton Creek (championship golf, multiple gated sections, $1.8M–$8M+), Davenport Ranch (lake-adjacent, mature trees, $1.5M–$4M+), and Rob Roy on the Lake (ultra-exclusive Lake Austin waterfront, $4M–$12M+). Each has distinct HOA structures, deed restrictions, amenity packages, and price tiers. Contact Shivraj Grewal at (512) 617-0001 for current availability in any of these communities.
