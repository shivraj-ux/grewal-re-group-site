Westlake / Eanes ISD Real Estate Market Report 2026

Austin's most prestigious school district and what it means for home values in West Lake Hills, Rollingwood, and the 78746 corridor, with school rankings, price-per-sqft data, and 2026 buyer trends.

By Shivraj Grewal, CLHMS Guild · CNE Published May 9, 2026 Updated May 2026 ~17 min read
Quick Answer

The Eanes ISD school district drives a 20–35% price premium over comparable homes outside its boundaries. West Lake Hills homes range from $1.2M to $5M+, Rollingwood from $900K to $2.5M, and the 78746 zip code commands $400–$800+ per square foot. Westlake High School's consistent ranking as one of the top high schools in Texas, alongside severely constrained inventory, sustains appreciation that has outperformed the broader Austin market for over a decade.

Introduction: Why Eanes ISD Is Austin's Most Coveted School District Address

In the Austin luxury real estate market, few factors command more consistent buyer attention than school district assignment, and no district in the metro area inspires more sustained demand than the Eanes Independent School District. Serving the communities of West Lake Hills, Rollingwood, and surrounding portions of unincorporated Travis County in the 78746 zip code, Eanes ISD has built a decades-long reputation as one of Texas's highest-performing school districts.

The Westlake / Eanes ISD market is distinctive among Austin-area submarkets for several reasons: it is geographically constrained by the Barton Creek greenbelt and the Colorado River, limiting buildable land and suppressing new supply; it commands the highest price per square foot among Travis County residential submarkets; and it attracts a buyer profile that is unusually resistant to macro economic pressure, driven primarily by high-income tech executives, finance professionals, and established Austin business leaders who view the school district as non-negotiable.

This 2026 market report draws on data from Eanes ISD, the Texas Education Agency, Travis County Appraisal District, GreatSchools, and the Texas Real Estate Research Center at Texas A&M University, synthesized with ground-level transactional intelligence from active work in this submarket.

The Eanes ISD School District Premium: What the Data Shows

The question buyers and sellers most frequently ask about the Westlake / Eanes ISD market is simple: exactly how much premium does the school district command? The honest answer is that the premium is real, measurable, and has proven remarkably durable across market cycles.

Research consistently shows that Eanes ISD properties command a 20–35% premium over statistically comparable homes, similar square footage, lot size, age, and condition, located just outside the district boundary. This premium is not just theoretical: it is observable in side-by-side comparisons of homes that sit within a few blocks of the district boundary line.

Critically, the Eanes ISD premium persists even for buyers who have no school-age children and no immediate plans to enroll students. This is because the market has internalized the school district quality as a proxy for neighborhood stability, income demographics, and long-term demand, essentially, buyers without children still want the school district because they know future buyers will want it. This makes Eanes ISD homes among the most liquid high-value residential assets in the Austin market.

Eanes ISD Schools: Rankings, Performance, and Buyer Impact

Understanding the specific schools within the Eanes ISD system helps buyers assess assignment by address and understand which feeder patterns align with their family's priorities. The district operates six elementary schools, two middle schools, and Westlake High School as the sole high school, a structure that creates a cohesive community from kindergarten through graduation.

Westlake High School

Grades 9–12 · 3,200+ students · 78746
GreatSchools Rating: 10/10 · TEA Rating: Exemplary | Consistently ranked #1 or #2 in Texas among large public high schools

Westlake High School is the single most powerful value driver in the Eanes ISD system and among the most impactful schools in Texas real estate markets. Its academic performance, with a college acceptance rate and AP participation rate that rival elite private schools, combined with an outstanding athletics program (football championships, swimming, academics) creates a cultural proposition that resonates across buyer profiles. All Eanes ISD properties feed to Westlake HS.

Hill Country Middle School

Grades 6–8 · West Lake Hills
GreatSchools Rating: 9.5/10 · TEA: Exemplary

One of two middle schools in the district. Hill Country MS feeds into Westlake HS and serves the western and central portions of the Eanes ISD territory. Strong STEM and fine arts programming. The feeder pattern for Hill Country MS is a frequent consideration for buyers with middle-school-age children choosing between West Lake Hills neighborhoods.

West Ridge Middle School

Grades 6–8 · Austin / Eanes ISD
GreatSchools Rating: 9/10 · TEA: Exemplary

Serves the eastern portions of the Eanes ISD territory. Also feeds to Westlake HS. Comparable academic standing to Hill Country MS with a slightly different student body demographics and extracurricular emphasis.

Bridge Point Elementary School

Grades K–5 · West Lake Hills
GreatSchools Rating: 9.5/10 · TEA: Exemplary

One of Eanes ISD's flagship elementaries, located in the heart of West Lake Hills. Bridge Point ES is frequently cited by buyers as a primary driver of neighborhood selection. Its assignment zone covers significant portions of the premium West Lake Hills residential area.

Eanes Elementary School

Grades K–5 · Rollingwood / West Lake Hills
GreatSchools Rating: 9/10 · TEA: Exemplary

The district's namesake elementary, serving Rollingwood and adjacent areas. Eanes ES feeds into the Hill Country MS → Westlake HS pipeline. The school's assignment zone is a major factor in Rollingwood's sustained buyer demand.

School boundaries are subject to change. Always verify current assignment with Eanes ISD using the specific property address before making any purchase decision based on school zoning.

West Lake Hills: Austin's Premier Hillside Residential City

West Lake Hills is a small, incorporated city of approximately 3,300 residents that occupies the wooded limestone hills west of Austin between MoPac (Loop 1) and Bee Cave Road. It is geographically intimate, the city covers only about 3.6 square miles, but its residential density is deliberately low, with large lots and significant tree cover creating an environment that feels remarkably private and removed from Austin's urban intensity despite being minutes from downtown.

Home prices in West Lake Hills reflect both the Eanes ISD premium and the scarcity of available inventory. The city has virtually no undeveloped buildable lots remaining, meaning supply is limited to resales and tear-down opportunities. Land-value buyers, developers and custom home builders, regularly acquire dated homes for demolition and reconstruction, and new custom homes in West Lake Hills command the highest price-per-square-foot in the Austin metro area outside of downtown high-rises.

Price Segmentation in West Lake Hills

Entry-level West Lake Hills ($1.2M – $2M): Properties in this range are typically original or lightly updated 1970s–1990s construction on standard lots. Many represent the "last best buy" in the city before tear-down economics take over. Buyers in this range are often families prioritizing school access over finish quality, or investors planning renovation or rebuild projects.

Mid-range West Lake Hills ($2M – $3.5M): This segment captures the bulk of the city's active market. Updated or newer construction, typically 3,500–5,500 sq ft, on lots ranging from 0.3 to 0.75 acres. Hill Country views, mature trees, and outdoor living spaces are standard expectations at this price point. This is where the competition among buyers is most active.

Luxury West Lake Hills ($3.5M – $5M+): Custom homes on premium lots, often with panoramic Hill Country views, long private driveways, resort-style outdoor amenities, and architectural distinction. At the upper end, properties can exceed 8,000 sq ft with multiple guest structures and expansive grounds. This segment trades more slowly but consistently attracts the highest-income buyer demographic.

Tear-Down and Lot Values

A critical dynamic unique to West Lake Hills is the tear-down market. Lots with Eanes ISD zoning and buildable West Lake Hills addresses routinely trade in the $900K–$1.5M range, often with existing structures that are demolished entirely. For buyers who want to build their dream home in this location, securing the right lot is step one. Working with an agent who tracks off-market opportunities and understands the nuances of West Lake Hills' slope regulations, heritage tree ordinances, and city permit processes is essential.

Rollingwood: Eanes ISD Access at a Lower Entry Point

Rollingwood is a small incorporated city immediately adjacent to West Lake Hills, sitting between Bee Cave Road and MoPac to the east. With a total area of roughly 0.5 square miles and approximately 1,500 residents, Rollingwood is even smaller than West Lake Hills and equally tightly controlled in terms of development density.

The key differentiation from West Lake Hills:

  • Price: Rollingwood's median home price of approximately $1.3M–$1.5M represents a meaningful discount to West Lake Hills, making it the more accessible entry into Eanes ISD for buyers with $900K–$2.5M budgets.
  • Proximity: Rollingwood is marginally closer to downtown Austin and the MoPac corridor, which matters for buyers with regular downtown commutes or those valuing proximity to Austin's cultural amenities.
  • Lot Size: Rollingwood lots tend to be smaller than in West Lake Hills, with more original 1960s–1970s ranch-style homes on quarter-acre lots.
  • Renovation Activity: Because Rollingwood's housing stock is older and the lots smaller, significant renovation and tear-down activity has reshaped much of the city's streetscape. New custom construction in Rollingwood now frequently trades in the $1.8M–$2.8M range.

For buyers who need to be in Eanes ISD but find West Lake Hills pricing beyond their budget, Rollingwood is the natural target. The Eanes Elementary feeder pattern, one of the district's strongest elementaries, covers significant portions of Rollingwood, making school assignment comparable to many parts of West Lake Hills at a materially lower price point.

Barton Creek Country Club: Lifestyle and Community Anchor

The Barton Creek Country Club, situated in the hills west of Austin within the Westlake / Eanes ISD market area, is a significant lifestyle and community anchor for the premium residential market. With four championship golf courses, a full spa, tennis facilities, and a robust social and dining program, Barton Creek CC membership is a common feature of the buyer profile in the upper tiers of the Westlake market.

Properties within convenient distance of Barton Creek CC, particularly those in the Barton Creek subdivision itself, which sits within the Eanes ISD boundary for some areas, command a lifestyle premium beyond the standard school district differential. Buyers purchasing in the Barton Creek neighborhood should verify school district assignment carefully, as the area sits near the boundary between Eanes ISD and Austin ISD, and individual streets can fall on either side.

The Barton Creek greenbelt, which borders significant portions of the Westlake residential market to the east, adds both amenity value and development restriction, no residential development is permitted in the greenbelt, making it a permanent open-space buffer that enhances views and privacy for adjacent properties.

Bee Cave Road Corridor: Connectivity and Commerce

Bee Cave Road (Texas State Highway 2244) is the primary commercial and transportation spine of the Westlake / Eanes ISD market. Running from MoPac westward through West Lake Hills, Rollingwood, and into Bee Cave, it connects the residential neighborhoods to Austin's employment centers and provides the corridor's retail and dining infrastructure.

Proximity to Bee Cave Road is a double-edged sword for residential properties: closer properties benefit from convenience, but properties within earshot of the road sacrifice the privacy and quiet that premium buyers in this market expect. The most sought-after residential locations in West Lake Hills are typically set back from Bee Cave Road on the ridgelines and in the canyons that characterize the area's terrain, where hill country views, mature trees, and distance from traffic create the premium residential experience that commands the highest prices.

This has the effect of creating a floor under older, well-located properties: even dated homes on good lots carry significant value because the cost to create a comparable new address is extraordinarily high.

Inventory: Why Scarcity Defines the Westlake Market

The Westlake / Eanes ISD market operates under conditions of structural inventory scarcity. The combination of West Lake Hills' and Rollingwood's fully developed land, the Barton Creek greenbelt's permanent open-space status, and the Texas Hill Country's topographic constraints means there is essentially no new land available for residential development within the core Eanes ISD market area.

In practical terms, the Westlake active listing count at any given time is typically 40–70 homes across all price ranges. For context, this is a market with approximately 4,500–5,000 single-family homes in West Lake Hills and Rollingwood combined. The annualized turnover rate of approximately 3–4% translates to roughly 150–200 transactions per year in the core market, a number that has been declining as long-term residents hold, prices rise, and the replacement cost of leaving the market increases.

This inventory scarcity has several practical implications for buyers:

  • Off-market transactions are common. An estimated 20–30% of Westlake / Eanes ISD transactions occur off-market, through broker-to-broker communication. Buyers who work with agents with deep local networks have access to opportunities that never reach the MLS.
  • Speed matters. Well-priced, well-presented listings in the sub-$2.5M range regularly receive multiple offers within the first 7–14 days. Buyers need pre-approval letters (or proof of funds) ready before making offers.
  • Competition intensifies in late spring. The Westlake market's most active season is March–June, driven by families trying to close before the school year ends. Competition during this window is meaningfully more intense than the fall market.

Who Buys in Westlake / Eanes ISD: The 2026 Buyer Profile

The Westlake / Eanes ISD buyer profile is more homogeneous than most Austin-area markets, not in terms of geography of origin, but in terms of income, profession, and family structure. Understanding who is buying helps sellers prepare their homes for the right audience and helps buyers understand their competition.

Technology Executives

The largest buyer segment. Executives and senior engineers from Dell, Apple (Apple Campus in Northwest Austin), Google, Tesla Gigafactory management, and Austin's rapidly growing venture-backed startup ecosystem. Often relocating from California or the Pacific Northwest, frequently with significant equity compensation and a preference for the tax environment Texas offers.

Finance Professionals

Hedge fund managers, private equity partners, investment bankers, and wealth management principals. Austin's growing financial services presence, along with the Texas tax advantage for high earners, has attracted a meaningful cohort of finance professionals who view Westlake as the natural address equivalent to comparable neighborhoods in Greenwich or the North Shore of Chicago.

UT Austin Faculty and Administrators

A smaller but consistent buyer segment. Senior UT faculty, deans, and senior administrators who value proximity to campus (a 15–20 minute drive from much of Westlake) and the academic community culture that characterizes the Eanes ISD school environment.

Established Austin Business Owners

Multi-generational Austin families who have accumulated wealth through real estate, law, medicine, or entrepreneurship. This segment tends to be the most loyal to specific streets and neighborhoods within West Lake Hills, with many buyers specifically seeking properties within a block or two of their current or childhood home.

A significant portion of buyers, particularly in the $2.5M+ range, are relocators from higher-cost markets, primarily California. The California-to-Austin migration, while slightly slower than its 2021–2022 peak, remains structurally positive for the Westlake market. These buyers often enter with all-cash or high-equity offers, having liquidated California properties, and their price sensitivity is meaningfully lower than local buyers who have grown up with Austin's historically more affordable market.

Westlake High School Athletic Culture: A Community Identity Driver

The Westlake Chaparrals athletic program deserves specific mention because it functions as more than just a school activity, it is a significant community identity driver that strengthens the social bonds of the Westlake / Eanes ISD residential market. The Westlake football program in particular has achieved national recognition, with multiple state championships and a consistent pipeline to Division I college football programs and NFL rosters.

The Westlake athletic culture, which extends to swimming, baseball, lacrosse, basketball, and academics, creates a community cohesion and school pride that outlasts children's enrollment. Parents of Westlake graduates frequently choose to remain in the neighborhood; grandparents of Westlake students often relocate to be nearby. This multi-generational loyalty contributes to the market's low turnover rate and the deep demand pool for homes in the area.

For buyers with student-athletes, Westlake's combination of Exemplary academic ratings and elite athletic programs is genuinely rare, matching or exceeding what is achievable in far more expensive private school markets in other major cities.

Investment and Resale Outlook: Why Eanes ISD Holds Value

From a long-term investment perspective, the Westlake / Eanes ISD market has historically been one of the most resilient residential segments in Central Texas. The structural reasons are clear: supply is fixed, demand is driven by one of the state's most desirable school districts, and the buyer pool is drawn from Austin's highest-income professional segments.

During the 2022–2023 Austin market correction, when many Austin-area submarkets saw 15–25% price pullbacks from peak 2022 levels, the Westlake / Eanes ISD market corrected significantly less, with most analysts observing price adjustments in the 8–12% range before recovering to new highs in 2024–2025. This resilience reflects the school district premium's function as a value floor.

Looking forward to 2026 and beyond, the primary risks to the Westlake market are:

  • Interest rate sensitivity: Fewer buyers at $2M+ qualify for conventional mortgages, meaning rate movements have a proportionally smaller impact. Most buyers in this range are cash or high-equity.
  • Tech sector concentration: A meaningful contraction in Austin's tech employment base would disproportionately impact the buyer pool. The diversification of Austin's economy (financial services, healthcare, government) provides some buffer.
  • School district governance: Eanes ISD's performance is a function of its governance, funding, and community engagement. Any structural change to the district's funding or boundaries would be a significant market event. No such changes are currently anticipated.

Frequently Asked Questions: Westlake / Eanes ISD Real Estate 2026

What is the Eanes ISD school district premium on home prices?

Eanes ISD properties command a measurable premium of 20–35% over comparable homes just outside the district boundary in the Austin area. This premium reflects the district's consistent ranking as one of the top school districts in Texas, with Westlake High School regularly ranked #1 or #2 in the state. The premium persists even for buyers without school-age children, as Eanes ISD homes maintain superior resale demand and liquidity across market cycles.

What are current home prices in West Lake Hills?

West Lake Hills homes in 2026 range from approximately $1.2M to $5M+ for single-family residences. The median price per square foot ranges from $450 to $800+, depending on lot size, view, condition, proximity to key amenities, and whether the home is original, updated, or new custom construction. Tear-down lots with Eanes ISD zoning trade in the $900K–$1.5M range.

How does Rollingwood compare to West Lake Hills for real estate?

Rollingwood is a small, incorporated city that sits just east of West Lake Hills, closer to MoPac and downtown Austin. Homes in Rollingwood range from approximately $900K to $2.5M, offering a more accessible entry into Eanes ISD than West Lake Hills proper. Rollingwood lots tend to be smaller and the architecture more eclectic, with many original 1960s–1970s homes alongside new custom builds. The Eanes ISD school assignment (primarily Eanes Elementary → Hill Country MS → Westlake HS) is comparable to much of West Lake Hills.

Is the 78746 zip code entirely in Eanes ISD?

No. While 78746 is the primary zip code associated with the Westlake / Eanes ISD market, zip codes and school district boundaries are not perfectly aligned. Some 78746 properties may be zoned to Austin ISD, and some Eanes ISD properties may carry different zip codes. Always verify the specific school zoning of any property with Eanes ISD directly at eanesisd.net using the property address before making any purchase decision based on school assignment.

Who are the typical buyers of Westlake / Eanes ISD homes in 2026?

The Westlake / Eanes ISD buyer profile in 2026 skews heavily toward high-income professionals: technology executives from Dell, Apple, Google, Tesla, and Austin's growing tech ecosystem; finance professionals and investment managers; UT Austin faculty and administrators; and established Austin business owners. A significant portion are California and Northeast relocators, drawn by Texas's income-tax-free environment combined with Eanes ISD's academic reputation. Many buyers at $2.5M+ are purchasing with cash or high equity from prior real estate transactions.
