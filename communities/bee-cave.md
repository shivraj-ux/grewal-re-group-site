## What's actually happening in Bee Cave right now

Bee Cave is the buy-more-house option for families priced out of the 78746 corridor but unwilling to compromise on school district quality. Lake Travis ISD draws serious buyers, and the newer housing stock — particularly in Spanish Oaks and Falconhead — holds value well. The Hill Country terrain means most homes have at least partial views and more private feel than equivalently priced properties closer to the urban core.

The trade-off is the commute. TX-71 at rush hour is not a fast road, and Bee Cave doesn't have Mopac proximity. For buyers who work hybrid or remotely, that calculus shifts dramatically — and that's where the Bee Cave value proposition really lands.

The broader Austin market (June 2026) shows roughly 4.7 months of inventory metro-wide, which gives buyers leverage in most price ranges. Bee Cave's sub-$1M segment is competitive; above $2M moves slower and buyers can negotiate.

## Bee Cave: Quick Answers

### Is Bee Cave part of Austin?

Bee Cave is an **independent city** in Travis County, not part of the City of Austin. It has its own government and police department. Residents pay Bee Cave city tax, Travis County tax, and Lake Travis ISD taxes — no Austin city tax.

### What is Spanish Oaks?

**Spanish Oaks** is Bee Cave's premier gated golf community, featuring a Jack Nicklaus Signature Course, custom and semi-custom homes from $2M to $10M+, and private club amenities. It is consistently one of the most expensive neighborhoods in the greater Austin area.

### How does Bee Cave compare to Westlake Hills?

Both offer Hill Country proximity and top-tier schools, but **Westlake Hills** (78746, Eanes ISD) sits closer to downtown, has higher price floors, and greater name recognition. **Bee Cave** (78738, Lake Travis ISD) offers more new construction, lower entry points, and a more suburban pace — better for buyers who prioritize space over commute.

### What is the Shops at the Galleria?

The **Shops at the Galleria** is Bee Cave's main retail and dining hub on TX-71, anchored by Whole Foods, restaurants, and boutiques. It's the commercial center of the Bee Cave corridor and a key amenity for residents in the 78738 zip code.

## Quick answers about Bee Cave

### Where is Bee Cave in Austin?

Bee Cave is an independent city in Travis County, about 20 miles west of downtown Austin (roughly a 25-35 minute drive via TX-71 or RR 620). Its primary ZIP code is 78738.

### What school district serves Bee Cave?

Bee Cave is served by Lake Travis Independent School District (LTISD). Students are zoned to Bee Cave Elementary, Bee Cave Middle School, and Lake Travis High School, which consistently ranks among the top public high schools in Texas.

### How much do homes cost in Bee Cave?

Bee Cave (78738) typically ranges from about $700K for entry-level townhomes to $3M+ for custom builds in gated communities like Falconhead and Spanish Oaks. It runs well above the broader Austin metro median due to its Hill Country positioning and Lake Travis ISD schools.

### What is Bee Cave known for?

Bee Cave is known for its Hill Country terrain, newer master-planned communities, and Lake Travis ISD schools, offering more house for the money than the nearby 78746 corridor. It is also home to Spanish Oaks, a premier gated golf community with a Jack Nicklaus Signature Course, and the Shops at the Galleria retail hub.

### What is there to do in Bee Cave?

The Shops at the Galleria on TX-71 is the area's main retail and dining hub, anchored by Whole Foods, restaurants, and boutiques. Residents also enjoy golf at Spanish Oaks and Falconhead, plus easy access to nearby Lake Travis and the surrounding Hill Country for outdoor recreation.
