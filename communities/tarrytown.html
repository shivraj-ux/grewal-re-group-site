Tarrytown FAQ

Quick answers about Tarrytown

Where is Tarrytown in Austin?

Tarrytown is an established West Austin neighborhood in the 78703 ZIP code, set just west of downtown near Lake Austin. It's known for tree-lined streets, mature oaks, and being one of Austin's most central, historic addresses.

What school district serves Tarrytown?

Tarrytown is served by Austin ISD, with many homes zoned to the well-regarded Casis Elementary School noted on this page. Families should always verify the exact attendance zone for a specific address.

How much do homes cost in Tarrytown?

Tarrytown is a luxury, estate-tier market where buyers trade square footage for character, walkability, and location rather than chasing the lowest price per foot. Inventory is famously tight, so pricing reflects scarcity and the neighborhood's premier address; reach out for current sold figures and a street-level comp set.

What is Tarrytown known for?

Tarrytown is known for its historic estates, mature oak canopy, and downtown adjacency, making it one of Austin's most established and sought-after neighborhoods. Its defining trait is limited supply, so many homes sell through quiet, pre-marketed processes rather than public listings.

What is there to do in Tarrytown?

Tarrytown's location near Lake Austin offers easy access to boating, waterfront parks, and outdoor recreation, while its proximity to downtown puts dining, shopping, and entertainment minutes away. The neighborhood's walkable, tree-lined streets and nearby greenspaces like Reed Park and the Lady Bird Lake trail system are also a major draw.
