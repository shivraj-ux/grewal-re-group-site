Shivraj's Take

What's actually happening in Rollingwood right now

Rollingwood is where Westlake families plant roots when they want less traffic and more trees. It's technically its own city, which means the HOA-free lot culture, darker skies, and zero commercial encroachment. Buyers pay a premium for that, and it holds. The 78746 zip is posting roughly $1.72M median while the broader Austin metro is at $440K — that spread tells you everything about the demand for Eanes ISD access and established neighborhoods.

New construction on larger lots is the active story here — teardown-rebuilds where a 1970s ranch becomes a 5,000-square-foot modern. If you're a seller on a larger lot, this is one of the better moments in recent memory. If you're a buyer, expect to move decisively — the well-priced homes don't sit.

Rollingwood: Quick Answers

What zip code is Rollingwood?

Rollingwood uses the 78746 zip code, which it shares with Westlake Hills and parts of West Lake Hills. This zip is one of the most expensive in the Austin metro, with a median near $1.72M (June 2026).

What schools serve Rollingwood?

Rollingwood is zoned to Eanes ISD — specifically Cedar Creek Elementary, Hill Country Middle, and Westlake High School. Westlake High consistently ranks among the top public high schools in Texas.

Is Rollingwood part of Austin?

Rollingwood is an independent incorporated city with its own government, entirely surrounded by the City of Austin within Travis County. Residents receive a separate Rollingwood property tax line alongside Travis County and Eanes ISD taxes.

How far is Rollingwood from downtown Austin?

Rollingwood is approximately 4 miles from downtown Austin, a 10–12 minute drive via Mopac (Loop 1) under normal conditions. It is one of the closest luxury neighborhoods to the urban core.

Rollingwood FAQ

Quick answers about Rollingwood

Where is Rollingwood in Austin?

Rollingwood is a small incorporated city of about 1,400 residents located within the 78746 zip code, entirely surrounded by the City of Austin in Travis County. It sits roughly 4 miles from downtown Austin, about a 10 to 12 minute drive via Mopac (Loop 1).

What school district serves Rollingwood?

Rollingwood is served by Eanes Independent School District (Eanes ISD), one of the highest-rated districts in Texas. Assigned campuses are Cedar Creek Elementary, Hill Country Middle School, and Westlake High School.

How much do homes cost in Rollingwood?

Rollingwood is a luxury market, with individual homes typically ranging from about $1.5M for updated ranch-styles to $6M+ for new construction on larger lots. The shared 78746 zip code posted a median near $1.72 million as of June 2026.

What is Rollingwood known for?

Rollingwood is known for being its own independent incorporated city with large wooded lots, an HOA-free lot culture, darker skies, and almost no through-traffic or commercial encroachment. It is prized for Eanes ISD access and a quiet enclave feel just minutes from downtown Austin.

What is there to do in Rollingwood?

Rollingwood's appeal centers on its quiet, tree-shaded streets and large lots in an established residential setting just minutes from downtown. Its central West Austin location puts residents close to Zilker Park, Barton Springs, the Lady Bird Lake trails, and the dining and shopping along Bee Cave Road and the broader Westlake area.
