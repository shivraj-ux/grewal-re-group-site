Lifestyle

Living in Barton Creek

Barton Creek is not a single subdivision. It's roughly 4,000 acres of master-planned Hill Country between Loop 360 and Bee Cave Road, built in the 1980s and '90s as a resort-residential community — the golf came first, and the neighborhoods were designed around it. What reads as one gate from the outside is actually a collection of distinct sub-communities: Barton Creek Estates, the guard-gated Preserve, the newer Amarra sections, and smaller enclaves like Escala, Mirador, and Calera Court. There is no single HOA; each section has its own governing documents and fee structure. Our Barton Creek buyer guide breaks down what each sub-community trades for its price.

The lifestyle anchor is Barton Creek Country Club and its four championship courses — Fazio Canyons, Fazio Foothills, Crenshaw Cliffside, and Palmer Lakeside — plus spa, tennis, pickleball, and dining shared with the Omni Barton Creek Resort on the ridgeline. Add 25+ miles of private trails inside the community and direct access to the Barton Creek Greenbelt's public canyon trails and swimming holes (Sculpture Falls, Twin Falls), and you get the combination buyers here actually pay for: privacy and amenity depth over raw downtown access. Downtown is still a 20–30 minute drive in normal traffic.

Housing stock spans several decades — classically styled 1990s masonry customs on half-acre-plus lots in the Estates, contemporary glass-and-steel builds in Amarra, and large architecturally controlled estates in The Preserve. Entry points start around $1.1M–$1.4M in the smaller sections and run past $6M at premier lot positions; the lot itself often carries 30–50% of the price. Full pricing detail by segment is in our 2026 Barton Creek guide and the resort-area deep dive.

Schools

Schools

Most of Barton Creek is zoned to Eanes ISD, consistently ranked among the top five public school districts in Texas with "A" accountability ratings from the Texas Education Agency. The feeder pattern for Eanes-zoned homes runs to West Ridge Middle School and Westlake High School; elementary assignment varies by address, with Barton Creek Elementary, Forest Trail Elementary, and Cedar Creek Elementary among the Eanes campuses serving the area.

The caveat that matters: the 78735 ZIP code is not entirely Eanes ISD. The development spans a boundary with Austin ISD, and that line does not follow subdivision sections cleanly — two homes on the same street can be zoned to different districts, and Eanes-zoned properties have commanded a 15–20% premium over otherwise comparable homes. Verify the assignment for the specific parcel with Eanes ISD and TCAD before you write an offer, not after. For district-by-district context across the metro, see our Austin schools guide.

Market Data

The Barton Creek market

This is a constrained-supply, patient market. The 78735 ZIP rarely carries more than 20–25 active listings at once, homes in this segment typically take 30–90 days to go under contract, and the buyer pool skews toward executives, established professionals, and relocations from higher-cost coastal markets. Well-priced homes find buyers reliably; overpriced ones sit.

The current numbers: Redfin showed a median sold price of $2,800,000 for the most recent month (as of June 11, 2026), while Realtor.com put the median listing price at $2,350,000 as of March 2026. The gap between those two figures is normal here — what actually sells skews larger and better-positioned than the average listing, and per-square-foot value swings hard on lot position, views, and renovation status.

Barton Creek FAQ

Quick answers about Barton Creek

Where is Barton Creek in Austin?

Barton Creek is a gated Hill Country community in West Austin, just west of downtown. It falls within the 78735 ZIP code.

What school district serves Barton Creek?

Barton Creek is served by Eanes ISD, one of the most highly regarded school districts in the Austin area.

How much do homes cost in Barton Creek?

Barton Creek is a luxury, estate-tier market and the benchmark for West Austin family luxury. The page does not list a specific price figure, but it positions the community at the top end of the market with estate pricing.

Is Barton Creek a gated community?

Yes. Barton Creek is a gated, amenity-rich community known for privacy, championship golf, tennis, and more than 25 miles of private trails. Buyers here tend to value privacy and amenities over raw downtown access.

What is there to do in Barton Creek?

Residents enjoy championship golf, tennis, and over 25 miles of private trails within the community. The neighborhood also offers direct access to the Barton Creek Greenbelt, a popular spot for hiking and outdoor recreation in the Hill Country.

Does buying a home in Barton Creek include country club membership?

No. Barton Creek Country Club membership is a separate transaction negotiated directly with the club. As of 2026, a full golf membership carries an initiation fee of roughly $65,000–$85,000 plus monthly dues of about $1,500–$1,800; social membership runs $40,000–$50,000 initiation with $800–$1,000 monthly dues. If club access is a primary reason for buying, start the membership inquiry before going under contract, since availability can shift and golf memberships periodically carry waiting lists.

Is Barton Creek one gated neighborhood or several?

Several. Barton Creek is a roughly 4,000-acre master plan made up of distinct sub-communities, including Barton Creek Estates, the guard-gated Preserve, the newer Amarra sections, and smaller enclaves like Escala, Mirador, and Calera Court. Some sections are gated and some are not, and there is no single HOA — each sub-association has its own covenants and fees, roughly $200 to $800 per month depending on the section. Verify the specific section's gate, HOA documents, and fees for any home you consider.

How does Barton Creek compare to Westlake Hills?

Both are West Austin luxury markets largely served by Eanes ISD, so schools are usually not the deciding factor. The difference is structure: Barton Creek is a master-planned, gated resort community built around Barton Creek Country Club's four championship courses, with 25+ miles of private trails and the Omni resort inside the community. That club-and-gate lifestyle is the reason many buyers choose Barton Creek over comparable options in Westlake Hills, trading some downtown proximity for privacy and amenity depth.
