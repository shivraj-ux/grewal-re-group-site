## Lost Creek: Quick Answers

### Where exactly is Lost Creek in Austin?

Lost Creek is in **West Austin**, west of Mopac Expressway (Loop 1) and south of Bee Cave Road, in the 78746 zip code. It borders Rollingwood to the north and the Barton Creek Greenbelt corridor to the south.

### What elementary school is Lost Creek zoned to?

Lost Creek is primarily zoned to **Forest Trail Elementary** (Eanes ISD), with middle school at West Ridge Middle and high school at Westlake High School — one of the top-ranked public high schools in Texas.

### What is the typical lot size in Lost Creek?

Lost Creek lots typically run **0.25 to 0.75 acres**, larger than many central Austin neighborhoods. The mature oak and cedar tree coverage is a defining feature — many lots feel significantly more private than the acreage would suggest.

### How does Lost Creek compare to Westlake Hills?

Both share Eanes ISD and the 78746 zip. **Westlake Hills** is an incorporated city with stronger name recognition and slightly higher price points. **Lost Creek** is within Austin city limits, often offers more lot for the price, and has a similar school district and commute profile.

## Quick answers about Lost Creek

### Where is Lost Creek in Austin?

Lost Creek is a wooded enclave in West Austin, west of Mopac Expressway (Loop 1) and south of Bee Cave Road, in the 78746 ZIP code. It borders Rollingwood to the north and the Barton Creek Greenbelt corridor to the south, with a 10 to 15 minute commute to downtown Austin.

### What school district serves Lost Creek?

Lost Creek is served by Eanes ISD. Students are primarily zoned to Forest Trail Elementary, West Ridge Middle School, and Westlake High School, one of the top-ranked public high schools in Texas.

### How much do homes cost in Lost Creek?

Lost Creek homes typically range from about $1.2M for smaller ranch-style properties to $4M+ for updated or new-build homes on larger lots. The broader 78746 ZIP code posted a median of approximately $1.72 million as of June 2026 (Unlock MLS via KXAN).

### What is Lost Creek known for?

Lost Creek is known as a quiet, wooded West Austin neighborhood with generous lots (typically 0.25 to 0.75 acres) and a mature oak and cedar canopy. It offers Eanes ISD schools and West Austin's lifestyle without the name-brand price premium of Westlake Hills, and is purposely insular with no retail or cut-through traffic.

### What is there to do in Lost Creek?

Lost Creek is a residential, low-traffic neighborhood, so most amenities are nearby rather than inside it. Residents have quick access to the Barton Creek Greenbelt corridor to the south for hiking and trails, plus the dining and retail of the Bee Cave Road corridor and downtown Austin within a 10 to 15 minute drive.
