Frequently asked questions

What is a typical mortgage payment on a $600,000 home in Austin?
At 20 percent down ($480,000 loan) and 6.75 percent on a 30-year fixed, principal and interest runs about $3,112 per month. Travis County property taxes add roughly $975 per month and insurance roughly $200, for a total PITI around $4,287.
Do I need PMI if I put less than 20% down in Texas?
Yes. Most conventional loans require PMI when the down payment is under 20 percent. PMI in Texas typically runs 0.5 to 1.5 percent of the loan per year and drops off once you reach 20 percent equity through payments or appreciation.
How much does Austin's property tax add to my monthly payment?
At the Travis County average of about 1.95 percent, a $600,000 home adds roughly $975 per month to your payment collected by your lender in escrow. This is one of the highest rates in Texas, which matters if you are relocating from a state with lower taxes.
15-year vs 30-year mortgage: which is better in Austin?
A 15-year mortgage has a lower rate and far less total interest but a higher required monthly payment. Most Austin buyers choose 30-year for cash flow flexibility and pay extra principal when they can. The right answer depends on your income stability and other financial goals.
