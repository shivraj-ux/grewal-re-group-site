Frequently Asked Questions

How much income do I need to buy a home in Austin?

For a $500,000 home with 10 percent down at a 7 percent rate, your total monthly housing cost including taxes and insurance runs about $3,400. Using the standard 28 percent front-end DTI guideline, you would need a gross monthly income of roughly $12,140, or about $145,700 per year. Lenders also look at your total debt-to-income ratio including car payments, student loans, and other obligations.

What DTI ratio do lenders use in Austin?

Most conventional lenders cap your total debt-to-income ratio at 43 to 45 percent. FHA loans may go up to 50 percent in some cases. Your housing payment alone should ideally stay under 28 percent of gross monthly income. The calculator uses both limits and shows you the more conservative result.

How much down payment do I need to buy in Austin?

Conventional loans require as little as 3 to 5 percent down for first-time buyers, though you will pay PMI below 20 percent. FHA loans require 3.5 percent down. A larger down payment lowers your monthly payment and removes PMI, but Austin's competitive market sometimes rewards buyers who keep more cash liquid for offers.

Does Austin's property tax affect how much home I can afford?

Yes, significantly. Travis County property taxes run roughly 1.95 percent per year, one of the higher rates in Texas. On a $500,000 home that adds about $812 per month to your housing cost before you pay a dollar of principal. Lenders include taxes and insurance in your DTI calculation, so high Texas taxes directly reduce the loan amount you qualify for.

Should I talk to a lender before using this calculator?

This calculator gives you a solid starting estimate, but a lender will look at your credit score, employment history, asset reserves, and the specific loan program you qualify for.
