Frequently asked questions

Is it cheaper to rent or buy in Austin right now?
It depends on the price range, your down payment, and how long you plan to stay. At current Austin prices around $550,000 to $650,000 with a 20 percent down payment, buying typically breaks even with renting in 3 to 5 years when you account for equity buildup and Austin's historical appreciation rate of roughly 4 percent annually.
What does this calculator include?
Rent side: total rent paid over the period plus renter's insurance. Buy side: your down payment, mortgage payments, property taxes, maintenance, HOA, minus the equity you build through principal paydown. It does not include the opportunity cost of your down payment or closing costs, which would make renting look slightly more competitive.
What property tax rate should I use for Austin?
Travis County effective rates average roughly 1.8 to 2.1 percent. The default of 1.95 percent is a reasonable middle estimate for most Austin ZIP codes. If you know the specific neighborhood, look up the appraisal district rate or ask Shivraj for the exact figure.
How long do I need to stay to make buying worth it?
Most buyers in Austin break even between 3 and 6 years depending on the purchase price, down payment, and rent comparison. If you plan to be in Austin less than 3 years, renting is usually smarter. The calculator above will tell you your specific number.
