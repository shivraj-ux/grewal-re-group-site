Frequently asked questions

What is a 2/1 mortgage rate buydown?
A 2/1 buydown temporarily lowers your rate by 2 percent in Year 1 and 1 percent in Year 2, then returns to your note rate for the remaining 28 years. The builder deposits the subsidy into an escrow account at closing to cover the payment difference each month.
Are Austin builders offering buydowns in 2026?
Yes. Many Austin new construction builders offer 2/1 buydowns as an incentive. The typical cost to the builder is roughly 1.5 to 2 percent of the loan amount. Whether that is better than a price reduction depends on how long you plan to stay in the home.
Is a buydown better than a lower purchase price?
A temporary buydown helps your first two years but the savings disappear after that. A lower purchase price saves you money every month for 30 years. If you can choose, a price reduction is usually worth more, especially if you plan to stay long-term.
Can I refinance during the buydown period?
Yes. If rates drop and you refinance, the unused portion of your buydown escrow is typically applied to closing costs or returned to you. Ask your lender how they handle the remaining escrow balance at payoff or refinance.
