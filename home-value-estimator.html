Frequently asked questions

How can I find out what my Austin home is worth?
The most accurate way is a comparative market analysis (CMA), a review of recent sales of similar homes in your neighborhood adjusted for your home's size, condition, and features. Request one through this tool and Grewal RE Group will prepare it.
Are online home value estimates accurate?
Automated estimates can be off by tens of thousands of dollars, especially in non-disclosure states like Texas where sale prices are not public record. A local agent's comp-based valuation is far more reliable.
How much does a home valuation cost?
Nothing. A professional comparative market analysis from Grewal RE Group is free and comes with no obligation to list.
How long does it take to get my home valuation?
After you submit your home's details, Grewal RE Group typically returns a personalized valuation within one to two business days.
How fast are homes selling in Austin right now?
It depends heavily on price point, neighborhood, and condition. Compared with the 2021 to 2022 frenzy, the typical Austin home now takes longer to sell and buyers have more negotiating room, but well-priced, well-presented homes in desirable areas still sell quickly. Accurate pricing from day one is the single biggest factor. We include current days-on-market for your specific area in your valuation.
What is the real estate agent commission in Austin in 2026?
Commissions are always negotiable and are not set by law. Since the 2024 National Association of REALTORS settlement, buyer-agent compensation is negotiated separately and disclosed up front rather than assumed. We will walk you through current Austin norms and exactly what your costs would look like; you can also estimate your bottom line with our Seller Net Proceeds Calculator.
When is the best time to sell a home in Austin?
Historically, spring through early summer (roughly February to June) brings the most buyer activity in Austin, which can support stronger pricing. That said, Austin's steady stream of relocating buyers shops year-round, and lower-competition seasons like fall and winter can mean motivated buyers and fewer competing listings. The right timing depends on your home and your goals.
What should I do to get my Austin home ready to sell?
The highest-return steps are usually decluttering and deep cleaning, handling minor repairs and fresh paint, improving curb appeal, and pricing correctly based on recent comparable sales. Professional photography and light staging help your listing stand out online, where nearly every buyer starts. We provide a tailored pre-listing checklist with your valuation.
