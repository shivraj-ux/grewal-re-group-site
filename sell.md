## The Austin Selling Process

### 1. Walk-Through Consultation & Data-Driven Pricing Strategy

We start by walking through your home to evaluate condition, upgrades, and presentation. Then we pull Austin MLS data, county appraisal records, and recent comparable sales in your neighborhood to set a list price that attracts qualified buyers and maximizes your net proceeds. The first few days on market matter most.

### 2. Preparation & Compass Concierge

Selling a home often means repairs, updates, and staging. With Compass Concierge, we front the cost of flooring, painting, landscaping, staging, and other preparation needs. You pay these costs at closing with no interest or fees. This keeps cash in your pocket now while getting your home show-ready. Our net proceeds calculator shows you exactly how these costs impact your final check.

[See Your Net Proceeds](/calculators/seller-net-proceeds-calculator)

### 3. Professional Marketing Launch

Once your home is ready, we launch a white-glove marketing strategy. This includes professional photography, targeted digital advertising, MLS placement with full details and high-resolution imagery, and open houses at strategic times. Austin's market rewards visibility and first impressions.

### 4. Showings & Feedback

We manage showing traffic, respond to buyer inquiries quickly, and collect feedback from agents and potential buyers after each showing. This data helps us adjust strategy, identify buyer concerns, and position your home for the strongest offers.

### 5. Negotiation & Contract Terms

When offers come in, we negotiate based on your goals, net proceeds, and timeline. We review contract terms, contingencies, inspection periods, and closing dates. Your priorities drive the strategy, not just the offer price.

### 6. Closing Management

From accepted offer through final walk, we coordinate with the buyer's lender, title company, and inspectors. We handle all paperwork, manage the closing schedule, and ensure funds transfer correctly. Closing typically occurs 30-45 days after accepted offer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What does the Austin selling process timeline look like?:** The Austin home selling timeline typically runs 30-45 days from accepted offer to closing. This includes inspection period (usually 7-10 days), appraisal (5-7 days), title search (3-5 days), and final closing steps (5-7 days). We handle all coordination to keep the process on track.

**Q: How is my home priced?:** We start with a walk-through consultation and pull recent sales data from Austin MLS, county records, and comparable properties in your neighborhood. We analyze condition, upgrades, lot size, location, and market trends to set a competitive list price that attracts qualified buyers and maximizes your net proceeds.

**Q: What is Compass Concierge and how does it work?:** Compass Concierge fronts the cost of home preparation like flooring, painting, landscaping, and staging. You pay these costs at closing, with no interest or fees. This helps your home show at its best without depleting your cash upfront. Use our net proceeds calculator to see how preparation costs impact your final payment.

**Q: What should I expect in terms of showings and offers?:** After your home hits the MLS with professional photography and targeted digital marketing, we manage showing traffic, collect feedback from agents and buyers, and track buyer interest signals. This helps us position the property for the strongest offers and best terms.

**Q: How much will I net after selling my home?:** Use our net proceeds calculator to estimate your cash at closing. We factor in your home value, seller costs (closing costs, title insurance, transfer tax, broker commission), and any preparation costs through Compass Concierge. This shows you exactly what to expect.

**Q: Can you sell my home quickly if I need to move fast?:** Yes. Market conditions matter, but we can discuss strategies for faster sales, from pricing to marketing intensity to flexible contingencies. Some sellers prioritize timing over price, others prioritize net proceeds. We help you balance these goals with local market data.

**Q: What happens if a deal falls through?:** If a buyer's financing falls through, inspection or appraisal issues derail a sale, or contingencies fail, your home goes back on the market. We re-strategize on pricing, marketing, or terms and work to get a new offer quickly. We also use this data to adjust the listing for better buyer fit.
