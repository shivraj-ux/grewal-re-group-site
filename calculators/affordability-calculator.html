Frequently Asked Questions

How much income do I need to buy a home in Austin?

For a $500,000 home with 10% down at 7%, total monthly housing cost including taxes and insurance is roughly $3,400. At the 28% front-end guideline, you need about $12,140/month gross income, or $145,700/year. Your total debt load matters too — add car payments and student loans and lenders apply a 43-45% total DTI cap.

What DTI ratio do lenders use in Austin?

Most conventional lenders cap total debt-to-income at 43-45%. FHA allows up to 50% in some cases. Your housing payment alone should ideally stay under 28% of gross monthly income. This calculator shows you both thresholds and takes the more conservative result as your "max."

How much down payment do I need to buy in Austin?

Conventional loans go as low as 3-5% for first-time buyers, though PMI applies below 20%. FHA requires 3.5%. A bigger down payment lowers your payment and drops PMI, but Austin's competitive market sometimes rewards buyers who keep cash liquid for strong offers.

How do Austin property taxes affect affordability?

Travis County property taxes average about 1.95% per year. On a $500,000 home that is roughly $812/month added to your housing cost. Lenders count taxes and insurance in your DTI, so Texas's high rates directly reduce the loan amount you qualify for compared to lower-tax states.

Should I get pre-approved before using this calculator?

This calculator gives you a strong estimate. A lender will also weigh your credit score, employment history, and asset reserves. I always recommend getting pre-approved before touring Austin homes — it makes your offers stronger and removes surprises. I can connect you with trusted Austin lenders.
