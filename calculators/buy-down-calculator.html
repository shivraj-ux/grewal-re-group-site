Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 2-1 buydown in Austin?

A seller or builder concession that temporarily reduces your mortgage rate for two years. Year 1: rate is 2% below the note rate. Year 2: rate is 1% below. Year 3+: full note rate. The cost is the total payment difference, deposited at closing.

Who pays for the buydown?

Typically the seller, builder, or lender. Austin builders frequently offer 2-1 buydowns to move inventory. Compare the buydown cost against a seller credit toward a permanent rate reduction or lower purchase price — sometimes the alternative is better.

What is a 3-2-1 buydown?

Rate is reduced 3% in year 1, 2% in year 2, and 1% in year 3, before returning to the full note rate in year 4. It costs more than a 2-1 but provides bigger relief in year 1. Most common on new construction and higher-priced Austin homes.

Is a buydown better than a lower purchase price?

A lower price permanently reduces your payment and equity basis. A buydown gives short-term relief but you eventually pay the full rate. If you plan to refinance when rates drop, a buydown bridges the gap. If you plan to hold long-term, a lower price usually wins.

Can I refinance out of a buydown?

Yes. The buydown applies to your current loan only. If rates drop, you can refinance at any time — even during the buydown period. The remaining subsidy does not transfer to a refinanced loan.
