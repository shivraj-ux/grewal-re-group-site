Frequently Asked Questions

Is it better to rent or buy in Austin in 2026?

It depends on how long you plan to stay. For 5+ year horizons, buying typically wins once equity is factored in. Austin's 2022-2023 price correction created buying opportunities, and long-term appreciation has averaged 4-6% per year. For shorter horizons, renting may be more cost-effective depending on current rent levels and mortgage rates.

How long do I need to stay in Austin for buying to make sense?

At 7% rates and Austin's 1.95% property tax rate, most scenarios favor buying after 4-5 years when equity is included. This calculator lets you model any horizon. Enter the years you plan to stay and see the break-even point.

What is Austin's home appreciation rate?

Long-term appreciation has averaged roughly 4-6% per year over 10+ year periods. The market corrected 15-20% in 2022-2023 after exceptional pandemic-era gains. The calculator defaults to 4% as a conservative estimate — adjust to 3% for a bear case or 6% for a bull case.

What costs go into the buying side of this comparison?

Principal and interest payments, property taxes, homeowners insurance, HOA, and maintenance (typically 1-1.5% of home value per year). The calculator subtracts equity built through principal paydown and appreciation to get a true net cost of buying.

Is Austin a good real estate investment in 2026?

Austin's fundamentals remain strong: population growth, a diversified tech and healthcare economy, and limited land in desirable areas. The 2022-2023 correction reset prices to more sustainable levels. For buyers with a 5-7 year horizon, 2026 is a reasonable entry point compared to peak 2021-2022 pricing.
