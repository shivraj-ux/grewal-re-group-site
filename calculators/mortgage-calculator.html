Frequently Asked Questions

What is a typical mortgage payment on a $600,000 home in Austin?

At 20% down ($480,000 loan) and 7% on a 30-year fixed, P&I is about $3,195/month. Add Travis County property taxes (~$975/month) and homeowners insurance (~$200/month), and total PITI runs around $4,370/month.

Do I need PMI on an Austin home?

If your down payment is under 20%, your lender will typically require private mortgage insurance. In Texas, PMI usually costs 0.5-1.5% of the loan per year. It drops off once you reach 20% equity — either through appreciation or principal paydown.

How do Austin property taxes affect my payment?

Travis County averages about 1.95% per year — among the highest in Texas. On a $600,000 home that adds roughly $975/month collected through escrow. This is a major reason Austin buyers often qualify for less home than they expect.

Is a 15-year or 30-year mortgage better in Austin?

A 15-year mortgage has a higher payment but a lower rate and far less total interest paid. A 30-year gives you flexibility with a lower required payment. Most Austin buyers choose 30-year fixed for cash flow, then make extra principal payments when comfortable.
